The 8 Dietary Protocols
Every Trainer Needs

Master these protocols and you can confidently serve any client who walks through your door.

1
High-Protein

Optimized for muscle building and recovery. Emphasizes lean meats, eggs, dairy, and plant proteins with 30%+ of calories from protein.

1,800 - 3,200 cal P 30% / C 40% / F 30% Muscle gain, athletes
2
Keto

Very low carbohydrate approach that shifts the body into ketosis. Under 20g net carbs daily with 70%+ calories from healthy fats.

1,500 - 2,500 cal P 20% / C 5% / F 75% Fat loss, metabolic
3
Vegan

100% plant-based nutrition with complete amino acid coverage through strategic food combining. Rich in legumes, grains, and vegetables.

1,600 - 2,800 cal P 20% / C 55% / F 25% Ethical, environmental
4
Gluten-Free

Celiac-safe protocol using grain alternatives like rice, quinoa, and oats (certified GF). Complete nutrition without wheat, barley, or rye.

1,600 - 2,800 cal P 25% / C 45% / F 30% Celiac, sensitivity

5
Paleo

Whole-food ancestral approach eliminating grains, dairy, and legumes. Built around meats, fish, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds.

1,800 - 3,000 cal P 30% / C 35% / F 35% Whole food, autoimmune
6
Mediterranean

Heart-healthy approach rich in olive oil, fish, whole grains, and vegetables. Clinically proven for longevity and cardiovascular health.

1,800 - 2,600 cal P 20% / C 45% / F 35% Heart health, longevity

Low-Carb

Moderate carbohydrate restriction under 100g daily with balanced fat and protein. Less restrictive than keto but effective for fat loss.

1,500 - 2,500 cal P 30% / C 20% / F 50% Fat loss, blood sugar
8
Dairy-Free

Complete nutrition without any dairy products. Uses lactose alternatives and ensures adequate calcium through leafy greens, fortified foods, and nuts.

1,600 - 2,800 cal P 25% / C 45% / F 30% Lactose intolerant, allergy

The Cheat Sheet
Matching Diets to Fitness Goals

Clip this page and keep it at your desk. The fastest way to prescribe the right protocol for any client.

Goal Recommended Protocol Calories Key Macro Notes
Fat Loss Low-Carb or Keto Deficit 500 cal Protein 1.2g/lb Preserve muscle mass with high protein
Muscle Gain High-Protein Surplus 300 cal Protein 1.6g/lb Time carbs around workouts
Endurance Mediterranean Maintenance Carbs 55%+ Prioritize complex carbs for fuel
General Health Mediterranean Maintenance Balanced Focus on whole foods and variety
Body Recomp High-Protein Slight deficit 200 Protein 1.4g/lb Cycle calories on training days
Athletic Perf. High-Protein + Paleo Surplus 200 Protein 1.4g/lb Anti-inflammatory whole foods
Weight Maint. Any protocol TDEE matched Protein 0.8g/lb Focus on adherence and variety

Fat Loss Clients

Start with moderate deficit (500 cal). If client is over 25% body fat, low-carb works best. Under 20%, keep carbs moderate and focus on protein timing around workouts.

Muscle Gain Clients

Surplus should be modest (250-400 cal). Push protein to 1.6g/lb minimum. Distribute protein across 4+ meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

Endurance Athletes

Carbs are king. 55-65% of calories from complex carbohydrates. Time high-carb meals 2-3 hours before training. Include recovery meals with 3:1 carb-to-protein ratio.

Body Recomposition

The hardest goal. Requires precise calorie cycling: maintenance on training days, slight deficit on rest days. Protein stays high at all times. Track weekly averages, not daily.
