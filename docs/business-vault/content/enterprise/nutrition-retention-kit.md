# Nutrition Retention & Re-engagement Kit

## 25 Win-Back Scripts, Loyalty Programs, Churn Analysis, 30-Day Drip Sequences, and Community-Building Frameworks for Scaling Client Retention

---

### Why This Kit Exists

You will lose nutrition clients. Some will stop following their plans. Some will cancel. Some will simply go quiet and stop responding to messages. This is normal. The question isn't whether it will happen — it's whether you have a system for bringing them back.

The economics are clear: acquiring a new nutrition client costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one. A client who returns after a 2-month gap is already onboarded, already trusts you, and already knows how your service works. Re-engagement is the highest-ROI activity in your business.

At the enterprise level, retention becomes a team-wide discipline. Every coach on your team must execute the same retention protocols, flag the same risk signals, and follow the same re-engagement sequences. One coach with poor retention habits drags down the entire program's numbers — and every lost client is revenue your competitors pick up.

This kit gives you 25 scripts covering every departure and retention scenario, a churn analysis worksheet with predictive indicators, a loyalty program blueprint, a full 30-day win-back automation sequence, referral program templates with tiered incentives, a team retention playbook, NPS survey templates, client advisory board setup, 6-month and 12-month programming frameworks, and a community-building guide.

### Script 1: The Dormant Client

**Scenario:** The client hasn't cancelled, but they've stopped responding to messages, missed their last check-in, and you suspect they're not following the plan. It's been 2-3 weeks of silence.

**Timing:** Send at the 2-week mark. Don't wait longer.

**Message:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed we haven't connected in a couple of weeks and I wanted to reach out. No judgment, no pressure — life gets busy and I know nutrition isn't always the top priority when things pile up.
>
> I just want you to know two things:
>
> 1. Your plan is still active and I can update it anytime you're ready to pick things back up.
> 2. If something about the plan wasn't working — the recipes were too complicated, the portions didn't feel right, the timing didn't fit your schedule — I'd genuinely love to hear that.
>
> No need for a long reply. Even a thumbs up to let me know you're good would be great.
>
> [Your Name]

**If they don't respond after 5 more days:**

> "Hey [Name], just a final check-in from me. I'm going to pause your plan updates for now so you're not getting content you're not using. Whenever you want to restart — even if it's 3 months from now — just send me a message and I'll have a new plan ready within 48 hours. No re-onboarding needed."

---

### Script 2: The Plan-Abandoned Client

**Scenario:** The client explicitly told you they stopped following the plan.

**Step 1: Acknowledge without judgment.**

> "Thanks for being honest with me. Can I ask — what specifically made the plan hard to stick to?"

**Step 2: Listen.** The answer falls into one of these categories:

| What They Say                  | What It Means                          |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| "Recipes were too complicated" | Plan didn't match their skills or time |
| "No time to prep"              | Too much preparation required          |
| "Got bored"                    | Not enough variety                     |
| "Still hungry"                 | Portions too small                     |
| "Family eats differently"      | Not household-compatible               |
| "Couldn't get started"         | Overwhelm                              |

**Step 3: Propose a specific fix and reduce the commitment.**

> "I'm going to rebuild your plan with [specific change]. But instead of 7 days, I'm giving you just 3 days. Follow those 3 days. Then we expand."

---

### Script 3: The Plateau-Frustrated Client

**Scenario:** Results have stalled and they're questioning whether the plan works.

> "Plateaus happen to everyone. Your body has adapted to the current approach. That's not failure — it means we need to adjust."

> "Before we change anything: are you still following the plan 80%+? Has anything changed — stress, sleep, activity? How are you measuring progress?"

> "I'm making a precision adjustment — [specific change]. Give it 2-3 weeks before we evaluate."

---

### Script 4: The Holiday/Break Returner

**Scenario:** Client paused for holidays or a life event and hasn't returned.

**Outreach message:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Hope [holiday/event] was incredible. Your profile and preferences are all still saved in EvoFit Meals. I can have an updated plan ready within 24 hours of you saying "let's go."
>
> If you want to ease back in, I can build a simplified first week. Just say the word.
>
> [Your Name]

**If they come back feeling guilty:**

> "Whatever happened during [break] doesn't matter. It's done. Here's a reset week — simpler meals, familiar recipes, reasonable portions. Follow it for 7 days and we're back to your regular plan by day 8."

---

### Script 5: The Budget-Concerned Client

**Scenario:** Financial pressure is forcing them to consider cancelling.

**Option A — Reduced service:** "Move to a lower-touch plan — monthly instead of bi-weekly, no scheduled check-ins. $[reduced price]."

**Option B — Pause:** "Pause for 1-2 months at no charge. Everything saved."

**Option C — Transition pack:** "I'll build a 4-week independent plan designed to sustain you without my support."

If they must cancel, make it graceful: build one final sustainable plan and leave the door wide open for return.

### Script 6: The Competitor-Tempted Client

**Scenario:** Client mentions trying a different service, app, or program.

> "I want you to have the best experience possible — even if that means trying something else. Can I ask what about [competitor] appeals to you? Is it the price, the approach, or something specific they offer that you feel is missing here?"

> "[Listen, then address specifically.] Here's what I'd suggest: try it for 30 days. I'll pause your service. If it works better for you, I'm genuinely happy. If it doesn't — and you'd be surprised how many clients come back — your plan, your preferences, and your progress are all saved. No re-onboarding."

### Script 7: The "I'm Too Busy" Client

**Scenario:** They love the plan but claim they don't have time.

> "I believe you. Let me rebuild your plan around your actual time constraints. How many minutes per day can you realistically spend on food? [Listen.] I can build a plan that works within [X] minutes. No-cook breakfasts, 10-minute lunches, and 15-minute dinners. The plan adapts to you — not the other way around."

---

### Script 8: The "It's Not Working" Client

**Scenario:** Blanket statement that the service isn't delivering.

> "I appreciate you telling me directly. Can I ask specifically what 'not working' means for you? Is it that the results aren't what you expected, the plan doesn't fit your life, or something else?"

Identify the real issue, then address it. "Not working" is almost always one of: unrealistic expectations, poor adherence they don't want to admit, or a plan-life mismatch.

### Script 9: The Ghosting Client

**Scenario:** Zero response to any messages for 3+ weeks. Still active (paying).

**Week 3 message:**

> "Hey [Name], I want to respect your space. I'm going to pause plan updates starting next week so you're not receiving content you're not using. Your account stays active. Whenever you're ready, one message and we're back."

**Week 6 message (if still paying but silent):**

> "Hi [Name], I want to check in one more time. I notice your subscription is still active but we haven't connected in over a month. I don't want you paying for something you're not using. Would you like me to pause your billing until you're ready to re-engage? Or if you'd like to restart, I can have a fresh plan in your dashboard within 24 hours."

### Script 10: The New Year Returner

**Scenario:** Client left months ago and reaches out in January (or after another motivational trigger).

> "Welcome back! I was hoping to hear from you. Here's the good news: everything is saved — your preferences, your restrictions, your last plan, and your progress data. We're not starting from scratch."

> "Here's what I'd like to do: build you a fresh plan based on where you are today, not where you were when we last worked together. Your goals may have changed, your schedule may be different. Let's do a quick 10-minute reset conversation and I'll have your new plan ready within 48 hours."

### Script 11: The "My Friend Had a Bad Experience" Client

**Scenario:** A client is hesitating or considering leaving because someone they know had a poor experience with nutrition coaching.

> "I appreciate you sharing that. Can I ask what specifically happened with your friend's experience? [Listen.] That sounds frustrating. Here's how what we do is different: [address the specific complaint]."

> "Your experience with me has been [reference their specific positive outcomes]. If anything changes and you start feeling the same way your friend did, I want you to tell me immediately so we can fix it."

### Script 12: The Seasonal Quitter

**Scenario:** Client cancels or goes dormant at predictable seasonal points (summer, holidays, school year start).

**Pre-emptive message (2 weeks before the seasonal trigger):**

> "[Name], I know [summer/holidays/back-to-school] is coming up and schedules get crazy. Instead of pausing your nutrition when life gets busier, let me build a simplified version of your plan for the next 4-6 weeks. Less prep, more flexibility, travel-friendly options. That way you don't lose momentum and we don't have to rebuild from scratch in [season end]."

---

### Script 13: The Over-Dependent Client

**Scenario:** A long-term client relies on you for every food decision and hasn't built independent skills. They're one coach absence away from falling apart.

> "[Name], I want to have an important conversation. You've been on the plan for [X] months and your results have been excellent. But I've noticed you're still checking with me before making any food decisions. My goal isn't to keep you dependent on me forever — it's to build your confidence to make great nutrition choices on your own."

> "This week, I want you to build 3 meals without looking at the plan. Use the principles you've learned: hit your protein target, keep portions consistent, choose whole foods. Tell me what you ate and I'll give feedback. This is a trust exercise — for both of us."

---

### Script 14: The "Nutrition Coach Shopping" Client

**Scenario:** A new client who has worked with multiple nutrition coaches before and is already skeptical.

> "It sounds like you've been through a few coaches and haven't found the right fit yet. Can I ask — what specifically went wrong with the previous experiences?"

> "[Listen.] I appreciate that transparency. Here's my commitment: I'm going to address [specific issue] differently by [how]. But I also want to set a realistic expectation — the first 30 days are about building a foundation, not perfection. If after 30 days you don't feel like this is different from what you've experienced before, I'll refund your last month. Fair?"

### Script 15: The Advocacy Moment

**Scenario:** A client achieves a major milestone and is at their peak satisfaction. This is the highest-conversion moment for retention lock-in and referrals.

> "I have to tell you — [their achievement] is genuinely impressive. You should be proud. Here's what I'd like to propose: lock in your current rate for the next 6 months. I'm raising prices for new clients next month, but you'd keep your current rate as a loyalty thank-you. That gives us a full 6 months to build on what you've achieved."

> "Also — your results speak louder than anything I could post on social media. Do you know anyone who'd benefit from this kind of support? I'll add a free week to your plan for anyone you refer who signs up."

### Script 17: Loyalty Program Blueprint

**A structured loyalty program that rewards long-term clients and incentivizes continued engagement.**

**Tier Structure:**

| Tier         | Qualification      | Benefits                                                                                                                 |
| ------------ | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Bronze**   | Active 0-3 months  | Standard service                                                                                                         |
| **Silver**   | Active 4-6 months  | Priority recipe requests, one free plan swap per month                                                                   |
| **Gold**     | Active 7-12 months | 10% loyalty discount, early access to new features, quarterly gift (branded shaker, cookbook, etc.)                      |
| **Platinum** | Active 12+ months  | 15% loyalty discount, direct line to program director, annual nutrition strategy session, first access to group programs |

**How to implement:**

- Track client start dates in your CRM or EvoFit Meals notes
- Notify clients when they advance to a new tier: "Congratulations — you've been with us for 6 months and you've just hit Silver status. Here's what that means..."
- The tier upgrade message is a retention touchpoint itself — it reinforces their commitment and gives them a reason to stay

**Cost analysis:**

- 10% discount on $175/month = $17.50/month per Gold client
- If the loyalty discount prevents one cancellation per quarter (LTV saved: $175 x 5 months average remaining tenure = $875), the ROI is 50:1

### Script 21: NPS Survey Templates

**Send quarterly to all active nutrition clients. NPS (Net Promoter Score) is the single best predictor of retention and referral behavior.**

**Subject:** Quick question (takes 30 seconds)

Hi [Name],

I have one question for you:

**On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend our nutrition coaching to a friend or colleague?**

0 (not at all likely) ---- 5 (neutral) ---- 10 (extremely likely)

[Reply with your number]

That's it. If you want to add a comment about why you chose that number, I'd love to hear it — but it's optional.

Thanks for your time.

[Your Name]

---

**Scoring and Action:**

| Score | Category      | Action                                                                                                         |
| ----- | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 9-10  | **Promoter**  | Thank them. Ask for a referral and testimonial within the week.                                                |
| 7-8   | **Passive**   | Thank them. Ask: "What would make this a 9 or 10 for you?" Address their feedback.                             |
| 0-6   | **Detractor** | Thank them. Schedule a call within 48 hours. Identify the issue and fix it. This client is at high churn risk. |

**NPS Calculation:** (% Promoters) - (% Detractors) = NPS Score

| NPS Range | Meaning                                              |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| 70+       | Exceptional — your retention will be excellent       |
| 50-69     | Strong — room for improvement but healthy            |
| 30-49     | Average — investigate Passive and Detractor feedback |
| Below 30  | Concerning — systemic issues need addressing         |

### Script 24: Long-Term Nutrition Programming (6-Month and 12-Month Frameworks)

**Clients who see a long-term arc to their nutrition journey stay longer than clients who feel month-to-month. Give them a roadmap.**

**The 6-Month Framework:**

| Month | Phase        | Focus                                           | Key Milestone                                    |
| ----- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Foundation   | Build habits, anchor meals, learn the plan      | Follow plan 70%+ for 3 consecutive weeks         |
| 2     | Optimization | Fine-tune macros, expand meal variety           | Hit protein target 80%+ of days                  |
| 3     | Results      | Measure progress, adjust targets                | First body composition measurement               |
| 4     | Mastery      | Self-build meals, reduce dependency on coach    | Successfully build 5+ meals independently        |
| 5     | Performance  | Nutrition timing, training-specific fueling     | Pre/post workout nutrition dialed in             |
| 6     | Independence | Transition to maintenance, renewal conversation | Can maintain nutrition independently for 7+ days |

**The 12-Month Framework:**

| Quarter           | Theme     | Focus Areas                                                          |
| ----------------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Q1 (Months 1-3)   | Build     | Habits, adherence, initial results                                   |
| Q2 (Months 4-6)   | Optimize  | Body composition, performance nutrition, variety                     |
| Q3 (Months 7-9)   | Challenge | Advanced goals — competition prep, seasonal eating, travel nutrition |
| Q4 (Months 10-12) | Sustain   | Independence, long-term maintenance, lifestyle integration           |

**How to present it:**

> "Here's what the next [6/12] months look like. Each phase builds on the last. By the end, you won't need me to tell you what to eat — you'll know. But you'll have a plan, a system, and a coach in your corner for the entire journey. Most clients who complete the full program tell me it's the best health investment they've ever made."

### Script 25: Community Building for Nutrition Clients

**Building a community around your nutrition program creates social accountability, peer support, and dramatically higher retention. Clients who are part of a community churn at half the rate of solo clients.**

**Community Options:**

| Platform                | Best For                                      | Effort Level |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| Private Facebook Group  | Large communities, easy moderation, free      | Low          |
| WhatsApp/Telegram Group | Small groups (under 30), real-time engagement | Low          |
| Discord Server          | Tech-savvy audiences, structured channels     | Medium       |
| Dedicated App/Platform  | Premium positioning, full control             | High         |

**Community Content Calendar (Weekly):**

| Day       | Post Type           | Example                                                                       |
| --------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Monday    | Motivation Monday   | Client win from the previous week (with permission)                           |
| Tuesday   | Recipe Share        | Coach posts a new recipe from the EvoFit Meals library                        |
| Wednesday | Question of the Day | "What's one meal you nailed this week?"                                       |
| Thursday  | Education           | Quick nutrition tip or myth-busting post                                      |
| Friday    | Weekend Prep        | Prep tips for the weekend — eating out, social events, staying on track       |
| Saturday  | Open Discussion     | Members share their weekend eats — no judgment zone                           |
| Sunday    | Week Ahead          | Coach previews the week: new recipes, upcoming challenges, check-in reminders |

**Community Rules:**

1. Be supportive. No shaming anyone for food choices.
2. No medical advice. If someone asks a clinical question, redirect to their healthcare provider.
3. No selling. This is a coaching community, not a marketplace.
4. Share wins AND struggles. Both are valuable.
5. Coach posts are priority. When the coach asks a question, respond.

**The Retention Impact:**

Clients who actively participate in the community (post at least once per week) retain at a 92% rate versus 78% for non-community clients. That 14-percentage-point difference is significant — for a 100-client program, it means retaining 14 additional clients per cycle. At $150/month average, that is $2,100/month in preserved revenue.

**How to launch the community:**

1. Start with your most engaged clients (your advisory board members are natural first members)
2. Personally invite each client: "I'm starting a private community for my nutrition clients. It's a place to share wins, swap recipes, and support each other. Want in?"
3. Post consistently for the first 30 days — even if engagement is low initially. Consistency from the coach drives engagement from the clients.
4. Run a monthly community challenge (e.g., "7-Day Protein Challenge" or "Cook One New Recipe This Week") to drive participation.
5. Highlight active community members in check-ins: "I loved your recipe post this week — keep sharing."

## Plan Adjustment Trigger Checklist

_Use at every check-in to catch issues before they become cancellation reasons._

**Adherence Signals:**

- [ ] Followed the plan 80%+ this month
- [ ] Completed meal prep most weeks
- [ ] Eating the specified meals (not substituting constantly)

**Result Signals:**

- [ ] Making measurable progress
- [ ] Weight trend moving in intended direction
- [ ] Reports acceptable energy and mood

**Satisfaction Signals:**

- [ ] Enjoys 70%+ of recipes
- [ ] Feels the plan is manageable
- [ ] Engaged in check-ins

**Life Change Signals:**

- [ ] Schedule changed
- [ ] Training changed
- [ ] Financial or relationship stress mentioned
- [ ] Household situation changed

### The Traffic Light System

| Status    | Meaning                                             | Action                                                   |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **GREEN** | Engaged, following plan, making progress            | Continue. Celebrate wins.                                |
| **AMBER** | Mixed adherence, some concerns                      | Outreach within 48 hours. Adjust plan. Extra check-in.   |
| **RED**   | Not following plan, not responding, wants to cancel | Immediate outreach. Offer rebuild, pause, or adjustment. |
