# Nutrition Client Onboarding Pack

## Dietary Questionnaires, Food Preference Surveys, Allergy Forms, Welcome Sequences, 30-Day Automated Drip, and Team-Based Onboarding SOPs

---

### What This Is

This is everything you need to onboard a new nutrition client professionally at enterprise scale. It includes the complete set of intake forms, a video welcome script, a comprehensive FAQ covering 20+ questions, a family meal accommodation guide, a full 30-day automated onboarding email drip, and a team-based onboarding SOP so every coach on your team delivers the same experience.

Copy the templates, customize them for your brand, and use them every time a new client signs up. A structured onboarding process does three things: it collects the information you need to build an accurate plan, it sets expectations so the client knows exactly what to expect, and it signals professionalism that justifies your pricing.

Each template in this pack is ready to use. Adjust the wording to match your voice, add your branding, and send them out.

**How Your Plan Is Built**

Your meal plan is created using EvoFit Meals, a professional nutrition platform. Here's the process:

1. I review your questionnaire, preferences, and restrictions
2. I calculate your calorie and macro targets based on your goals, activity level, and body composition
3. I generate a plan that matches your preferences, cooking skills, and schedule
4. I review every plan personally before it reaches you — no generic output
5. You receive the plan with full recipes, portions, and a shopping list

If something doesn't work — a recipe you hate, an ingredient you can't find, a day that doesn't fit your schedule — tell me and I'll adjust it. The plan should serve you, not the other way around.

---

**How to Get the Best Results**

1. **Follow the plan for at least 5 full days before requesting changes.** Give your body and your routine a chance to adapt. Minor discomfort in the first few days (cravings, adjustment to new foods) is normal.

2. **Prep what you can.** You don't need to prep every meal, but having 2-3 days of lunches ready to go dramatically improves adherence. Your plan includes prep-friendly meals for this reason.

3. **Track your intake for the first 2 weeks.** Even rough tracking helps you build awareness of portions. After 2 weeks, most clients can eyeball portions accurately.

4. **Communicate honestly.** If you skip meals, eat off-plan, or have a rough week — tell me. I'm not here to judge. I'm here to adjust. A plan that accounts for real life works better than a perfect plan you can't follow.

5. **Ask questions.** There are no dumb questions in nutrition. If you don't understand why a food is in your plan, or you want to swap something, or you're confused about a recipe — ask.

**Frequently Asked Questions: Your Nutrition Service**

**Getting Started**

**Q1: How quickly will I receive my first meal plan?**
Within 48 hours of submitting your completed dietary questionnaire, food preference survey, and allergy form. If you complete the forms on Monday, you'll have your plan by Wednesday at the latest.

**Q2: What if I don't have time to fill out the forms right away?**
The sooner you complete them, the sooner your plan is built. That said, there is no expiration. Complete them when you can — but know that your plan clock starts when I receive the completed forms, not when you sign up.

**Q3: Can I sign up for a specific start date?**
Yes. Let me know your preferred start date and I'll time the plan delivery accordingly. This is common for clients who want to start on a Monday or after a vacation.

**Q4: Do I need any special kitchen equipment?**
No. All recipes work with standard kitchen equipment: stovetop, oven, and basic utensils. If you have an air fryer, Instant Pot, or slow cooker, let me know in your preference survey and I'll include recipes that use them.

**The Meal Plan**

**Q5: How many meals per day will my plan include?**
This is based on your preference — most clients get 3 meals and 1-2 snacks. If you prefer 2 meals, 4 meals, or intermittent fasting, I'll build around that.

**Q6: Can I swap meals I don't like?**
Absolutely. Message me and I'll swap any meal within 24 hours. No limits on swaps. The plan should include food you enjoy eating.

**Q7: What if I can't find an ingredient at my local store?**
Let me know and I'll suggest a substitute or swap the recipe entirely. If a specific ingredient is consistently unavailable in your area, I'll remove it from all future plans.

**Q8: Are the recipes complicated?**
Most recipes take 15-25 minutes. I tailor the complexity to your cooking skill level (which you indicate in the preference survey). If you're a beginner, your plan will feature simple, minimal-ingredient recipes.

**Q9: Will the plan include a grocery list?**
Yes. Every plan comes with a consolidated grocery list organized by category (proteins, produce, dairy, pantry staples). It accounts for all meals in the plan so you can shop once and have everything you need.

**Q10: Can I eat the same meals as my family?**
Yes. I build plans with family-friendly meals wherever possible. The main difference is your portion sizes, which are calibrated to your specific calorie and macro targets. See the Family Meal Accommodation Guide below for details.

**Nutrition and Results**

**Q11: How soon will I see results?**
Most clients notice changes in energy and digestion within the first week. Visible body composition changes typically appear by weeks 3-4. Measurable weight changes depend on your starting point and goal — I'll set realistic expectations at your first check-in.

**Q12: Do I have to count calories or track macros?**
No. Your plan has everything pre-calculated. If you follow the plan as written — eating the portions specified — your calories and macros are handled automatically. Tracking is optional and only recommended for clients who enjoy data.

**Q13: What if I eat off-plan?**
It happens. Don't stress it. Get back to the plan at your next meal. One off-plan meal doesn't undo a week of good nutrition. If you consistently eat off-plan, tell me so we can figure out what's not working.

**Q14: Can I drink alcohol on this plan?**
In moderation, yes. Let me know your typical alcohol consumption and I'll account for it in your calorie targets. I won't tell you to stop drinking — I'll build a framework that accommodates responsible consumption while keeping you on track.

**Q15: Will this plan work if I have dietary restrictions?**
Yes. The EvoFit Meals platform supports every common dietary restriction: gluten-free, dairy-free, nut-free, vegan, vegetarian, pescatarian, halal, kosher, keto, paleo, low-FODMAP, and more. Your allergy form captures all restrictions before your first plan is built.

**Q16: What happens if I hit a plateau?**
Plateaus are normal and expected. At your next check-in, I'll review your adherence data, recalculate your macro targets if needed, and adjust your plan. Plateaus usually break within 2-3 weeks of targeted adjustments.

**Q17: Is this safe if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?**
General nutrition guidance is within scope, but pregnancy and breastfeeding require specific nutritional considerations. I'll coordinate with your OB/GYN or midwife to ensure your plan meets the increased calorie and nutrient requirements during this period.

**Coaching and Support**

**Q18: How often will I hear from my coach?**
Your check-in frequency depends on your package — monthly, bi-weekly, or weekly. Between check-ins, you have messaging access for quick questions. Your coach aims to respond within [X] business hours.

**Q19: Can I change my coach?**
Yes. If for any reason you'd prefer to work with a different coach on our team, let the program director know. We'll transfer your full profile and plan history to the new coach seamlessly.

**Q20: What if I have a question that my coach can't answer?**
Your coach will escalate to the program director or refer you to a registered dietitian if the question falls outside the scope of general nutrition coaching. We always prioritize getting you accurate information from the right professional.

**Billing and Account**

Monthly, on the same date you signed up. You'll receive a receipt via email with each charge.

**Q22: Can I pause my subscription?**
Yes. We offer pauses of up to [X] months for travel, illness, or life events. Your profile, preferences, and plan history are preserved. When you return, your coach can have a new plan ready within 24 hours.

## Template 8: Family Meal Accommodation Guide

_Send this to any client who lives with a partner, children, or roommates. It addresses the number one logistical objection to following a meal plan._

---

**Family Meal Accommodation Guide**
_How to follow your meal plan without cooking separate meals for your household._

---

**The Core Principle**

Your meal plan is designed so that your household eats the same dinner. The only difference is your portion size and possibly a side dish. Nobody at the table should feel like they're eating "diet food" — because they aren't.

**How It Works**

| Meal Component   | Your Version                        | Family Version                                     |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Main protein     | Weighed to your macro target        | Served family-style, everyone takes what they want |
| Starch/carb side | Portioned to your plan              | Served in a bowl for the table                     |
| Vegetables       | Included as specified in your plan  | Served alongside — encourage but don't force       |
| Sauce/dressing   | Measured if included in your macros | Available for everyone                             |

**Example Dinner Scenario:**

Your plan says: Grilled chicken thigh (180g), roasted sweet potato (150g), steamed broccoli (1 cup), with a tablespoon of olive oil.

What you cook: Enough grilled chicken for the family, a tray of roasted sweet potatoes, and steamed broccoli.

What happens at the table: You plate your portions according to the plan. Your partner takes whatever they want. The kids take chicken and sweet potato, skip the broccoli (predictable), and you add a side of pasta for them if they need more.

**One meal cooked. Everyone eats. Your macros are hit.**

**Strategies for Common Family Situations**

**Picky kids:** Keep your core recipe family-friendly and add a "kid-approved" side (pasta, bread, fruit). Your plan accommodates this by building the core dinner around universally accepted foods.

**Partner who hates "healthy food":** The recipes in your plan are not health-food stereotypes. They include things like taco bowls, stir-fries, burgers (with quality ingredients), pasta dishes, and sheet-pan dinners. Show your partner the weekly menu — most are surprised by how normal the food looks.

**Multi-generational household:** If you're cooking for parents or grandparents with different dietary needs (low sodium, soft foods, etc.), let your coach know during onboarding. We can build your dinners around a base recipe that accommodates multiple needs with simple modifications.

**Roommates who share the kitchen:** If you're not cooking for others, this doesn't apply. But if you share groceries or cooking duties, let your coach know and we'll factor in shared ingredients and cooking schedules.

**Meal Prep With a Family**

The biggest win for families: **batch prep proteins and carbs on Sunday, then assemble meals differently for each person throughout the week.**

- Cook 2 proteins (e.g., chicken and ground turkey) in bulk
- Cook 2 carbs (e.g., rice and sweet potatoes) in bulk
- Wash and chop vegetables for the week
- Your dinners are assembled from these components according to your plan
- Family dinners are assembled from the same components in whatever combination they prefer

Total additional prep time versus cooking for yourself: 15-20 minutes per week.

## Template 10: Team-Based Onboarding SOP

_This SOP is for program directors and lead coaches managing a team. It standardizes the onboarding process so every new client receives the same quality experience regardless of which coach handles them._

---

**Nutrition Client Onboarding SOP**
**Version:** 1.0
**Applies to:** All nutrition coaches, intake coordinators, and program directors

---

**Purpose:** Ensure every new nutrition client is onboarded consistently, professionally, and within the committed timelines.

**Scope:** From client signup through delivery of first meal plan and scheduling of first check-in.

**Roles:**

| Role               | Responsibility                                                                                    |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Intake Coordinator | Sends forms, collects completed forms, creates client profile in EvoFit Meals, assigns to a coach |
| Assigned Coach     | Reviews forms, builds meal plan, delivers plan, sends welcome message, schedules first check-in   |
| Program Director   | Monitors onboarding pipeline, handles escalations, reviews quality of first plans                 |

**Timeline:**

| Step | Action                                                               | Owner                     | Deadline                                                       |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Client signs up                                                      | System/Sales              | Day 0                                                          |
| 2    | Welcome email sent with forms attached                               | Intake Coordinator        | Within 2 hours of signup                                       |
| 3    | Video welcome recorded and sent                                      | Assigned Coach            | Within 24 hours of signup                                      |
| 4    | Forms completed by client                                            | Client                    | Day 0-3 (follow up if not received by Day 2)                   |
| 5    | Client profile created in EvoFit Meals with all restrictions flagged | Intake Coordinator        | Within 4 hours of receiving completed forms                    |
| 6    | Client assigned to a coach based on capacity and specialty           | Intake Coordinator        | Same day as profile creation                                   |
| 7    | Coach reviews forms and builds first meal plan                       | Assigned Coach            | Within 48 hours of form completion                             |
| 8    | Program Director reviews first plan for quality                      | Program Director          | Within 24 hours of plan creation (spot-check 20% of new plans) |
| 9    | Meal plan delivered to client with personal message                  | Assigned Coach            | Within 48 hours of form completion                             |
| 10   | First check-in scheduled                                             | Assigned Coach            | At plan delivery — check-in set for 7-10 days after delivery   |
| 11   | Automated drip email sequence activated                              | System/Intake Coordinator | Day 0 (triggered by signup)                                    |

**Quality Standards:**

- Every first plan must be reviewed by the coach for accuracy before delivery — no auto-generated plans go out unreviewed
- Allergy flags must be double-checked: cross-reference the allergy form with the plan's ingredients
- The welcome message must include the client's name and reference their specific goal
- Forms that are incomplete must be returned to the client with specific guidance on what's missing
- If a client reports a medical condition on the questionnaire, the Intake Coordinator flags it to the Program Director before assignment

**Escalation Triggers:**

| Trigger                                                          | Escalate To                                     | Action                                                                        |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Client forms not received within 72 hours                        | Intake Coordinator sends a reminder, CC's coach | Follow up with a phone call if still missing at Day 5                         |
| Client reports medical condition requiring dietary management    | Program Director                                | Director reviews and determines if RD referral is needed before plan is built |
| Client reports 3+ severe allergies                               | Program Director                                | Director reviews the plan personally before delivery                          |
| First plan not built within 48-hour window                       | Lead Coach                                      | Lead Coach builds the plan or reassigns the client                            |
| Client expresses dissatisfaction with first plan within 48 hours | Assigned Coach + Lead Coach                     | Coach makes immediate adjustments; Lead Coach reviews for systemic issues     |

**Monthly Review:**

The Program Director reviews onboarding metrics monthly:

- Average time from signup to plan delivery (target: under 48 hours)
- Form completion rate within 72 hours (target: 90%+)
- First-plan satisfaction score (target: 4.0+ out of 5)
- Drop-off rate within first 14 days (target: under 10%)
