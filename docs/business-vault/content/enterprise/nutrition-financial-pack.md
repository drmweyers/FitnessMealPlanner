### About This Pack

Running a nutrition coaching business without financial visibility is like coaching a client without tracking their macros — you are guessing. This pack gives you the financial tools to run your nutrition program like a real business: a profit and loss template designed for nutrition services, a tax preparation checklist specific to food and nutrition businesses, expense tracking categories, quarterly business review templates, revenue projection models at three growth scenarios, a cost-per-client calculator, break-even analysis, and profit margin benchmarks by service type.

Print the templates. Fill them in monthly. Review them quarterly. The trainers who succeed in nutrition coaching are the ones who know their numbers — not just their clients' numbers.

### How to Read Your P&L

**Gross margin above 60%:** Your pricing and coach compensation are healthy. Focus on growing revenue.

**Gross margin 40-60%:** Acceptable, but look for efficiency gains. Can EvoFit Meals reduce plan creation time? Are coaches spending too much time per client?

**Gross margin below 40%:** Either your pricing is too low or your coach costs are too high. Address before scaling.

**Net margin above 30%:** Excellent. Reinvest in marketing and team growth.

**Net margin 15-30%:** Healthy for a service business. Look for operating expense optimization.

**Net margin below 15%:** Investigate operating expenses. You may be spending too much on marketing relative to results, or administrative costs are too high.

**Review this checklist quarterly. Complete it fully before year-end tax preparation.**

### Income Documentation

- [ ] Total nutrition revenue by service type (monthly subscriptions, one-off plans, challenges, corporate)
- [ ] Revenue by payment method (credit card, bank transfer, cash, corporate invoice)
- [ ] All 1099 forms received (if operating as independent contractor)
- [ ] All 1099 forms issued (if subcontracting other coaches)
- [ ] Corporate contract invoices and payment records

### Deductible Expenses

**Business Operations:**

- [ ] EvoFit Meals platform subscription receipts
- [ ] CRM and software tool subscription receipts
- [ ] Professional liability insurance premiums
- [ ] Business phone and internet (allocated percentage)
- [ ] Office supplies (printer ink, paper for forms, etc.)
- [ ] Branded merchandise purchased for client gifts
- [ ] Client meeting expenses (coffee, co-working space)

**Education and Professional Development:**

- [ ] Nutrition certification costs (Precision Nutrition, NASM CNC, ISSA, etc.)
- [ ] Continuing education courses and workshops
- [ ] Conference registration fees and travel
- [ ] Books and educational materials related to nutrition coaching
- [ ] Industry membership dues (NSCA, ACSM, etc.)

**Marketing:**

- [ ] Paid advertising receipts (Facebook, Google, Instagram, LinkedIn)
- [ ] Website hosting and domain costs
- [ ] Graphic design and content creation expenses
- [ ] Printed marketing materials (flyers, brochures, business cards)
- [ ] Photography costs (food photography, client photos with consent)

**Home Office (if applicable):**

- [ ] Square footage calculation for dedicated workspace
- [ ] Allocated rent or mortgage interest
- [ ] Allocated utilities
- [ ] Home office furniture and equipment

**Vehicle (if applicable):**

- [ ] Mileage log for business-related travel (client visits, corporate presentations, grocery runs for recipe testing)
- [ ] Vehicle expenses (gas, insurance, maintenance) allocated by business use percentage

**Food and Recipe Testing:**

- [ ] Grocery receipts for recipe development and testing
- [ ] Dining expenses for menu research (with business purpose documented)
- [ ] Note: Keep receipts and document the business purpose for every food-related expense

### Important Tax Notes for Nutrition Businesses

1. **Keep digital copies of all receipts.** Use a receipt scanning app or photograph receipts on the day of purchase. Paper receipts fade.

2. **Separate business and personal accounts.** If you haven't already, open a dedicated business bank account and business credit card. Commingling personal and business expenses creates tax headaches.

3. **Track income by source.** Your accountant needs to know which revenue comes from individuals versus corporate contracts versus challenges. Different sources may have different tax implications.

4. **Estimated quarterly taxes.** If your nutrition business generates significant income, you likely owe estimated quarterly tax payments (April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15). Underpayment triggers penalties.

5. **Meal and entertainment deductions.** Under current tax law, business meals are typically 50% deductible. However, meals provided to clients as part of a business event may qualify for 100% deduction. Consult your accountant.

6. **Equipment depreciation.** If you purchased laptops, tablets, cameras, or kitchen equipment for the business, these may be depreciable assets. Your accountant can determine the optimal depreciation method.

### Projection Rules of Thumb

- **Acquisition rate is the growth engine.** The difference between 3 and 8 new clients per month compounds dramatically over 12 months.
- **Churn rate is the retention multiplier.** Reducing churn by 3 percentage points (e.g., 15% to 12%) adds more revenue than acquiring 2 extra clients per month.
- **Use the moderate scenario for planning, the conservative for cash flow, and the aggressive for fundraising or partnership pitches.**

## Section 8: Profit Margin Benchmarks by Service Type

**Use these benchmarks to evaluate which service types to prioritize for growth.**

| Service Type                  | Typical Revenue         | Typical Cost  | Typical Margin | Notes                                            |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Basic meal plan (no coaching) | $75-125/mo              | $25-40/mo     | 60-70%         | Lowest touch, highest margin per hour            |
| Standard nutrition coaching   | $150-200/mo             | $50-70/mo     | 60-65%         | Best balance of revenue and retention            |
| Premium coaching (weekly)     | $250-400/mo             | $100-150/mo   | 55-65%         | Higher revenue but more coach time               |
| Training + nutrition bundle   | $75-150/mo add-on       | $25-45/mo     | 65-70%         | Coach time is shared with training sessions      |
| One-off meal plans            | $100-300 one-time       | $40-80        | 60-73%         | No recurring revenue — good for leads            |
| Group nutrition (per person)  | $50-100/mo              | $10-20/mo     | 75-80%         | Highest margin — coach time divided across group |
| Corporate wellness (PEPM)     | $15-200/mo PEPM         | $5-80/mo PEPM | 60-70%         | Volume-dependent; higher administrative cost     |
| Nutrition challenges          | $97-197 per participant | $30-60        | 60-70%         | Event-based; strong lead gen value               |

**Strategic insight:** Group nutrition programs and basic meal plans have the highest margins. Premium coaching has the highest absolute profit per client. The optimal service mix depends on your growth phase:

- **Early stage (0-20 clients):** Focus on standard coaching and bundles — build recurring revenue.
- **Growth stage (20-50 clients):** Add group programs and challenges — scale without proportional coach hiring.
- **Enterprise stage (50+ clients):** Layer in corporate wellness and franchise models — volume plays with high margins.
