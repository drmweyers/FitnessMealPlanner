# 90-Day Nutrition Adherence Protocol

## A Full Automation-Ready System With Team Delegation, KPI Tracking, and Churn Prediction for Scaling Nutrition Coaching Operations

---

### The Adherence Framework

Training adherence and nutrition adherence are two completely different problems. When a client skips a workout, they know they skipped it. When a client drifts off their meal plan, they barely notice until the results stall. There is no trainer standing next to them at the kitchen counter. There is no gym buddy texting "where are you?" when they open a takeout app instead of prepping lunch.

Nutrition adherence happens at home, alone, surrounded by family preferences, emotional triggers, and a lifetime of habits. That is why the drop-off rate for meal plans is higher than for training programs, and why most trainers hand over a meal plan and never hear about it again until the client complains that "nothing is working."

This protocol fixes that. It gives you a 90-day system of specific touchpoints, messages, and interventions designed for the unique challenge of keeping clients on their nutrition plans. At the enterprise level, this protocol is designed for team-based execution — meaning a gym owner or program director can delegate each phase to different coaches while maintaining quality and consistency across the entire client roster.

**Why 90 days?** Research consistently shows that dietary habits take 66-90 days to become automatic. Clients who follow a meal plan for 90 days are 4x more likely to maintain those habits at the 6-month mark. Your goal is to get them through those 90 days.

**The Three Drop-Off Triggers:**

1. **Week 1 Overwhelm.** The meal plan looks like a foreign language. Too many new foods, too much prep time, too many decisions. They feel like they have to change everything at once. Result: they abandon the plan entirely by Day 5 and go back to what they know.

2. **Week 3 Boredom.** The initial motivation has faded. They have eaten the same chicken-and-rice meal seven times. They are tired of cooking. The novelty of having a plan is gone, and the results have not arrived yet. Result: they start "improvising" meals that drift further and further from the plan.

3. **Month 2 Plateau.** The early wins (water weight, bloating reduction, energy boost) have leveled off. The scale is not moving. They start questioning whether the plan is working. Result: they either quit quietly or ask to "try something different" — which usually means a fad diet they saw online.

Every phase of this protocol is designed to pre-empt these triggers before they cause a drop-off.

---

### How to Use This Protocol

Each phase has:

- **Goal:** What you are trying to achieve
- **Weekly breakdown:** Specific actions, messages, and check-ins
- **Templates:** Copy-paste messages you can send from your EvoFit Meals dashboard
- **Red Flags:** Warning signs that adherence is slipping
- **Intervention scripts:** Exactly what to say when you spot trouble

Time investment: roughly 10-15 minutes per client per week. That is the cost of a client who actually gets results, renews, and refers.

**Enterprise note:** If you are managing a team, each coach handles the touchpoints for their assigned clients. The program director reviews KPI dashboards weekly and intervenes only when a coach's client metrics fall below threshold. This keeps overhead low while maintaining quality at scale.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

**Goal:** Remove overwhelm and build one successful nutrition habit before adding complexity.

### Week 1

**Day 1 (Plan Delivery):**

_Touchpoint:_ Send a welcome message within 1 hour of delivering their meal plan. Do not just hand them the plan and disappear.

> "Hey [Name]! Your meal plan is live in your EvoFit Meals dashboard. Before you look at the full plan, I want you to focus on ONE thing this week: nailing your breakfasts. That's it. Don't worry about perfecting every meal. If you can get your morning meal on track for 5 out of 7 days, we're winning. I'll check in Wednesday to see how it's going."

**Why one meal?** Because telling someone to change breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks simultaneously is a recipe for failure. One anchor meal builds confidence. Confidence builds momentum. Momentum builds adherence.

_Touchpoint:_ Send a prep reminder Sunday evening (or the day before their meal prep day).

> "Quick reminder — if you can spend 20-30 minutes tonight prepping your breakfasts for the week, the rest takes care of itself. Your plan has a prep-friendly option for every breakfast. Check the recipe details in your dashboard for batch-prep tips."

**Day 3 (Mid-week check-in):**

_Touchpoint:_ Send a brief check-in. Keep it specific.

> "Hey [Name], how's the breakfast routine going? Have you tried the [specific recipe from their plan] yet? That one's a client favorite. Let me know if anything feels off — portions too big, ingredients you can't find, prep taking too long. I can swap things around for you in two minutes."

**Day 5-7:**

_Touchpoint:_ End-of-week acknowledgment. Reference something specific.

> "[Name], you made it through Week 1. That's the hardest week for everyone — the plan is new, the grocery list is different, and the kitchen routine hasn't clicked yet. If you hit your breakfast target most days, you're ahead of 80% of people who start a meal plan. How did it feel?"

**Red Flags:**

- They do not open their meal plan in the dashboard within 48 hours
- They message you asking if they "really have to eat all of this" (overwhelm signal)
- They do not respond to your Day 3 check-in

**Intervention:** If they seem overwhelmed, simplify immediately. Open their plan in EvoFit Meals and swap complex recipes for simpler alternatives.

> "I hear you — it's a lot when it's all new. I just swapped a few of your meals for simpler versions. Same nutrition, half the prep time. Check your updated plan. We'll build up to the more involved recipes once this feels easy."

### Week 2

**Focus shift:** Expand from one meal to two. Add lunch or dinner to the plan (whichever they are more likely to control — usually lunch if they work from home, dinner if they cook for themselves).

_Touchpoint:_ Start of Week 2 message.

> "You nailed the breakfast routine last week. This week, let's add [lunch/dinner] to the mix. Same approach — don't aim for perfect, aim for consistent. If you hit both meals 4-5 days out of 7, that's excellent progress. Your plan already has everything laid out."

_Touchpoint:_ Mid-week check-in with a practical focus.

> "How's the grocery shopping going? One trick that helps a lot of my clients: do one big shop on Sunday and one quick top-up on Wednesday. That way you're never stuck without ingredients. Are there any items on your list that are hard to find? I can swap those out."

**Red Flags:**

- They mention their family is not on board ("My partner won't eat this")
- Grocery costs come up as a concern
- They skip the second meal entirely and only stick with breakfast

**Intervention for family resistance:**

> "Totally get it — feeding a family is a different game. Let's adjust your plan so dinner is something everyone can eat, with your portion controlled separately. I'll swap in some family-friendly recipes. Your nutrition targets stay the same, but nobody at the table feels like they're on a diet."

Use the recipe swap feature in EvoFit Meals to replace individual meals without rebuilding the entire plan.

**Week 2 Checkpoint:**

- [ ] Welcome message sent within 1 hour of plan delivery
- [ ] Sunday prep reminder sent
- [ ] Mid-week check-in sent (Week 1 and Week 2)
- [ ] End-of-week acknowledgment sent
- [ ] Week 2 expansion message sent
- [ ] Any overwhelm signals addressed with recipe swaps
- [ ] Family/budget concerns addressed if raised

## Phase 2: Adaptation (Weeks 3-4)

**Goal:** Break through the boredom wall. Introduce variety before monotony kills motivation.

### Week 3

**The Boredom Wall:**

This is where most meal plans die. The excitement of something new is gone. They have eaten the same meals for two weeks. The results are not dramatic enough to sustain motivation on their own. You need to inject freshness.

_Touchpoint:_ Proactive variety message. Do not wait for them to complain.

> "Hey [Name], I just refreshed a few meals on your plan — swapped in some new recipes to keep things interesting. Check out [specific recipe name] for dinner this week. It's one of the most popular recipes in the system. Same macros, completely different flavor profile. Let me know what you think."

Use the plan adjustment feature in EvoFit Meals to rotate 2-3 meals. This takes under a minute but signals that you are actively managing their experience.

_Touchpoint:_ Reduce check-in frequency from mid-week to twice weekly. You are building their independence, not dependence on your messages.

> "Quick check-in — how's adherence feeling this week? Rate yourself 1-5 (1 = totally off plan, 5 = nailing it). No judgment either way. I just want to know where we stand so I can help."

**Red Flags:**

- They rate themselves 1-2 on the adherence scale
- They mention eating out frequently ("I've been so busy, just grabbing food on the go")
- They stop logging meals or viewing the plan in the dashboard

**Intervention for low adherence:**

> "Thanks for being honest. A 2 is not failure — it's information. Let's figure out what's tripping you up. Is it the cooking time, the ingredients, or just not feeling excited about the meals? I can fix any of those. The worst thing we can do is keep a plan you're not following."

### Week 4

**The One-Month Conversation:**

_Touchpoint:_ Schedule a 5-10 minute call or in-app voice note. This is not a text — it is a conversation.

Cover three questions:

1. "What meals are you actually enjoying?"
2. "What's been the hardest part of sticking to the plan?"
3. "Is there anything going on outside of nutrition that's making this harder?"

That third question matters. Nutrition adherence is downstream of life stress, sleep, relationships, and work. If they just started a new job or their kid is sick, their meal prep will suffer. Acknowledge it. Adjust expectations.

_Touchpoint:_ Send a one-month summary.

> "One month in! Here's where you stand:
>
> - **Adherence:** You've followed the plan [X]% of the time — that's [above average / solid / something we'll build on]
> - **Habit formed:** Your breakfasts are locked in. That wasn't happening 4 weeks ago.
> - **Macro average:** You're hitting roughly [X]g protein daily, up from [Y]g when we started
> - **Non-scale win:** [Something they told you — 'better energy in the afternoon' / 'sleeping deeper' / 'less bloating']
>
> The first month is the hardest. You've done it. Now we build on the foundation."

**Red Flags:**

- They do not show up for the one-month call
- They say "I've just been doing my own thing" (they have gone off-plan)
- They ask to "take a break" from the meal plan

**Intervention for "taking a break":**

> "I hear you. Nutrition fatigue is real, and I'd rather adjust the plan than have you abandon it. What if we simplify for the next two weeks? Fewer unique meals, more repeat-friendly options, and I'll drop the prep time requirement. A simpler plan you follow beats a perfect plan you don't."

**Week 4 Checkpoint:**

- [ ] Proactive recipe swaps made in Week 3
- [ ] Adherence self-rating requested
- [ ] One-month conversation completed
- [ ] One-month summary sent
- [ ] Plan adjusted based on feedback
- [ ] Any "boredom wall" signals addressed

## Phase 3: Results Phase (Weeks 5-8)

**Goal:** Make progress visible and undeniable. Connect their daily meal choices to measurable outcomes.

### Weeks 5-6

**Measurement Checkpoint:**

_Touchpoint:_ Request progress data. Be specific about what you want.

> "[Name], it's time for a progress check. This week, I need three things from you:
>
> 1. Current weight (same scale, same time of day as your starting weigh-in)
> 2. A progress photo (same lighting and angle as your starting photo — I know it's awkward, but this is the most honest measure we have)
> 3. Your honest answer: do your clothes fit differently?
>
> Send them to me through the app whenever you're ready."

_Touchpoint:_ Deliver the results. Even modest progress should be framed as meaningful.

> "Here's what I'm seeing after 5 weeks:
>
> - Weight: [X] to [Y] ([Z] lbs change)
> - Photo comparison: [specific observation — 'visible reduction around your midsection' / 'your face is noticeably leaner']
> - Your protein consistency has improved from [X]% to [Y]% of target
>
> This is real. This is your body responding to what you're feeding it. The next 4 weeks is where it accelerates."

**Macro Adjustments:**

If progress has stalled, this is where you earn your fee. Review their adherence data in EvoFit Meals and make targeted adjustments.

> "I looked at your numbers and I'm making a small tweak — dropping your carbs slightly on rest days and keeping them higher on training days. This is a precision adjustment, not a diet overhaul. You won't feel a difference in hunger, but your body composition should start moving again. Updated plan is in your dashboard."

**Red Flags:**

- They refuse to send progress photos (shame signal — they think they have not made progress)
- The scale has not moved and they are getting frustrated
- They mention trying a supplement, cleanse, or fad diet they saw online

**Intervention for fad diet temptation:**

> "I get the temptation — [fad diet] sounds like a shortcut. But here's the thing: you've spent 5 weeks building real habits. A crash approach will undo that in days. The scale stall you're seeing is normal at this stage — your body is recomposing. Let's give the macro adjustment two weeks. If the scale still hasn't moved, we'll dig deeper. Deal?"

**Scope-of-Practice Note:** If a client presents symptoms that suggest a medical or clinical nutrition issue — disordered eating patterns, food allergies or intolerances, hormonal imbalances affecting weight, or any diagnosed condition requiring therapeutic diet management — refer them to a registered dietitian. Your role is meal plan adherence support, not clinical nutrition intervention. Know the line.

> "I want to be upfront with you — what you're describing sounds like it might need a specialist's input. I'm going to recommend you see a registered dietitian who can evaluate [specific concern]. I'll keep managing your meal plan on this side, and if the dietitian recommends changes, I'll build those into your plan. You'll have both of us in your corner."

### Weeks 7-8

**Social Proof and Momentum:**

_Touchpoint:_ Ask for a testimonial. Timing matters — ask when they have just seen their progress data.

> "You've made some really impressive progress. Would you be comfortable sharing a quick quote about your experience? Something like 2-3 sentences about what's changed. I'll use it (with your permission) to help other clients see what's possible. Totally optional, but your story could help someone take the first step."

_Touchpoint:_ Introduce a nutrition-specific challenge to re-engage.

> "I have a challenge for you this week: hit your protein target 7 days in a row. Not close — exact. You've been averaging [X]g and your target is [Y]g. Seven days. If you do it, I guarantee you'll feel a difference in your energy and recovery. You in?"

**Red Flags:**

- Their adherence self-rating has dropped below 3 for two consecutive weeks
- They have stopped viewing their plan in the app (check their activity in EvoFit Meals)
- They are less responsive to messages (replies getting slower or shorter)

**Intervention for disengagement:**

> "[Name], I noticed things have been quieter on your end. No judgment at all — life happens. But I'd rather check in than assume. Are you still feeling good about the plan, or do we need to have an honest conversation about what's working and what isn't? I'm on your team either way."

**Week 8 Checkpoint:**

- [ ] Progress measurements taken (weight, photos, clothing fit)
- [ ] Results delivered with specific data points
- [ ] Macro adjustments made if needed
- [ ] Testimonial requested
- [ ] Nutrition challenge issued
- [ ] Any plateau frustration addressed
- [ ] Scope-of-practice boundaries maintained

## Phase 4: Independence Phase (Weeks 9-12)

**Goal:** Transition the client from plan-dependent to self-managing. Have the renewal conversation. Ask for referrals.

### Weeks 9-10

**Building Self-Management:**

The client should now be making good nutrition decisions without checking the plan for every meal. Your job shifts from directing to coaching.

_Touchpoint:_ Test their independence.

> "[Name], I want to try something this week. For three of your meals, I want you to build them yourself using the principles from your plan — hit your protein target, keep your portions consistent, choose whole foods. Don't check the plan for those three meals. Then tell me what you ate and I'll give you feedback. I want to see if the habits are sticking."

_Touchpoint:_ Provide feedback on their self-built meals.

> "Here's what I noticed about your self-planned meals:
>
> - Protein: [on target / a bit low — here's how to fix that]
> - Portions: [solid / slightly high on carbs at dinner — easy adjustment]
> - Food quality: [great choices / swap X for Y next time]
>
> Overall: you're [X]% of the way to managing this on your own. That's the goal — I don't want you dependent on a plan forever. I want you to understand nutrition well enough to make smart choices at any restaurant, any grocery store, any family dinner."

**The Renewal Conversation:**

Do not wait until the plan expires. Have this conversation in Week 9 or 10.

> "[Name], your current plan runs through [date]. I want to talk about what comes next. Based on your progress, I see two strong options:
>
> 1. **Next-level plan:** We build Phase 2 of your nutrition — more variety, tighter macros for your updated body composition, and some performance-focused meals around your training schedule
> 2. **Maintenance mode:** A simplified plan that keeps you on track with less structure — great if the habits are locked in and you want more flexibility
>
> Which sounds right for where you are?"

**Red Flags:**

- They say "I think I've got it from here" (they may genuinely be ready, or they may be looking for a polite exit)
- They hesitate on renewal and mention budget
- They have not responded to the renewal conversation within 48 hours

**Intervention for "I've got it from here":**

> "That's actually the goal — for you to feel confident managing your nutrition independently. And you've earned that. Here's what I'd suggest: let's do a 30-day check-in plan instead of a full rebuild. I'll review your weekly nutrition log, make adjustments as needed, and you handle the day-to-day. Lower commitment, but you still have me in your corner if things drift. Interested?"

### Weeks 11-12

**The Referral Ask:**

This is your highest-conversion moment. The client has results. They are confident. They are grateful. Ask now.

> "I'm really proud of what you've accomplished. You went from [starting situation] to [current results] in 12 weeks. That's not luck — that's discipline and consistency on your part.
>
> I have a couple of openings for new clients next month. Do you know anyone — a friend, training partner, someone at work — who has been struggling with their nutrition? I'd love to help someone you trust. And honestly, having an accountability buddy following a plan at the same time makes it easier for both of you."

_Touchpoint:_ The 90-day summary. This is your most important message of the entire protocol.

> "90 days. Here's everything you've accomplished:
>
> **Physical Changes:**
>
> - Starting weight: [X] to Current weight: [Y] ([Z] lbs)
> - Body measurements: [specific changes if tracked]
> - Progress photos: [brief note on visible changes]
>
> **Nutrition Habits Built:**
>
> - Average weekly adherence: [X]%
> - Protein consistency: [X]% of target (up from [Y]% in Week 1)
> - Meals prepped per week: [X] average
> - New recipes mastered: [X]
>
> **Non-Scale Wins:**
>
> - [Energy, sleep, mood, digestion, clothing fit — reference what they told you]
>
> **What changed:** You didn't just follow a meal plan for 90 days. You rebuilt your relationship with food. You learned to prep, to track, to make smart choices without a plan in front of you. That's a skill you have for life.
>
> Ready for the next chapter?"

**Red Flags:**

- No response to the 90-day summary within 48 hours
- They renewed but engagement drops sharply in the first week of the new cycle

**Intervention for post-summary silence:**

Pick up the phone. Do not send another text.

> "Hey [Name], I sent over your 90-day summary — did you get a chance to see it? I'd love to spend five minutes mapping out what comes next. When works for a quick call?"

## Adherence Scoring System

Use this 1-5 scale during weekly check-ins. Ask the client to self-rate, and track it over time in your notes.

| Score | Meaning                             | Your Response                                                                                 |
| ----- | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **5** | Followed the plan 90-100% this week | "Crushing it. Keep going."                                                                    |
| **4** | Followed 70-89% — minor deviations  | "Strong week. Those small deviations are normal and fine."                                    |
| **3** | Followed 50-69% — noticeable gaps   | "Decent foundation. Let's identify the gap — is it one specific meal or a time-of-day thing?" |
| **2** | Followed 25-49% — significant drift | "Thanks for being honest. Let's simplify. What CAN you commit to this week?"                  |
| **1** | Followed less than 25%              | "No judgment. Something's off and I want to fix it. Can we talk for 5 minutes this week?"     |

**Tracking pattern:** If the score trends downward for two consecutive weeks, intervene immediately. Do not wait for a third week.

**Score 3 or below for three consecutive weeks:** Schedule a plan rebuild conversation. The current plan is not working for their life, and no amount of motivation will fix a structural problem.

### Handoff Protocol Between Coaches

When a client needs to transfer between coaches — due to coach departure, schedule changes, or location transfers — follow this protocol:

1. **Outgoing coach** writes a client brief: current phase, adherence score trend, key preferences, any red flags, and the client's personality type (e.g., "Needs frequent encouragement" or "Prefers minimal contact — just deliver the plan")
2. **Incoming coach** reads the brief and reviews the client's EvoFit Meals profile, including plan history and dietary restrictions
3. **Program Director** sends the client a transition message:

> "Hi [Name], I want to let you know that [New Coach] will be taking over your nutrition coaching starting [date]. They've already reviewed your plan history, your preferences, and your progress so far — so you won't need to re-explain anything. [New Coach] will reach out within 24 hours to introduce themselves and confirm your plan for the coming week. If you have any concerns about the transition, reach out to me directly."

4. **Incoming coach** sends an introduction within 24 hours:

> "Hi [Name], I'm [New Coach] and I'll be working with you on your nutrition going forward. I've reviewed everything — your progress has been impressive, especially [specific detail from brief]. I'm keeping your current plan intact for this week, and I'll reach out Wednesday for our first check-in. Looking forward to working with you."

5. **Program Director** monitors the client's adherence score for 2 weeks post-handoff. Any decline triggers a check-in with both the client and the incoming coach.

### Phase Handoff SOPs

For team-based operations where different coaches might handle different phases (e.g., an intake specialist handles Phase 1, a senior coach takes over Phase 3):

**Phase 1 to Phase 2 Handoff:**

- Outgoing person completes the Week 2 Checkpoint
- Writes handoff note: "Client is solid on breakfast, expanding to lunch. Family resistance flagged — see recipe swap notes. Adherence score: 4."
- Incoming person reviews and sends the Week 3 proactive swap message within 48 hours

**Phase 2 to Phase 3 Handoff:**

- Outgoing person completes the Week 4 Checkpoint including the one-month summary
- Writes handoff note: "Client's adherence trend is 4-4-3-4. Boredom risk managed with swaps. Ready for measurement checkpoint."
- Incoming person reviews and requests progress data within the first 3 days of Phase 3

**Phase 3 to Phase 4 Handoff:**

- Outgoing person completes the Week 8 Checkpoint
- Writes handoff note: "Client has 6-week photo comparison showing visible progress. Macro adjustment made at Week 6 — dropped rest-day carbs by 25g. Testimonial collected. Ready for independence testing and renewal conversation."
- Incoming person reviews and initiates the independence test within the first week

## Client Segmentation by Adherence Risk

**Segment every client into one of four risk categories at their monthly review.** This allows you to allocate coaching attention where it has the highest impact — instead of spending equal time on every client, spend more time on clients at risk of dropping off.

### The Four Segments

**Segment A: Thrivers (Adherence score 4-5 consistently)**

- Profile: Following the plan, seeing results, engaged in check-ins
- Touchpoint frequency: Standard protocol (no increase needed)
- Key action: Ask for referrals and testimonials. These clients sell your service for you.
- Risk of churn: Low (under 10% per quarter)
- Automated intervention: None needed

**Segment B: Steady Eddies (Adherence score 3-4, stable)**

- Profile: Following the plan most of the time, minor deviations, generally satisfied
- Touchpoint frequency: Standard protocol + one extra proactive message per month
- Key action: Pre-empt boredom with recipe variety. Ask about upcoming schedule changes.
- Risk of churn: Moderate (15-25% per quarter)
- Automated intervention: Week 3 recipe swap, Week 6 progress comparison

**Segment C: Drifters (Adherence score 2-3, declining trend)**

- Profile: Started strong but engagement is dropping. Responses are shorter. Plan viewing is less frequent.
- Touchpoint frequency: Increased — add one additional mid-week check-in
- Key action: Schedule a 10-minute call within 48 hours. Identify the barrier and rebuild the plan around it.
- Risk of churn: High (40-60% per quarter)
- Automated intervention: Simplified plan rebuild, reduced meal count, shorter-prep recipes

**Segment D: At-Risk (Adherence score 1-2, or no response for 10+ days)**

- Profile: Effectively off-plan. May not be viewing the dashboard. Minimal or no communication.
- Touchpoint frequency: Escalated — Lead Coach or Program Director outreach
- Key action: Phone call (not text). Offer pause, plan rebuild, or service adjustment. This is a save-or-lose moment.
- Risk of churn: Very high (70-90% per quarter)
- Automated intervention: Pause plan updates to avoid sending content they are not using. Trigger 60-day re-engagement sequence if they cancel.

Set these triggers in your team operations. When a condition is met, the corresponding action fires without waiting for the next scheduled check-in.

## Churn Prediction Indicators

**These are the early warning signals that a client is likely to cancel within the next 30 days.** Train your coaching team to recognize these patterns and intervene immediately — do not wait for the client to initiate the cancellation conversation.

### High-Confidence Churn Signals (75%+ likelihood of cancellation)

| Signal                                             | What It Looks Like                                        | Intervention Window        |
| -------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Silent withdrawal                                  | No messages in 14+ days, no plan views in 10+ days        | 24-48 hours from detection |
| Explicit plateau frustration + declining adherence | "Nothing is working" combined with scores dropping        | Same day                   |
| Budget mention                                     | Any reference to cost being a concern                     | Same day                   |
| Life event disclosure                              | Divorce, job loss, illness, new baby — without adaptation | Within 48 hours            |
| "Taking a break" request                           | Client asks to pause without a return date                | Same conversation          |
| Negative comparison                                | "My friend uses [competitor/app] and pays less"           | Same conversation          |
| Check-in no-shows                                  | Misses 2 consecutive scheduled check-ins                  | After the second miss      |

### Moderate-Confidence Churn Signals (40-60% likelihood)

| Signal                        | What It Looks Like                                        | Intervention Window |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Response time increase        | Replies that used to come in hours now take days          | Within the week     |
| Shorter messages              | Went from detailed check-in responses to "fine" or "good" | Next check-in       |
| Plan viewing decline          | Went from daily views to 1-2 per week                     | Within the week     |
| Stopped sending progress data | Skips photo submissions or weigh-ins                      | Next check-in       |
| Social media activity change  | Posts about trying a new diet or detox                    | Within 48 hours     |
| Referral decline              | Was previously willing to refer, now says "not right now" | Note and monitor    |

### Low-Confidence Signals (Track but Don't Panic)

| Signal            | What It Looks Like                            | Action                                                  |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Seasonal patterns | Engagement dips in December, summer vacations | Pre-emptive simplification                              |
| Schedule changes  | New job, new routine, kids back in school     | Proactive plan rebuild                                  |
| Platform fatigue  | "I've been looking at the app less"           | Fresh UI experience — new recipe categories, challenges |

| 6    | Results      | Results delivered + macro adjustments                      | Coach | 15 min |

**At scale:** A coach managing 20 clients spends approximately 50 hours per quarter on adherence touchpoints. That is roughly 4 hours per week — easily manageable within a full-time coaching role. The Program Director spends 2-3 hours per week on dashboard reviews and escalations.
