# Nutrition Sales & Objection Scripts

## 25 Conversations That Close Nutrition Coaching Clients at Every Level — From Individual Prospects to Corporate Contracts

---

### Why Scripts Matter for Nutrition Sales

Selling nutrition coaching is harder than selling training sessions. With training, the client can see the gym, feel the workout, and picture themselves getting stronger. Nutrition is invisible. The results are slower. The resistance is deeper — because food is emotional, personal, and tangled up with identity, family, and years of failed diets.

At the enterprise level, the challenge compounds. You are not just selling to individuals anymore. You are selling to gym owners, studio directors, corporate wellness managers, school boards, insurance companies, and franchisees. Each audience has different objections, different decision-making processes, and different definitions of value. A script that closes a one-on-one training client will fall flat with a corporate wellness director evaluating your nutrition program against three competing proposals.

That is exactly why you need frameworks for these conversations. Not word-for-word scripts you recite like a robot, but proven structures that help you navigate the most common pushback without losing your footing. Every script in this guide follows the same pattern:

1. **Acknowledge** — Show them you genuinely heard the concern. Never dismiss it.
2. **Reframe** — Shift the conversation from their past experience to your unique approach.
3. **Bridge** — Connect back to the specific health, body composition, or business goal they told you about.
4. **Close or Advance** — Ask for the commitment, or set a concrete next step.

One more thing: this is not about pressure selling. Nutrition coaching only works when the client trusts you and opts in willingly. Your job in these conversations is to remove confusion, address real concerns, and help them make a decision that serves their goals. If the answer is genuinely no, let it be no — and leave the door open. The best nutrition clients often come back two months later when the thing they tried instead didn't work.

**This enterprise edition includes all 25 scripts organized into three sections:** individual prospect objections (Scripts 1-8), professional client scenarios (Scripts 9-15), and enterprise and institutional conversations (Scripts 16-25). If you are a gym owner managing a team of nutrition coaches, these scripts should be distributed to your entire coaching staff. Every coach on your team should know Scripts 1-15 cold. Scripts 16-25 are for your sales leadership — the people pitching partnerships, corporate contracts, and institutional programs.

## Script 1: "I Can Meal Prep Myself"

**Why they say this:** They think nutrition coaching is just someone handing them recipes. They have watched meal prep videos on YouTube, bought containers from Amazon, and maybe even had a good run for two or three weeks. They genuinely believe the only thing standing between them and results is a collection of recipes — and they can find those for free. What they don't see is the difference between having recipes and having a system that adapts to their life week after week.

**The Reframe:** Recipes are ingredients — a nutrition system is the architecture that makes those ingredients work for your body, your schedule, and your goals consistently.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I appreciate the offer, but honestly I can just meal prep on my own. There are tons of recipes online."
>
> **You:** "You're absolutely right — there are thousands of great recipes out there. And I'd bet you've probably tried meal prepping before, yeah?"
>
> **Client:** "Yeah, I've done it a few times. It went well for a couple weeks."
>
> **You:** "That's really common. Here's what I've noticed with almost every client I work with: the recipes were never the problem. The problem is what happens in week three when you're bored of chicken and rice, your schedule shifts, you eat out twice unexpectedly, and suddenly the whole plan falls apart. Does that sound familiar?"
>
> **Client:** "Yeah, actually. That's pretty much what happens."
>
> **You:** "That's exactly what I solve. I don't just hand you a list of meals. I build a system around your life — your schedule, your preferences, your training days versus rest days, even what your family eats. And when things go sideways — because they always do — I adjust the plan in real time instead of you having to start over from scratch. The platform has over 6,000 recipes with filters for every dietary need, so you'll never get bored and you'll never have to Google 'high protein dinner ideas' at 5 PM again. This isn't meal prep. It's meal architecture."
>
> **You:** "Want to see what a week built around your actual schedule looks like?"

**If They Still Say No:** "Totally fair. If you ever hit that wall in week three and want someone to build the system around you, I'm here. I'll check in with you in a few weeks."

## Script 2: "Diets Don't Work for Me"

**Why they say this:** This one carries emotional weight. They have tried keto, paleo, Whole30, calorie counting, intermittent fasting — maybe all of them. Each time they lost weight, regained it, and ended up feeling worse than before. They are not just skeptical of your program. They are skeptical of themselves. The repeated failure has convinced them that their body is somehow different, that they are the exception. What they need to hear is that the diets failed them, not the other way around.

**The Reframe:** Diets are temporary restrictions with expiration dates — nutrition coaching is a permanent skill set that adapts with you.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "Look, I've tried every diet out there. Nothing sticks. I don't think diets work for me."
>
> **You:** "Can I be honest? I actually agree with you. Diets don't work — not for you, not for most people. And that's not because something is wrong with you. It's because diets are designed to be temporary. They give you rigid rules, you white-knuckle through them for a few weeks, and the second real life shows up, the whole thing collapses."
>
> **Client:** "That's exactly what happens every time."
>
> **You:** "Right. So here's what's different about what I do: I'm not putting you on a diet. There's no food you can't eat. No phase one, phase two nonsense. What I build is a flexible nutrition framework around your actual life — your training schedule, your preferences, the foods you love, even the meals you eat out. We adjust it as we go based on how your body responds and what your week looks like."
>
> **You:** "Every plan is customized to your macros and your goals, and the system has dietary filters for literally any restriction or preference. So if you hate broccoli, broccoli is gone. If you love pasta, we work pasta in. The reason those other diets failed is because they asked you to live someone else's life. I'm building nutrition around yours."
>
> **You:** "Would it help if I showed you what a day of eating looks like when it's built around foods you actually enjoy?"

**If They Still Say No:** "I completely understand the hesitation. You've been burned before. If you ever want to try an approach that doesn't feel like a diet, reach out. I'll keep a spot open."

## Script 3: "I Already Know What to Eat"

**Why they say this:** They probably do know, at least in broad strokes. Lean protein, vegetables, whole grains, less sugar — the basics are not a mystery. But there is a canyon between knowing what to eat and consistently eating it in the right amounts, at the right times, adjusted for training days, recovery, and real-life chaos. This objection is really about pride — they don't want to admit that knowledge alone hasn't been enough.

**The Reframe:** Everyone knows what to eat. The gap between knowing and doing is where nutrition coaching lives.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I appreciate it, but I already know what I should be eating. I just need to be more disciplined."
>
> **You:** "I believe you. Honestly, most of my clients knew exactly what to eat before they started working with me. That wasn't what was missing."
>
> **Client:** "So what was missing?"
>
> **You:** "Execution. Structure. Accountability. Knowing that chicken and vegetables is a good dinner doesn't help you at 6 PM on a Wednesday when you're exhausted, the kids are hungry, and the easiest option is ordering takeout. What helps is having a plan already built for that exact scenario — a 20-minute meal that fits your macros, uses ingredients you bought on Sunday, and feeds the whole household."
>
> **You:** "That's what I build. Not education — implementation. Your plan is customized, it adjusts to your training schedule, and when life throws a curveball, we pivot the plan instead of abandoning it. Plus, I'm tracking your progress and adjusting your nutrition targets as your body changes. That's the piece that discipline alone can't replace."
>
> **You:** "Think of it this way: you know how to exercise too, but having a structured training program still makes a difference, right? Nutrition is the same. Knowing isn't the bottleneck — having a system is. Want me to build that system for you?"

**If They Still Say No:** "That's fair. If you find that discipline alone isn't getting you where you want to be, the door is always open. I'll follow up in a month to see how things are going."

## Script 4: "It's Too Expensive on Top of Training"

**Why they say this:** This is a real concern for most clients. They are already paying for training sessions, and now you are suggesting they add nutrition coaching on top. It feels like the bill is stacking up. What they need to understand is that nutrition isn't an add-on — it's the multiplier that makes their training investment actually pay off. Without it, they are leaving 70% of their results on the table.

**The Reframe:** Nutrition coaching doesn't add to your training cost — it multiplies the return on the investment you're already making.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I'm already paying for training. Adding nutrition on top feels like a lot."
>
> **You:** "I totally understand that. You're already making a real investment in your health, and I respect that. Can I share something that might reframe this?"
>
> **Client:** "Sure."
>
> **You:** "You've probably heard the saying that results are 70% nutrition and 30% training. I don't know if those exact numbers are right, but directionally it's true — you cannot out-train a poor diet. Right now, you're investing in the 30%. The training is working. Your form is improving, you're getting stronger. But if the nutrition isn't dialled in, you're paying full price for 30% of the results."
>
> **You:** "Adding nutrition coaching doesn't double your cost — it completes the equation. It's the difference between working hard in the gym and actually seeing the body composition changes you're training for. Most of my clients who add nutrition see more visible progress in the first month than they saw in the previous three months of training alone."
>
> **You:** "And here's the thing — the meal plans are fully built for you through the platform. Customized to your macros, your dietary preferences, your schedule. You're not paying for generic PDFs. You're getting a system that runs alongside your training program and makes every session count. Want me to show you the cost per day? It's usually less than the coffee you're buying on the way to the gym."

**If They Still Say No:** "No pressure at all. Keep training, and when you feel ready to unlock the other 70%, I'm here. I'll mention it again when the time feels right."

## Script 5: "My Partner/Family Won't Eat That"

**Why they say this:** This is a logistics concern disguised as an objection. They are imagining having to cook two separate meals every night — one "diet meal" for themselves and one "normal meal" for the family. They have probably tried this before with a restrictive diet and it was unsustainable. What they need to know is that a well-designed nutrition plan doesn't require a separate kitchen. It fits into the household.

**The Reframe:** A good nutrition plan doesn't isolate you at dinner — it feeds the whole table while still hitting your targets.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "The problem is my family. My husband won't eat 'diet food' and my kids are picky. I'd have to cook two meals every night."
>
> **You:** "That's one of the most common concerns I hear, and it's completely valid. Cooking two separate meals every night is a non-starter — nobody can sustain that."
>
> **Client:** "Exactly. That's why every diet I've tried falls apart."
>
> **You:** "Here's the thing — I don't build meal plans that require you to eat in isolation. The platform has over 6,000 recipes, and a huge portion of them are family-friendly meals that your husband and kids will actually eat. We're talking things like taco bowls, stir-fries, pasta dishes, sheet-pan dinners — real food that happens to be portioned and balanced for your goals."
>
> **You:** "The difference is in the portions and maybe a side swap. Your family gets the full meal. You get the same meal, portioned to your macros, maybe with extra vegetables instead of a second helping of rice. Same dinner table. Same food. No separate cooking required."
>
> **You:** "I also build plans that account for the nights your family does takeout or eats something that doesn't fit your targets. We plan around those nights instead of pretending they won't happen. Want me to pull up some of the family-friendly recipes so you can see what I mean?"

**If They Still Say No:** "I get it — the family dynamic is real. If you want to try a one-week sample plan to see how it fits with your household, I'm happy to put that together. No commitment."

## Script 6: "I Travel Too Much for a Meal Plan"

**Why they say this:** They are picturing a rigid meal plan that requires them to be in their kitchen with specific ingredients at specific times. Since they are on the road, in hotels, or eating out frequently, they have written off structured nutrition as incompatible with their lifestyle. What they don't realize is that a good nutrition system is designed for exactly this kind of unpredictability.

**The Reframe:** A nutrition plan that only works in your kitchen isn't a plan — it's a fantasy. Real plans travel with you.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I'm on the road three or four days a week. A meal plan just isn't realistic for my lifestyle."
>
> **You:** "How many days a week are you actually at home with access to your own kitchen?"
>
> **Client:** "Maybe three or four, depending on the week."
>
> **You:** "Perfect. So here's what I'd build: for the days you're home, you get structured meal plans — prepped, portioned, optimized for your goals. For the days you're traveling, I build a flexible framework: restaurant guidelines, fast-casual go-to orders, hotel room meal options, and macro targets you can hit with what's available wherever you are."
>
> **You:** "The platform lets me set up different day templates — home days and travel days — so your plan adapts to your actual week instead of pretending every day looks the same. I also include travel-friendly meal options that require zero cooking. Some of my busiest clients are travelers, and they see the best results because we've built a system that accounts for the chaos instead of ignoring it."
>
> **You:** "The biggest mistake people make with nutrition is assuming it only works under perfect conditions. I build plans for real conditions — your conditions. Want me to map out what a typical travel week would look like?"

**If They Still Say No:** "Totally understand. If you want to test-drive the travel framework for a week on your next trip, let me know. It might surprise you how manageable it is."

## Script 7: "I'll Start Monday" / "After the Holidays"

**Why they say this:** This is a delay tactic, and it usually comes from one of two places: fear of commitment or the belief that they need a perfect starting condition. They are waiting for a Monday, a new month, the end of a vacation, post-holidays, after the birthday party — there is always something. The reality is that there will never be a clean starting line. The perfect time doesn't exist, and every week they wait is a week of results they won't get back.

**The Reframe:** There's no perfect Monday. The best nutrition plan starts in the middle of the mess — because that's where real life happens.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I love this, but the timing isn't great. Let me start after the holidays. I'll reach out in January."
>
> **You:** "I hear you. And honestly, that instinct makes total sense — start fresh, clean slate, new year energy. Can I push back just a little bit?"
>
> **Client:** "Sure."
>
> **You:** "The holidays are actually the perfect time to start. Not because I want you counting macros at Christmas dinner — that's not what this is. But because the clients who learn to navigate holidays, travel, parties, and social eating with a flexible plan are the ones who never fall off. If your nutrition system only works when life is calm and predictable, it's not a system. It's a fair-weather plan."
>
> **You:** "Here's what I'd do: we start now with a flexible framework. Through the holidays, the goal isn't perfection — it's building the skill of making better choices without restricting yourself. By January, while everyone else is starting from scratch, you'll already have momentum, habits, and a system that works."
>
> **You:** "Every week you wait is a week you could have been making progress. And I promise, there will never be a week with zero obstacles. So we might as well start learning to navigate them now. What if we kicked off this week with a simple baseline plan — nothing extreme?"

**If They Still Say No:** "No problem at all. I'm going to check in with you on January 2nd. And when I do, let's not wait for another Monday. Deal?"

## Script 8: "I Have Too Many Restrictions/Allergies"

**Why they say this:** They have had bad experiences with generic meal plans that ignore their dietary needs. Maybe they are celiac, lactose intolerant, vegetarian, or managing multiple food sensitivities. They have received plans before that were useless on day one because half the meals contained foods they can't eat. They assume your system will be the same — one-size-fits-all with no room for their complexity.

**The Reframe:** Your restrictions aren't a problem to work around — they're the blueprint I use to build your plan.

**The Script:**

> **Client:** "I don't think this would work for me. I'm gluten-free, dairy-free, and I can't have soy. Every meal plan I've tried ignores at least one of those."
>
> **You:** "That's incredibly frustrating. Let me guess — you've gotten plans where you had to substitute half the meals yourself, which basically meant the plan was useless?"
>
> **Client:** "Yes. Every single time."
>
> **You:** "That won't happen here. The system I use has built-in dietary filters for every restriction you just named — and dozens more. When I build your plan, gluten, dairy, and soy are excluded before I even start selecting recipes. Every single meal in your plan will be safe for you from day one. No substitutions required."
>
> **You:** "The platform has over 6,000 recipes across every dietary category you can think of: gluten-free, dairy-free, soy-free, nut-free, vegan, keto, paleo — and combinations of all of them. Your restrictions actually make my job more focused, not harder. I'm not trying to fit you into a template. I'm building a template around you."
>
> **You:** "On top of that, if you have a reaction to something or discover a new sensitivity, I update the plan immediately. No waiting for next month's meal plan. We adjust it that day. Want me to show you how many recipes are available with your specific combination of filters? The number might surprise you."

**If They Still Say No:** "Completely understand. If you ever get tired of piecing together your own meals around your restrictions, this is literally what the system was built for. I'll be here."

## Script 9: Upselling Training Clients to Nutrition Add-Ons

**Context:** You have a training client who has been with you for 3+ months. They are getting results from training but their nutrition is clearly the limiting factor. They have never asked about nutrition services, but you can see the gap.

**The Script:**

> **You:** "Hey [Name], I want to bring something up that I've been thinking about. Your training consistency is excellent — you haven't missed a session in six weeks. But I'm noticing that your body composition changes aren't matching your effort level. I think I know why."
>
> **Client:** "Yeah, I've been wondering about that too."
>
> **You:** "It's the nutrition side. You're sending your body the right signals through training — 'build muscle, burn fat, get stronger.' But your body needs specific raw materials to respond to those signals. Without the right nutrition framework, your body is hearing the signal but doesn't have what it needs to follow through."
>
> **You:** "I've started offering integrated nutrition coaching for my training clients. I build a meal plan that syncs with your training program — higher carb on heavy training days, adjusted macros during recovery weeks, pre-workout and post-workout meal timing that maximizes what we're doing in the gym. It's not a separate service — it's the other half of what we're already doing."
>
> **You:** "The investment is $[X]/month on top of your training. That includes a fully customized weekly plan through EvoFit Meals, adjustments based on your progress, and a monthly nutrition check-in. Based on where you're at and what we've built in training, I think you'd see more visible change in the next 30 days than the last 90. Want to try it for a month?"

**If they hesitate:** "How about this — let me build you a free 3-day sample plan based on your current training program. You try it, see how it feels, and if you like it, we'll set up the full service."

**The Script:**

> **You:** "Based on what you've told me about your goals and your lifestyle, I'd recommend my Premium Nutrition Coaching. Let me walk you through exactly what that looks like."
>
> **You:** "Every week, you'll receive a fully customized meal plan built around your macros, your food preferences, your training schedule, and your real-life commitments. We're talking restaurant-quality recipes — not bodybuilder food. Each plan comes with a complete grocery list, prep instructions, and detailed nutritional breakdowns."
>
> **You:** "But the plan is just the foundation. What makes this premium is the coaching layer. We check in weekly — not monthly. I review your adherence, your progress photos, your energy levels, and your feedback. If something isn't working by Tuesday, we fix it by Wednesday. You also get direct messaging access to me — when you're standing in a restaurant looking at a menu, you can text me a photo and I'll tell you what to order."
>
> **You:** "The investment is $[X]/month. That's higher than a basic meal plan service, and here's why: basic services hand you a PDF and disappear. I'm building a living, breathing nutrition system that evolves with you every week. My average premium client stays for 9 months and achieves results they genuinely didn't believe were possible in the first consultation. The ROI isn't just physical — it's the time you save, the mental energy you reclaim, and the confidence that comes from knowing your nutrition is handled."
>
> **You:** "Are you ready to get started?"

**If they ask about cheaper options:** "I do offer a standard monthly plan at $[X]. It includes bi-weekly plans and monthly check-ins. It's a great service. But based on the speed and specificity of results you described wanting, the premium coaching is what's going to get you there on the timeline you want."

**The Script:**

> **You:** "I'm launching a [4/8]-week Nutrition Challenge starting [date], and I think you'd be a perfect fit. Here's the deal."

The challenge creates accountability — you're not doing this alone. There are [X] people already signed up, and the energy in a group setting is completely different from going solo."

I do a live coaching call every week where we troubleshoot, swap recipes, and celebrate wins. At the end, we measure results and the top [3] transformations get [prize/incentive]."

Most participants go on to become monthly nutrition clients because they experience firsthand what structured nutrition coaching actually delivers."

> **You:** "We have [X] spots left. Want me to hold one for you?"

## Script 12: Converting Free Consultation to Paid Client

**Context:** You offered a free 15-minute nutrition consultation as a lead magnet. The prospect attended and is interested but hasn't committed. You need to bridge from free value to paid enrollment.

**The Script:**

> **You:** "Now that I've seen your current eating patterns and heard your goals, I want to tell you exactly what I'd build for you if we worked together."
>
> **You:** "Your protein is significantly below what you need for your training volume — you're at roughly [X]g and you need closer to [Y]g. Your carb timing is working against your energy levels because most of your carbs are at dinner when you need them earlier in the day around your workouts. And there are about [Z] meals per week where you're defaulting to convenience food because there's no plan in place."
>
> **You:** "None of this is a criticism — it's data. And here's what the data tells me: with the right meal plan and a few structural changes, you're going to feel different within a week and look different within four. That's not a promise — it's what I see consistently when clients go from unstructured eating to a system that matches their goals."
>
> **You:** "My monthly nutrition coaching is $[X]. You'll get your first customized plan within 48 hours of signing up, and we'll review it together before you start. Ready?"

> **You:** "When do you think you'll have a chance to talk it over? I'd love to follow up [specific day] so we don't lose momentum."

## Script 14: Renewing an Expiring Client

**Context:** A client's commitment period is ending. You need to have the renewal conversation before they decide to leave.

**The Script:**

> **You:** "Before we wrap up today, I want to talk about what's next. Your current plan runs through [date], and I've been reviewing your progress to figure out the best path forward."
>
> **You:** "When you started, you were [starting point — specific data]. Today, you're [current point — specific data]. That's [specific improvement] in [timeframe]. Here's what I think the next phase looks like."
>
> **You:** "Option one: we continue with the current approach and start Phase 2 — tighter macro targets based on your new body composition, more variety in recipes, and we introduce some performance-oriented meal timing around your training. This is the aggressive path if you want to keep pushing toward [their stated long-term goal]."
>
> **You:** "Option two: we move to a maintenance plan. Lighter touch — I build monthly plans instead of weekly, and we check in every 6 weeks instead of monthly. This is for clients who've built strong habits and want a safety net without the full coaching intensity. Lower investment, too — $[X]/month instead of $[Y]."
>
> **You:** "Which one feels right for where you are?"

**If they want to cancel:** "I respect that. Before you go, let me build you a 30-day transition plan — a self-sustaining plan you can follow independently. And just know: if you want to come back in 3 months or 6 months, your profile, your preferences, and all your progress data are saved. No re-onboarding. We'd pick up right where you left off."

> **You:** "Having an accountability partner going through the same process actually makes it easier for both of you. And honestly, your recommendation carries more weight than any post I could make on social media."

> **Client:** "Actually, my friend [Name] has been asking me what I've been doing differently."
>
> **You:** "Perfect. Would you be comfortable making an introduction? I can send you a message you can forward to them — something simple that explains what I do and offers a free consultation. And if they sign up, I'll add an extra week to your plan on me as a thank you."

## Script 16: Gym/Studio Owner Pitch for Nutrition Program

**Context:** You are approaching a gym or studio owner about implementing a nutrition coaching program within their facility. This is a B2B conversation — you're not selling a meal plan, you're selling a revenue stream.

**Why this conversation matters at scale:** A single gym partnership can generate 20-50 nutrition clients in a quarter. The gym owner cares about member retention, additional revenue per member, and differentiation from competing facilities. Position your nutrition program as a solution to all three.

**The Script:**

> **You:** "Thanks for taking the time. I want to show you something that could add a meaningful revenue stream to your facility without adding headcount or overhead."
>
> **Owner:** "I'm listening."
>
> **You:** "Here's the problem I see in most gyms: members come in, they train hard, they don't see results fast enough, and they cancel. The average gym loses 30-40% of its members every year. The number one reason people cancel isn't that they don't like the gym — it's that they're not seeing results. And the number one reason they're not seeing results is nutrition."
>
> **You:** "What I'm proposing is a white-label nutrition coaching program embedded in your facility. Here's how it works: your trainers offer customized meal plans to their clients through a professional platform called EvoFit Meals. The plans are personalized to each client's goals, preferences, and dietary restrictions. The platform handles the heavy lifting — recipe selection, macro calculations, grocery lists — so your trainers can deliver a premium service without needing a nutrition degree."

### A Note on Trust Over Pressure

The best nutrition clients are the ones who choose to work with you — not the ones you talked into it. These scripts are designed to remove confusion and address real concerns, not to manipulate someone into a purchase they'll regret.

If a client says no after you've genuinely addressed their objection, respect it. Send them a follow-up in two to four weeks. Stay visible. Share nutrition tips. Be the person they think of when they are finally ready. The long game in nutrition coaching is trust, and trust compounds. A client who comes back to you on their own terms will stay longer, comply better, and refer more people than someone you pressured into signing up.

At the enterprise level, this principle matters even more. A corporate wellness manager who feels pressured will never sign a second contract. A franchise group that feels oversold will pull the program after one quarter. Build relationships first. Demonstrate value second. Close with confidence third. And always leave the door open.

Sell with clarity. Close with confidence. But always leave the door open.
