# Nutrition Client Acquisition Playbook

## How to Upsell Training Clients, Attract Nutrition-Only Clients, Land Corporate Contracts, and Scale Acquisition Across Multiple Locations

---

### Who This Is For

You run a gym, studio, or multi-location fitness business. You have a team of nutrition coaches — or you are building one. Your goal is not to acquire your first 10 nutrition clients. It is to build a systematic acquisition engine that feeds your nutrition program with qualified clients at every level: individual upsells, nutrition-only leads, corporate wellness contracts, and B2B partnerships.

This playbook gives you the complete system — from the individual training-to-nutrition upsell all the way through corporate contract negotiations and multi-location acquisition strategies. It covers the revenue math, positioning, sales conversations, a 30-day action plan for your team, and advanced acquisition channels that only work at enterprise scale.

If you are a solo trainer, start with Section 3. If you are managing a team, read the whole thing and assign sections to the right people.

## Section 1: Why Add Nutrition Services

### The Revenue Math That Changes Everything

Most trainers earn exclusively from session fees. The problem: session fees are capped by your available hours. You can only train so many people per day. Nutrition services break that ceiling because meal plan delivery is not time-bound the same way a training session is.

Let's run the numbers.

**Training-only model:**

- 20 clients at 2 sessions/week = 40 sessions/week
- Average session rate: $80
- Monthly revenue: 40 sessions x 4.3 weeks x $80 = **$13,760/month**
- Time required: 40 hours of sessions + 10 hours of programming and admin = 50 hours/week

**Training + nutrition model:**

- 20 clients at 2 sessions/week + meal plans for 15 of them = same 40 sessions + 15 meal plans
- Average session rate: $80
- Meal plan add-on: $75/month per client
- Monthly revenue: $13,760 (training) + $1,125 (nutrition) = **$14,885/month**
- Additional time: 3-5 hours/week using EvoFit Meals to generate and customize plans

That's $1,125/month in additional revenue for roughly 4 extra hours of work per week. That's $281/hour for the nutrition work — significantly higher than your per-session training rate.

Now extend the model. Add 10 nutrition-only clients who don't train with you:

**Nutrition-only clients:**

- 10 clients at $150/month (meal plan + monthly check-in) = **$1,500/month**
- Additional time: 5-7 hours/week

**Combined total:** $13,760 + $1,125 + $1,500 = **$16,385/month**

You have added $2,625/month with roughly 10 extra hours of work per week. And here is the kicker: those nutrition-only clients often convert to full training clients within 3-6 months once they see results and want to level up.

### The Enterprise Revenue Model

Now scale to a team. Five coaches, each managing 20 nutrition clients at $150/month average:

**Team-based nutrition program:**

- 5 coaches x 20 clients = 100 nutrition clients
- Average revenue per client: $150/month
- Total nutrition MRR: **$15,000/month** = **$180,000/year**
- Coach compensation (40% of revenue): $72,000/year
- Platform costs: ~$3,600/year
- Net nutrition revenue to the business: **~$104,400/year**

That is a six-figure revenue stream built entirely on top of your existing training business with no additional rent, no equipment, and no inventory.

### The Compound Effect

Nutrition services create three revenue layers:

1. **Upsell revenue** — existing training clients adding meal plans
2. **Standalone revenue** — nutrition-only clients
3. **Conversion revenue** — nutrition clients upgrading to training

Each layer feeds the others. Training clients who add nutrition get better results and stay longer (retention goes up 30-40% when nutrition is included). Nutrition-only clients see your training results and want in. And everyone refers more because their outcomes are dramatically better.

### Why Clients Want This From You

Here is what most trainers miss: your clients already trust you with their body. They do not want to find a separate nutritionist. They do not want to explain their training schedule to someone who does not understand progressive overload or periodization. They want one person who understands the full picture.

When you offer nutrition services, you are not adding complexity. You are removing a gap that was already frustrating your clients.

## Section 2: Positioning Your Nutrition Offering

### Choose Your Lane

Before you sell anything, decide what kind of nutrition service you are offering. There are four common models:

**Model 1: Meal Plan Delivery (Simplest)**
You create customized meal plans tailored to the client's goals, preferences, and restrictions. They receive a weekly or bi-weekly plan with recipes, macros, and a shopping list. No ongoing coaching — just the plan.

- Best for: Trainers who want to add revenue with minimal time investment
- Pricing range: $50-100/month
- Time per client: 30-45 minutes/month after initial setup
- EvoFit Meals makes this model extremely efficient — you can generate a complete plan in minutes and customize from there

**Model 2: Meal Plans + Monthly Check-In**
Same as Model 1, but you add a 30-minute monthly nutrition check-in (video call or in-person). You review adherence, adjust the plan based on progress, and troubleshoot barriers.

- Best for: Trainers who want deeper client relationships and higher pricing
- Pricing range: $100-200/month
- Time per client: 60-90 minutes/month
- Higher retention because clients feel supported

**Model 3: Full Nutrition Coaching**
Weekly or bi-weekly coaching touchpoints, meal plans, food diary review, habit tracking, and accountability messaging. This is a comprehensive nutrition coaching program.

- Best for: Trainers with nutrition certifications who want to build a coaching practice
- Pricing range: $200-400/month
- Time per client: 2-3 hours/month
- Requires stronger nutrition knowledge and communication skills

**Model 4: Training + Nutrition Bundle (Highest Value)**
Your training sessions include integrated nutrition guidance. Clients get both their workout program and their meal plan from you, with everything coordinated.

- Best for: Trainers who want to maximize per-client revenue
- Pricing range: Training rate + $75-150/month nutrition add-on
- This is the model most clients actually want — they just need you to offer it

**Recommendation for Enterprise tier:** Offer all four models simultaneously. Model 1 is your gateway. Model 2 is your bread and butter. Model 3 is for high-value clients. Model 4 is your default upsell for existing training clients. Having a tiered offering allows every coach on your team to place every prospect in the right product.

### Naming Your Nutrition Service

Don't call it "meal planning." That sounds like a commodity. Name it something that communicates value and outcome.

**Examples:**

- "Nutrition Blueprint" — your custom eating strategy
- "Fuel Plan" — optimized nutrition for your goals
- "Performance Nutrition" — for athletic or performance-focused clients
- "The Complete Package" — training + nutrition bundled

Pick a name. Use it consistently in your marketing, consultations, and client communications. For multi-location operations, the name must be the same at every facility.

### Scope of Practice — The Line You Must Respect

**This is critical.** As a personal trainer, you can provide general nutrition guidance: meal plans, macro recommendations, food suggestions, and healthy eating strategies. You cannot diagnose conditions, prescribe diets for medical conditions, or claim to treat diseases through nutrition.

**You CAN do:**

- Create meal plans based on client goals and preferences
- Recommend macro ratios for fat loss, muscle gain, or performance
- Suggest foods and recipes
- Educate clients on portion sizes and nutrient timing
- Help clients build sustainable eating habits

**You CANNOT do (unless separately credentialed):**

- Prescribe meal plans to treat diabetes, eating disorders, or other medical conditions
- Claim that specific foods cure or treat diseases
- Override advice from a client's doctor or registered dietitian
- Provide clinical nutrition therapy

**The safe phrasing:** "This meal plan is designed for general health and your fitness goals. If you have a medical condition that affects your diet, please consult with your doctor or a registered dietitian, and I'll coordinate with them to make sure your plan works with their recommendations."

Include a version of this disclaimer in your onboarding materials, your client agreement, and any published nutrition content. **For enterprise operations:** train every coach on this language during onboarding. Include it in your SOPs. Make it non-negotiable.

## Section 3: The Training-to-Nutrition Upsell

### Why Your Existing Clients Are Your Best First Customers

You already have trust. You already have context on their goals, their schedule, and their struggles. And you almost certainly have clients who are training hard but not eating to support their goals.

The upsell conversation is the fastest path to your first nutrition clients.

### The Natural Transition

Don't create a separate sales event. Integrate the nutrition conversation into your existing coaching.

**Trigger moments — when to bring it up:**

1. **When they plateau.** "You've been crushing the training side. I think the next unlock for you is dialling in your nutrition. Can I show you what I mean?"

2. **When they ask about food.** This happens constantly. Instead of giving a casual answer, upgrade the conversation: "Actually, I've started offering customized meal plans for my training clients. Want me to walk you through what that looks like?"

3. **When they hit a milestone.** "You've made incredible progress. If you want to accelerate from here, the biggest lever we haven't pulled is nutrition. I can build you a plan that matches exactly what we're doing in training."

4. **During a check-in or progress review.** "Looking at your numbers, your training consistency is excellent. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is almost entirely nutrition. Here's what I'd recommend."

5. **When they complain about food.** "I don't know what to eat." "I just grab whatever's fast." "I'm so inconsistent with meals." These are buying signals. Respond with a solution, not just advice.

### The Upsell Script

Here is a conversation you can have during or after a training session. Adapt it to your natural speaking style.

**The Setup (1 minute):**

> "Hey [Name], I want to talk to you about something I've been thinking about. You've been doing great in our sessions — your [specific metric] has improved significantly. But I think we're leaving results on the table because we haven't addressed the nutrition side. Can I share something with you?"

**The Problem (2 minutes):**

> "Right now, you're training [X] times a week and putting in serious effort. But your body can only adapt if it has the right fuel. Think of it this way: if training is the signal telling your body to change, nutrition is the raw material your body needs to actually make that change. Without the nutrition piece, you're sending the signal but not giving your body what it needs to respond."

**The Solution (2 minutes):**

> "What I've started doing for my training clients is building customized meal plans that are specifically designed to support their training goals. I use a platform called EvoFit Meals — it lets me create a plan that matches your macros, your food preferences, your schedule, and any dietary restrictions you have. You get a full meal plan with recipes, portions, and a shopping list. And I update it based on your progress."

**The Offer (1 minute):**

> "For training clients, I add this as a monthly add-on — it's $[X]/month on top of your training package. That includes your customized meal plan, adjustments as we go, and a quick nutrition check-in once a month. Given where you're at and what you want to achieve, I genuinely think this is the thing that's going to get you to [their specific goal]. Want to try it for a month?"

**If they say yes:** "Brilliant. I'll send you a dietary questionnaire today — takes about 10 minutes. Once I have that back, I'll build your first plan and we'll review it together at our next session."

**If they hesitate:** "No pressure at all. How about this — let me build you a sample 3-day plan based on what I already know about your goals. You try it for a week. If you like it, we'll do the full service. If not, no cost, no commitment. Fair?"

The free sample is your secret weapon. Once they experience a professionally built meal plan, most clients want the full service.

## Section 4: Attracting Nutrition-Only Clients

### A Different Audience, A Different Approach

Nutrition-only clients are people who want meal planning and nutrition guidance but do not necessarily want personal training. They might be:

- People who already exercise on their own (home workouts, running, CrossFit, yoga) but don't know how to eat for their goals
- People who can't afford personal training but can afford a meal plan service
- People in a different geographic location (online nutrition has no physical boundary)
- People who have been referred by a training client's friend
- Busy professionals who want someone else to figure out what to eat

This audience is larger than the personal training market, and they are underserved. Most nutrition coaching is either very clinical (registered dietitian) or very generic (app-based). There is a massive gap for trainers who understand fitness and can provide practical, goal-oriented meal plans.

### Where to Find Nutrition-Only Clients

**Channel 1: Referrals from Training Clients**

Your training clients know people who need nutrition help. Ask specifically:

> "Hey [Name], I've started offering nutrition-only services — meal plans and monthly check-ins for people who aren't training with me but want help with their eating. Do you know anyone who's been struggling with that? I'm looking for 5 people to work with this month."

Make it easy: draft a text they can forward.

**Channel 2: Social Media — Education-First Content**

Nutrition content performs exceptionally well on social media because everyone eats and everyone has food questions. Post content that demonstrates your expertise:

- **"What I'd eat if I wanted to lose 10kg"** — a day-of-eating post with macros and reasoning
- **"Meal prep Sunday"** — show a real meal prep with portions and nutritional breakdown
- **"3 meals that look complicated but take 15 minutes"** — recipe posts with photos
- **"The most common nutrition mistake I see in my clients"** — education posts that position you as an expert
- **"Macro myth-busting"** — short posts correcting common misconceptions

End every post with a call to action: "I build customized meal plans for people who want to stop guessing. DM me 'PLAN' if you want to see what a week would look like for your goals."

**Channel 3: Local Business Partnerships**

Partner with businesses whose customers overlap with your target market:

- **Yoga and Pilates studios** — their clients exercise but often lack nutrition structure
- **CrossFit boxes** — athletes who want performance nutrition
- **Physiotherapy clinics** — patients recovering from injury who need nutrition support
- **Corporate wellness programs** — companies that want to offer nutrition resources to employees
- **Health food stores and cafes** — display business cards or flyers, offer to run a free workshop

The pitch: "Your clients already trust you with their health. I can complement what you do by helping them with the nutrition side. Would you be open to referring clients who need nutrition guidance? I'll refer clients who need your services in return."

**Channel 4: Free Workshops and Webinars**

Host a free 30-minute workshop: "How to Build a Meal Plan That Actually Works." Do it at a local gym, community center, or online via Zoom.

Structure:

1. Why most people fail at nutrition (5 min)
2. The 3 principles of a sustainable meal plan (10 min)
3. Live demo: building a sample plan (10 min)
4. How to work with me (5 min) — your offer and call to action

Collect emails from attendees. Follow up within 24 hours with a free resource (a PDF version of the principles) and an offer for a free consultation.

**Channel 5: Online Platforms and Groups**

Join Facebook groups, Reddit communities, or forums where your target audience hangs out. Don't sell — contribute. Answer nutrition questions. Share knowledge. Over time, people will check your profile and reach out.

Groups to join:

- Local fitness groups
- Diet-specific groups (keto, plant-based, macro counting)
- Weight loss support groups
- Health and wellness communities in your city

Spend 15-20 minutes a day contributing genuine value. Within 60 days, you'll start getting inbound inquiries.

### The Nutrition-Only Consultation

The consultation for a nutrition-only client follows the same structure as a training consultation, adapted for nutrition:

**Part 1: The Opening (2 minutes)**

> "Thanks for taking the time. Here's how this works — I'll ask you about your goals, your current eating habits, and any restrictions or preferences you have. Then I'll explain how my nutrition service works and we'll see if it's a fit. No pressure either way."

**Part 2: Their Story (10 minutes)**

Key questions:

- "What's your primary goal right now — fat loss, muscle gain, performance, general health?"
- "What does your current eating look like on a typical day?"
- "Have you tried any specific diets or meal plans before? What happened?"
- "What's the hardest part about food for you right now?"
- "Do you have any dietary restrictions, allergies, or foods you absolutely won't eat?"
- "How much time do you realistically have for cooking each day?"

**Part 3: The Gap (3 minutes)**

> "Here's what I'm hearing: you want [outcome], but right now [obstacle] is getting in the way. You've tried [past approach] but it didn't stick because [reason]. Does that sound right?"

**Part 4: The Solution (5 minutes)**

> "What I do is build you a completely customized meal plan based on everything you just told me. I use a platform called EvoFit Meals — it calculates your exact nutritional needs based on your goals and activity level, then generates a plan with recipes you'll actually enjoy, a full shopping list, and all the macro breakdowns. I update the plan regularly based on your progress, and we check in [monthly/bi-weekly] to make sure everything's working."

**Part 5: The Close (5 minutes)**

> "The investment is $[X]/month. That includes your customized plan, [frequency] updates, and our monthly check-in. Based on what you've told me, I think you'll start seeing results within the first 2-3 weeks once we get your nutrition dialled in. Ready to get started?"

## Section 6: Objection Handling

### The 7 Most Common Objections and How to Respond

**Objection 1: "I can find meal plans for free online."**

> "You absolutely can — and most of my clients tried that before coming to me. The difference is that a generic plan doesn't account for your specific goals, your food preferences, your schedule, or how your nutrition needs to sync with your training. What I build is customized to you and adjusted based on your progress. Free plans give you information. What I offer is implementation."

**Objection 2: "I already know what to eat, I just don't do it."**

> "That's actually the most common thing I hear. And it tells me the issue isn't knowledge — it's structure and accountability. That's exactly what a customized plan provides. When someone hands you a specific plan with specific meals, portions, and a shopping list, the decision fatigue disappears. You don't have to figure out what to eat — it's already figured out. Most clients tell me that's the biggest shift."

**Objection 3: "I don't want to eat boring/restrictive food."**

> "Neither do I — and that's not what this is. Your plan will be built around foods you actually enjoy. I'll ask you about your preferences, your dislikes, and any restrictions before I build anything. If you hate broccoli, broccoli won't be in your plan. The whole point is to create something you'll actually follow."

**Objection 4: "That's expensive on top of training."**

> "I understand. Here's how I think about it: you're investing [X] per month in training. If your nutrition isn't supporting that training, you're getting 50-60% of the results you could be getting. The meal plan add-on closes that gap. It's not an additional cost — it's the thing that makes your training investment actually pay off."

**Objection 5: "I'll just do it myself."**

> "I respect that. If you want, I can give you some general guidelines to start with. But here's what I've seen: building a proper meal plan that accounts for your macros, your training schedule, your food preferences, and your calorie targets takes real time and expertise. Most people who try to do it themselves spend hours, get frustrated, and end up back at square one. The value of what I offer isn't just the plan — it's the hours you don't spend trying to figure it out yourself."

**Objection 6: "I need to think about it."**

> "Of course. Can I ask — what's the main thing you want to think through? Is it the cost, the time commitment, or whether this is the right approach for you? [Listen to their answer, then address the specific concern.] How about this — let me build you a free 3-day sample so you can see exactly what you'd be getting. No commitment. If you love it, we'll set up the full service. If not, you've got 3 days of great meals for free."

**Objection 7: "I tried a meal plan service before and it didn't work."**

> "I hear that a lot, and I'm curious — what didn't work about it? [Listen.] That makes sense. What's different about what I do is [address their specific complaint]. And because we're working together, I can adjust the plan in real-time based on what's working and what isn't. It's not a static PDF — it's an evolving strategy."

Your pitch must address all three.

## Section 10: Webinar Funnel Framework

**A webinar funnel is the highest-converting acquisition channel for nutrition coaching at scale.** It works for both B2C (individual clients) and B2B (corporate and partnership prospects).

### The Webinar Structure (45 minutes)

**Title:** "Why Your Diet Keeps Failing — And the 3-Step System That Actually Works"

**Minutes 0-5: The Hook**
Open with a surprising statistic: "95% of people who start a diet will quit within 90 days. Not because they lack willpower — because the approach is fundamentally broken."

**Minutes 5-15: The Problem (Education)**
Explain why generic meal plans fail:

- They ignore individual preferences and schedules
- They require perfect conditions that never exist
- They have no feedback loop — when something isn't working, there's no one to adjust

**Minutes 15-30: The Framework (Value Delivery)**
Teach your 3-step system:

1. **Personalization** — How to calculate your specific calorie and macro targets
2. **Flexibility** — Why a plan must accommodate real life, not replace it
3. **Accountability** — Why coaching outperforms willpower by 3x

Deliver genuine value here. Attendees should walk away with actionable knowledge even if they never buy.

**Minutes 30-40: The Offer**
Present your nutrition coaching service as the done-for-you version of what you just taught:

- "I just taught you the framework. For most people, the challenge isn't understanding it — it's implementing it consistently. That's exactly what my coaching does."
- Present pricing, packages, and a webinar-exclusive incentive (first month at X% off, bonus meal plan, etc.)

**Minutes 40-45: Q&A and Close**
Answer questions live. Every question is an objection you can handle publicly — which sells to everyone else watching.

## Section 12: Multi-Location Acquisition Strategy

**For enterprise operators running nutrition programs across 3+ locations.** The challenge at multi-location scale is not generating leads — it is generating them consistently across every location and ensuring each facility converts at a competitive rate.

### Centralized vs. Decentralized Acquisition

| Approach                 | When to Use                                                 | How It Works                                                                                                                                        |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Centralized**          | Brand-level campaigns, corporate contracts, webinar funnels | Marketing team generates leads centrally, distributes to locations based on geography                                                               |
| **Decentralized**        | Local partnerships, community events, in-gym upsells        | Each location manager runs their own outreach with shared templates and scripts                                                                     |
| **Hybrid (recommended)** | Most enterprise operations                                  | Central team handles paid ads, webinars, and corporate outreach. Location managers handle local partnerships, in-gym upsells, and community events. |

| Cost per acquisition                  | $\_\_\_    | $\_\_\_    | $\_\_\_    | $\_\_\_    |

**Action framework:** The bottom-performing location each month receives a targeted intervention from the program director — whether that is additional staff training, a local marketing push, or a leadership conversation about why nutrition is being deprioritized.

### Multi-Location Launch Playbook

When launching nutrition services at a new location:

**Week 1: Infrastructure**

- Set up EvoFit Meals profiles for all coaches at the new location
- Train coaching staff (60-minute session using the staff training script from the Sales Scripts guide)
- Create 5 sample meal plans that represent the location's typical member demographics

**Week 2: Soft Launch**

- Each coach identifies their 5 most engaged training clients and has the upsell conversation
- Location manager sends the group announcement email to all members
- Post 3 pieces of nutrition content on the location's social media

**Week 3: Full Launch**

- Host an in-gym nutrition workshop (free, open to all members)
- Launch the "founding member" offer — 10 spots at a discounted rate
- Activate the local business partnership outreach (3-5 nearby businesses)

**Week 4: Accelerate**

- Follow up with all soft-launch leads who haven't converted
- Request testimonials from early adopters
- Review numbers and identify what's working for this specific location

**Target:** 15-25 nutrition clients per location within 60 days of launch.

## Section 13: B2B Nutrition Consulting Sales Process

**For enterprise operators selling nutrition coaching as a service to other businesses** — not just gyms, but any organization that wants to offer nutrition support to their people.

### B2B Prospect Categories

| Prospect Type           | Decision Maker                       | Key Value Proposition                        |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| Gyms & studios          | Owner / General Manager              | Revenue stream + member retention            |
| Corporate employers     | HR Director / Wellness Manager       | Healthcare cost reduction + productivity     |
| Schools & universities  | Athletic Director / Student Services | Athletic performance + student wellbeing     |
| Sports teams            | Head Coach / Team Manager            | Performance optimization + injury prevention |
| Medical practices       | Practice Owner / Wellness Director   | Complementary service for patients           |
| Insurance companies     | Wellness Program Manager             | Preventive care cost reduction               |
| Residential communities | HOA / Community Manager              | Resident wellness programming                |
| Hotels & resorts        | General Manager / Wellness Director  | Guest experience differentiation             |

### The B2B Sales Process (6 Steps)

**Step 1: Prospect Identification (Week 1)**
Build a list of 50 target organizations. Use LinkedIn, local business directories, and industry associations. Prioritize organizations that already have wellness programs — they have budget allocated.

**Step 2: Initial Outreach (Weeks 1-2)**
Send personalized outreach to the decision maker. Reference something specific about their organization.

> "Hi [Name], I noticed [Company/Gym/School] recently [opened a new wellness center / launched an employee wellness initiative / hired a new athletic director]. I specialize in implementing nutrition coaching programs for organizations like yours — personalized meal plans that measurably improve [health outcomes / athletic performance / member retention]. Would a 15-minute conversation be worth your time?"

**Step 3: Discovery Call (Week 2-3)**
Ask: What are your current wellness offerings? Where are the gaps? What outcomes matter most? What budget exists? Who else needs to approve this?

**Step 4: Custom Proposal (Week 3-4)**
Build a proposal tailored to their answers. Include: program description, implementation timeline, pricing (with volume options), expected outcomes, and your qualifications.

**Step 5: Pilot Program (Month 2)**
Offer a 30-day pilot with 10-20 participants. Set clear success metrics before starting.

**Step 6: Full Contract (Month 3)**
Present pilot results. Propose full-program pricing with a 6-12 month commitment.

### B2B Pipeline Management

Track every prospect through these stages:

| Stage       | Definition                       | Target Duration                   |
| ----------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Prospect    | Identified, not contacted        | Max 2 weeks                       |
| Contacted   | Outreach sent, awaiting response | Max 2 weeks                       |
| Discovery   | Call scheduled or completed      | Max 1 week                        |
| Proposal    | Custom proposal delivered        | Max 2 weeks                       |

| Negotiation | Discussing full contract terms   | Max 2 weeks                       |
