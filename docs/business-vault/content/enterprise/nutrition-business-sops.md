# Nutrition Business SOPs

## 20 Standard Operating Procedures for Team-Based Nutrition Coaching Operations

---

### About This Document

This is your operational manual for running a nutrition coaching business with a team. Each SOP follows the same structure: Purpose, Scope, Procedure (numbered steps), Exceptions, and Review Schedule. Print this document, distribute it to every member of your team, and treat it as the single source of truth for how your nutrition program operates.

SOPs exist to make quality consistent and training efficient. When a new coach joins your team, hand them this document. When a mistake happens, reference the relevant SOP to identify where the process broke down. When you scale to additional locations, this document ensures every location operates the same way.

**Review cadence:** The program director reviews all SOPs annually. Individual SOPs are reviewed ad hoc whenever an incident reveals a gap. Changes are documented with a version number and date.

## SOP 1: New Client Intake

**Purpose:** Ensure every new nutrition client is processed consistently from signup through first plan delivery.

**Scope:** Applies to all new client signups across all locations and coaches.

**Procedure:**

1. Client completes signup (payment processed through billing system).
2. Intake Coordinator sends the welcome email within 2 hours of signup. The welcome email includes: dietary questionnaire, food preference survey, allergy and intolerance disclosure form, and the "What to Expect" client guide.
3. Intake Coordinator monitors for form completion. If forms are not returned within 48 hours, send a reminder email. If not returned within 72 hours, send a text message. If not returned within 5 business days, make a phone call.
4. Upon receiving completed forms, Intake Coordinator reviews for completeness. Any missing fields are flagged and the client is asked to complete them before proceeding.
5. Intake Coordinator creates the client profile in EvoFit Meals. All dietary restrictions, allergies, and preferences are entered. Macro targets are calculated based on the client's goals, activity level, and body composition data.
6. If the client reports a medical condition on the questionnaire that requires dietary management (diabetes, eating disorders, kidney disease, etc.), the Intake Coordinator flags the file to the Program Director before assignment. The Program Director determines whether an RD referral is needed before plan creation.
7. Intake Coordinator assigns the client to a nutrition coach based on current capacity, specialty match, and location.
8. Assigned coach reviews all forms and the EvoFit Meals profile within 24 hours of assignment.
9. Coach builds the first customized meal plan within 48 hours of form completion. The plan includes: 7 days of meals, full recipes with portions, macro and calorie breakdowns per meal, and a consolidated grocery list.
10. Coach sends a personal welcome message to the client alongside the plan delivery. The message references the client's specific goal.
11. Coach schedules the first check-in for 7-10 days after plan delivery.
12. The automated 30-day onboarding drip sequence is activated.

**Exceptions:**

- VIP or corporate clients may require expedited intake (24-hour form-to-plan turnaround). Flag these at signup.
- Clients transferring from another coach within the organization skip steps 2-6 — their profile already exists.

**Review Schedule:** Quarterly, or after any intake-related client complaint.

## SOP 2: Meal Plan Creation Workflow

**Purpose:** Standardize how meal plans are built, reviewed, and delivered to ensure quality and accuracy.

**Scope:** Applies to every meal plan created by any coach on the team.

**Procedure:**

1. Coach opens the client's EvoFit Meals profile and verifies that all dietary restrictions, allergies, and preferences are current.
2. Coach confirms the client's macro and calorie targets are accurate for their current phase (check if any recent adjustments were made).
3. Coach generates a base plan using the platform's recommendation engine, filtered by the client's restrictions and preferences.
4. Coach reviews every meal in the plan for: accuracy of macro totals, ingredient compatibility with allergies, recipe complexity matching the client's cooking skill level, variety (no more than 2 repeat meals per week unless the client prefers repeats).
5. Coach customizes the plan: swaps any recipes that don't fit, adjusts portions if needed, and verifies the grocery list is consolidated and organized.
6. For first-time plans, the coach completes the Plan Quality Checklist (see below) before delivery.
7. Coach delivers the plan through the platform with a personal message that highlights 1-2 specific recipes the client will enjoy.
8. If this is a plan update (not the first plan), the coach notes what changed and why in the delivery message.

**Plan Quality Checklist (must pass before delivery):**

- [ ] All allergies and restrictions reflected — zero violations
- [ ] Macro totals within 5% of target for each day
- [ ] No recipe appears more than twice in the 7-day plan (unless client-requested)
- [ ] Prep time per recipe matches client's stated time availability
- [ ] Grocery list is complete and consolidated (no duplicate items)
- [ ] At least 2 different protein sources used across the week
- [ ] At least 3 different dinner recipes

**Exceptions:**

- Simplified plans (for clients requesting minimal variety) may have higher repeat rates — document the client's request.
- Clients on medical dietary protocols may have a narrower recipe pool — quality check focuses on safety, not variety.

**Review Schedule:** Monthly spot-check by Lead Coach (review 5 random plans per coach per month).

## SOP 3: Allergy Verification

**Purpose:** Prevent allergenic ingredients from appearing in any client's meal plan.

**Scope:** Applies to every client with a reported allergy or intolerance. Non-negotiable.

**Procedure:**

1. At intake, the Intake Coordinator enters every reported allergy and intolerance into the client's EvoFit Meals profile using the platform's dietary restriction fields.
2. Severe allergies (anaphylactic) are flagged with a HIGH PRIORITY marker in the client profile and in the coaching team's client roster.
3. When building any plan, the coach enables the dietary restriction filters before generating the plan. This prevents allergenic recipes from appearing in the base plan.
4. After generating the plan, the coach manually reviews the ingredient list for every meal. Platform filters catch the primary allergen, but cross-contamination ingredients or less obvious sources (e.g., soy lecithin in chocolate, casein in "non-dairy" products) require manual review for severe allergies.
5. If the coach identifies a potential allergen that was not caught by the filter, the recipe is removed and replaced immediately.
6. The plan delivery message for clients with severe allergies includes a reminder: "Your plan has been built with your [allergen] restriction applied to every meal. If you notice any ingredient you're unsure about, do not eat it — message me immediately and I'll verify or swap it."
7. If a client reports a new allergy or intolerance after intake, the coach updates the EvoFit Meals profile within 24 hours and rebuilds the current plan if any existing meals contain the newly reported allergen.
8. All allergy-related interactions are documented in the client's file with date, details, and action taken.

**Exceptions:** None. This SOP has no exceptions. Allergy safety is absolute.

**Review Schedule:** Reviewed after any allergy-related incident. Minimum annual review.

## SOP 4: Plan Adjustment Process

**Purpose:** Define when and how to adjust a client's meal plan.

**Scope:** All ongoing client plan modifications outside of scheduled plan rebuilds.

**Procedure:**

1. Client requests a change (recipe swap, portion adjustment, schedule accommodation) through messaging or at a check-in.
2. Coach acknowledges the request within 4 business hours.
3. Coach evaluates the request: Does it change macro targets? Does it require a full plan rebuild or a single-meal swap?
4. For single-meal swaps: Coach makes the change in EvoFit Meals and notifies the client within 24 hours. "Done — I've swapped [old meal] for [new meal]. Same macros, different flavor."
5. For macro adjustments: Coach recalculates targets based on current data (weight, adherence, activity level), updates the profile, and generates a new plan. This counts as a scheduled plan update.
6. For schedule changes (e.g., travel, new work hours): Coach rebuilds the affected days with appropriate alternatives and delivers within 48 hours.
7. All adjustments are logged in the client's notes: date, what was changed, and why.

**Exceptions:**

- Emergency changes (e.g., client discovered a new food allergy after eating a recipe) are handled within 2 hours, not 24.

**Review Schedule:** Semi-annually.

## SOP 5: Team Handoffs

**Purpose:** Ensure seamless client transitions between coaches.

**Scope:** Any client changing from one coach to another, for any reason.

**Procedure:**

1. Program Director or Lead Coach initiates the handoff by notifying both the outgoing and incoming coach.
2. Outgoing coach writes a Client Handoff Brief within 48 hours. The brief includes: current phase in the adherence protocol, adherence score trend (last 4 weeks), key food preferences and dislikes not captured in the profile, any red flags or sensitivities (e.g., history of disordered eating, family resistance, budget concerns), the client's communication style ("Prefers minimal contact" vs. "Needs frequent encouragement"), and the status of any pending actions (upcoming check-in, plan rebuild, etc.).
3. Incoming coach reviews the brief and the client's full EvoFit Meals profile within 24 hours.
4. Program Director sends the client a transition notification: "Hi [Name], [New Coach] will be taking over your nutrition coaching starting [date]. They've reviewed your full history and plan. You won't need to re-explain anything."
5. Incoming coach sends a personal introduction within 24 hours of the transition notification.
6. Incoming coach maintains the existing plan for the first 1-2 weeks (no changes unless urgent) to build trust before making adjustments.
7. Program Director monitors the client's engagement for 2 weeks post-handoff. Any decline triggers a check-in with both the client and the incoming coach.

**Exceptions:**

- If the outgoing coach is unavailable (e.g., emergency departure), the Lead Coach writes the brief from the client's file and EvoFit Meals data.

**Review Schedule:** Annually, or after any handoff-related client complaint.

## SOP 6: Client Escalation

**Purpose:** Define when a client situation exceeds a coach's authority and must be escalated.

**Scope:** All coaches and support staff.

**Procedure:**

1. Coach identifies an escalation trigger (see table below).
2. Coach documents the situation in the client's file: what happened, when, and what has been done so far.
3. Coach notifies the Lead Coach (or Program Director, for high-severity issues) within 24 hours via the escalation channel (email/message thread designated for escalations).
4. Lead Coach or Program Director acknowledges the escalation within 4 business hours.
5. The escalation owner (Lead Coach or Director) takes appropriate action: contacts the client, adjusts the plan, makes a referral, or modifies the service.
6. The resolution is documented in the client's file with the date and outcome.
7. The escalation is added to the monthly escalation log for pattern review.

**Escalation Triggers:**

| Trigger                                                     | Escalate To                | Urgency         |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- | --------------- |
| Client reports a medical condition not previously disclosed | Program Director           | Within 24 hours |
| Client displays signs of disordered eating                  | Program Director           | Same day        |
| Client requests refund                                      | Program Director           | Within 24 hours |
| Client threatens to leave and coach cannot resolve          | Lead Coach                 | Within 24 hours |
| Client reports an allergic reaction to a plan ingredient    | Program Director           | Immediately     |
| Client harassment or inappropriate behavior                 | Program Director           | Immediately     |
| Client plateau lasting 6+ weeks despite good adherence      | Lead Coach                 | Next check-in   |
| Client complaint about another coach                        | Program Director           | Within 48 hours |
| Payment dispute or billing issue                            | Program Director + Billing | Within 24 hours |

**Exceptions:** None. All situations in the trigger table must be escalated regardless of the coach's confidence in handling them independently.

**Review Schedule:** Quarterly review of escalation log to identify patterns.

## SOP 7: Data Handling and Privacy

**Purpose:** Protect client dietary, health, and personal information.

**Scope:** All team members with access to client data.

**Procedure:**

1. Client data is stored only in approved systems: EvoFit Meals (nutrition data), the billing platform (payment data), and the coaching CRM (notes and communications). No client data may be stored in personal spreadsheets, text messages, or personal email accounts.
2. Access to client data is restricted to: the assigned coach, the Lead Coach, the Program Director, and the Intake Coordinator. Other team members may not access a client's file without authorization from the Program Director.
3. Client dietary and health information is never shared publicly, on social media, or with other clients — even in anonymized form — without the client's explicit written consent.
4. Client progress photos, before/after images, and testimonials require a separate signed media release before use.
5. When a client cancels, their data is retained in the system for 12 months to facilitate easy re-enrollment. After 12 months of inactivity, the client is notified that their data will be archived. After 18 months, data is permanently deleted unless the client requests retention.
6. All coaches complete a data privacy acknowledgment during their onboarding, confirming they understand and will comply with this SOP.
7. If a data breach is suspected (unauthorized access, lost device, compromised account), the team member reports it to the Program Director immediately. The Director assesses the scope, notifies affected clients if necessary, and documents the incident.

**Exceptions:** Court orders or legal requirements may mandate data disclosure. In such cases, the Program Director and legal counsel handle the request.

**Review Schedule:** Annually. Updated whenever privacy regulations change in applicable jurisdictions.

## SOP 8: Food Photography Standards

**Purpose:** Maintain consistent, professional quality when photographing meals for marketing, social media, or client communication.

**Scope:** All team members creating nutrition-related visual content.

**Procedure:**

1. Photograph in natural light whenever possible. Position the dish near a window with indirect sunlight. Avoid overhead fluorescent lighting.
2. Use a clean, neutral background — white plates on a wooden surface or marble countertop. Remove clutter from the frame.
3. Shoot at a 45-degree angle for plated meals. Use a direct overhead (flat-lay) angle for bowls and prep shots.
4. Include the full plate or bowl in frame. Do not crop off portions of the food.
5. Style the dish before photographing: wipe plate edges, add a garnish if appropriate (fresh herbs, a drizzle of sauce), and ensure the food looks appetizing, not staged.
6. Include macro information as a text overlay or caption — clients and prospects want to see that the food is both beautiful and functional.
7. All food photos used in marketing must be of real food prepared from EvoFit Meals recipes. Stock photos may only be used with an explicit "stock image" label.
8. Client meal photos shared publicly require client permission (covered by the media release in SOP 7).

**Exceptions:** Informal behind-the-scenes content (Stories, quick posts) may use lower production quality to maintain authenticity. Core marketing materials (website, ads, sales pages) must meet the full standard.

**Review Schedule:** Semi-annually.

## SOP 9: Recipe Testing and Approval

**Purpose:** Ensure any new recipe added to the recommendation pool meets nutritional accuracy, taste, and practicality standards.

**Scope:** All custom recipes created by coaches for client plans (in addition to the EvoFit Meals standard library).

**Procedure:**

1. Coach creates the recipe with full ingredient list, quantities, and preparation steps.
2. Coach calculates the nutritional information per serving (calories, protein, carbs, fats, fiber). Use a verified nutrition database — do not estimate.
3. Coach prepares the recipe personally and verifies: preparation time matches the stated time, ingredients are commonly available, the recipe tastes good (subjective but critical), and portion sizes are realistic.
4. Coach submits the recipe to the Lead Coach for review: nutritional accuracy check, clarity of instructions, and appropriateness for the target audience.
5. If approved, the recipe is added to the team's custom recipe library with tags for dietary categories (gluten-free, dairy-free, etc.), prep time, cooking skill level, and cuisine type.
6. If rejected, the Lead Coach provides specific feedback and the coach revises.
7. Custom recipes are reviewed annually for continued inclusion. Recipes with low client satisfaction scores (tracked via check-in feedback) are rotated out.

**Exceptions:** Emergency recipe swaps for a specific client do not require the full approval process — but the custom recipe should be submitted for review within the week.

**Review Schedule:** Annually for recipe library audit. Ongoing for new submissions.

## SOP 10: Supplier Partnerships

**Purpose:** Establish and manage relationships with food suppliers, grocery delivery services, or meal prep companies that benefit clients.

**Scope:** Program directors and designated partnership managers.

**Procedure:**

1. Identify potential partners whose products or services complement your nutrition coaching: local grocery delivery services, meal prep companies, supplement brands, kitchen equipment retailers.
2. Evaluate the partner against three criteria: quality (does their product/service meet your standards?), reliability (will they consistently deliver?), and alignment (does their brand complement yours?).
3. Negotiate partnership terms: referral fee or commission structure, co-branded content opportunities, exclusive discounts for your clients, and any exclusivity requirements.
4. Document the partnership agreement in writing, including: term length, termination clause, financial terms, and brand usage guidelines.
5. Announce the partnership to your coaching team and provide them with the information they need to mention it to clients when appropriate.
6. Review partner performance quarterly: client feedback, partner reliability, and financial value.
7. Terminate partnerships that receive negative client feedback or fail to meet quality standards. Provide 30 days written notice.

**Exceptions:** Informal, non-monetary referral relationships (e.g., "I recommend this local farm stand") do not require formal agreements. Formalize only when money or brand equity is exchanged.

**Review Schedule:** Quarterly.

## SOP 11: Quality Control

**Purpose:** Systematically monitor and maintain the quality of nutrition coaching delivered across the team.

**Scope:** All coaches and program leadership.

**Procedure:**

1. Lead Coach conducts monthly plan audits: review 5 random plans per coach for adherence to the Plan Quality Checklist (SOP 2).
2. Program Director reviews monthly NPS scores by coach. Coaches with NPS below 40 receive a quality improvement plan.
3. Client satisfaction surveys are sent quarterly (separate from NPS). Survey covers: plan quality, coach responsiveness, recipe variety, and overall value.
4. Monthly team calibration meeting (30 min): review one anonymized client case as a team. Discuss: "Would you have built this plan differently? Would you have handled this check-in differently?" Calibration ensures coaches converge on quality standards.
5. Mystery client review (annually): a designated team member or external person signs up as a client and evaluates the full experience end-to-end. Findings are presented to the team without identifying the evaluator.
6. Quality metrics are tracked in the program dashboard:

| Metric                             | Target | Red Flag      |
| ---------------------------------- | ------ | ------------- |
| Plan accuracy (allergy compliance) | 100%   | Any violation |
| Plan delivery on time              | 95%+   | Below 90%     |
| Average client satisfaction score  | 4.0+/5 | Below 3.5     |
| Check-in completion rate           | 95%+   | Below 85%     |
| NPS by coach                       | 50+    | Below 40      |

**Exceptions:** None. Quality control runs regardless of team size or workload.

**Review Schedule:** Monthly for operational metrics. Annually for SOP revision.

## SOP 12: Emergency Protocols

**Purpose:** Define responses to health, safety, or operational emergencies.

**Scope:** All team members.

**Procedure:**

**Client health emergency (allergic reaction, medical event reported during coaching):**

1. Instruct the client to call emergency services (911) immediately if they are in physical danger.
2. Document what happened: what they ate, when symptoms started, and what they are experiencing.
3. Notify the Program Director immediately.
4. Review the meal plan for the triggering ingredient and remove it. Cross-reference with the allergy form.
5. Follow up with the client within 24 hours of the event.
6. Conduct a root cause analysis: did the allergy form capture the allergen? Was the plan filter applied correctly? Document findings and corrective actions.

**Coach unavailability (illness, personal emergency):**

1. Coach notifies the Lead Coach as soon as possible.
2. Lead Coach reassigns the coach's clients to available team members using the Emergency Coverage List (maintained by the Program Director).
3. Coverage coaches access the client's EvoFit Meals profile and continue plan delivery and check-ins.
4. Clients are notified only if the absence exceeds 3 business days: "[Coach] is temporarily unavailable. [Coverage Coach] will be supporting you this week."

**System outage (EvoFit Meals platform down):**

1. Coach reports the outage to the Program Director.
2. Program Director contacts the platform support team and communicates the estimated resolution time.
3. Coaches notify affected clients: "There's a temporary issue with the platform. Your plan is intact and will be accessible once the issue is resolved. In the meantime, here's a text-based summary of today's meals: [list meals with portions]."
4. If the outage exceeds 24 hours, coaches deliver plans via email or PDF as a backup.

**Exceptions:** None for health emergencies. System outage procedures may vary based on outage severity.

**Review Schedule:** Annually. Updated after any emergency incident.

## SOP 13: New Coach Onboarding

**Purpose:** Bring new nutrition coaches up to speed quickly and consistently.

**Scope:** Every new coach joining the team.

**Procedure:**

1. **Day 1:** New coach receives the full SOP document, the sales scripts guide, the adherence protocol, and the retention kit. They are assigned a mentor (Lead Coach or senior coach).
2. **Day 1-2:** Platform training. The mentor walks the new coach through EvoFit Meals: creating a client profile, setting restrictions, generating a plan, customizing it, delivering it, and navigating the recipe library. The new coach builds 3 practice plans.
3. **Day 3:** Sales and conversation training. The new coach reads the Nutrition Sales Scripts and practices Scripts 1-8 with their mentor in a role-play session.
4. **Day 4:** Shadow a senior coach during 2 client check-ins (with client permission). Observe communication style, how feedback is collected, and how plans are adjusted.
5. **Day 5:** New coach builds their first real plan (for a low-complexity client) under mentor supervision. Mentor reviews the plan against the Quality Checklist before delivery.
6. **Week 2:** New coach manages 3-5 clients independently with daily mentor check-ins. Mentor reviews all plans before delivery.
7. **Week 3-4:** New coach manages 5-10 clients. Mentor reviews plans twice per week (spot-checks).
8. **Month 2:** New coach is fully independent. Mentor check-ins move to weekly. Full client load is assigned gradually based on performance.
9. **Month 3:** 90-day review with Program Director. Review metrics: plan quality scores, client satisfaction, retention rate, check-in completion. Address any gaps.

**Exceptions:** Coaches with prior nutrition coaching experience may have an accelerated onboarding (skip Day 4 shadows, reduce mentor review period to 1 week).

**Review Schedule:** After each new coach completes onboarding. Annually for SOP revision.

## SOP 14: Client Complaint Handling

**Purpose:** Resolve client complaints promptly and use them as improvement signals.

**Scope:** All client-facing team members.

**Procedure:**

1. Any team member who receives a complaint acknowledges it within 4 business hours: "Thank you for letting me know. I take this seriously and I'm looking into it right now."
2. The complaint is logged in the complaint tracker: date, client name, nature of complaint, who received it, and initial response.
3. The assigned coach (or Lead Coach, if the complaint is about the assigned coach) investigates within 24 hours. Investigation includes: reviewing the client's plan, check-in history, and communication log.
4. A resolution is proposed to the client within 48 hours of the initial complaint. Resolutions may include: plan rebuild, recipe swaps, schedule adjustment, coach change, service credit, or refund.
5. The client's response to the resolution is documented. If the client is satisfied, the case is closed. If not, the case is escalated to the Program Director.
6. The Program Director reviews all complaints monthly for patterns. If 3+ clients report the same issue within a 30-day period, it triggers a systemic review (process change, coach training, or service adjustment).

**Exceptions:** Complaints involving safety (allergic reactions, medical events) bypass this process and go directly to the Emergency Protocol (SOP 12).

**Review Schedule:** Monthly for complaint log review. Annually for SOP revision.

## SOP 15: Refund and Cancellation

**Purpose:** Handle refund requests and cancellations consistently and fairly.

**Scope:** All cancellation and refund requests.

**Procedure:**

1. Client requests cancellation or refund through any channel (message, email, phone, in-person).
2. Assigned coach initiates a retention conversation (see Retention Kit scripts) before processing the cancellation. The goal is to understand why and offer alternatives — not to pressure the client into staying.
3. If the client proceeds with cancellation: the coach confirms the cancellation date (end of current billing cycle unless otherwise specified), sends the exit survey, and builds a final transition plan (a 4-week independent plan for the client to follow on their own).
4. Refund eligibility:

| Timeframe                | Policy                                                                         |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Within 14 days of signup | Full refund, no questions asked                                                |
| 15-30 days after signup  | Prorated refund (unused portion of the month)                                  |
| After 30 days            | No refund — but unused days are credited if the client returns within 6 months |

5. Refund requests are processed by the Program Director within 5 business days.
6. The cancellation is documented: date, reason (from exit survey), tenure length, and whether a retention offer was made.
7. The client is added to the re-engagement sequence (30-day post-cancellation drip).

**Exceptions:** Extraordinary circumstances (medical emergency, family crisis, billing error) may warrant a full refund outside the standard policy. Program Director has discretion.

**Review Schedule:** Semi-annually.

## SOP 16: Weekly Team Meeting Agenda

**Purpose:** Keep the team aligned, informed, and accountable through a structured weekly meeting.

**Scope:** All coaches, Lead Coaches, and Program Director.

**Procedure:**

**Meeting details:** Every [day of week], [time], 30 minutes maximum. Attendance mandatory for all active coaches.

**Agenda:**

1. **Wins of the Week (5 min):** Each coach shares one client win. This builds morale and shares best practices.
2. **Numbers Review (5 min):** Program Director presents: total active clients, new signups, cancellations, and retention rate for the week.
3. **At-Risk Client Discussion (10 min):** Coaches flag clients in AMBER or RED status. Team discusses intervention strategies.
4. **Escalations and Complaints (5 min):** Review any open escalations or complaints from the past week.
5. **Action Items (5 min):** Each coach states their top priority for the week. Program Director assigns any team-level tasks.

**Rules:**

- Start on time. End on time.
- No phones or multitasking during the meeting.
- If a topic requires more than 2 minutes of discussion, take it offline.
- Meeting notes are documented and shared within 2 hours.

**Exceptions:** Weekly meetings may be cancelled during holiday weeks with Program Director approval. Missed meetings are not "made up" — they are skipped.

**Review Schedule:** Annually.

## SOP 17: Monthly Reporting

**Purpose:** Compile and distribute monthly performance reports for the nutrition program.

**Scope:** Program Director is responsible for compiling. Reports are distributed to gym ownership and coaching leadership.

**Procedure:**

1. By the 3rd business day of each month, the Program Director compiles the monthly report.
2. The report includes:

| Section           | Metrics                                                              |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Revenue           | MRR, revenue by service type, revenue by coach, revenue vs. target   |
| Clients           | Total active, new signups, cancellations, net change, average tenure |
| Retention         | Monthly retention rate, churn rate, reasons for cancellation         |
| Quality           | Average satisfaction score, NPS, plan delivery on-time rate          |
| Coach Performance | Clients per coach, retention per coach, satisfaction per coach       |
| Pipeline          | Leads in pipeline, conversion rate, projected next-month signups     |
| Escalations       | Number and types of escalations, resolution rate                     |

3. The report is delivered to gym ownership and lead coaches via email by the 5th business day.
4. A 30-minute monthly review meeting is held to discuss the report: what improved, what declined, and what actions are needed.
5. Action items from the review are documented and assigned with deadlines.

**Exceptions:** If the data source is unavailable (platform outage, delayed data), the report may be delayed by up to 3 business days with notification to stakeholders.

**Review Schedule:** Annually for SOP revision. Monthly for report content.

## SOP 19: Compliance Audit

**Purpose:** Verify that all operations comply with applicable regulations, scope-of-practice requirements, and internal policies.

**Scope:** All aspects of the nutrition coaching business.

**Procedure:**

1. The Program Director conducts a semi-annual compliance audit covering:

| Area                | What to Check                                                              |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scope of practice   | Are all coaches operating within their credentialed scope?                 |
| Client forms        | Are allergy forms on file for every active client?                         |
| Data privacy        | Is client data stored only in approved systems?                            |
| Marketing claims    | Do all marketing materials use approved language (no medical claims)?      |
| Referral compliance | Are clients with medical conditions referred to appropriate professionals? |
| Insurance           | Is professional liability insurance current for all coaches?               |
| Credentialing       | Are all nutrition-related certifications current (not expired)?            |
| Billing             | Are billing practices transparent and consistent with stated policies?     |

2. The audit is documented in a Compliance Audit Report. Each area receives a status: COMPLIANT, NEEDS ATTENTION, or NON-COMPLIANT.
3. NON-COMPLIANT areas are addressed within 7 business days. The responsible party is notified and corrective action is documented.
4. NEEDS ATTENTION areas are addressed within 30 days.
5. The Compliance Audit Report is retained for 3 years.
6. If a regulatory change affects nutrition coaching in the business's jurisdiction, the Program Director reviews all relevant SOPs and updates them within 30 days.

**Exceptions:** None. Compliance audits run on schedule regardless of business conditions.

**Review Schedule:** Semi-annually (the audit itself). SOP revised annually.

## SOP 20: Technology Setup

**Purpose:** Ensure all technology systems are properly configured, maintained, and used consistently across the team.

**Scope:** All software, platforms, and tools used in nutrition coaching operations.

**Procedure:**

1. **Core Technology Stack:**

| System                  | Purpose                                                | Admin                      |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| EvoFit Meals            | Meal plan creation, client profiles, recipe library    | Program Director           |
| Billing platform        | Payment processing, subscription management            | Program Director + Finance |
| CRM / Client Notes      | Communication history, escalation logs, handoff briefs | Program Director           |
| Communication tool      | Team messaging, client messaging                       | Lead Coach                 |
| Video conferencing      | Check-in calls, team meetings                          | All coaches                |
| Social media management | Content scheduling, engagement tracking                | Marketing lead             |

2. **New Coach Setup:**
   - Create accounts in all systems within 24 hours of hire
   - Assign appropriate access levels (coaches see their own clients only; Lead Coaches see their pod's clients; Program Director sees all)
   - Complete platform training (SOP 13)

3. **Departing Coach Teardown:**
   - Reassign all clients before access is revoked
   - Transfer all client notes to the new coach or Lead Coach
   - Revoke access to all systems within 24 hours of departure
   - Change shared passwords that the departing coach had access to

4. **Platform Updates:**
   - When EvoFit Meals releases new features, the Program Director evaluates them within 1 week and decides whether to adopt
   - If adopted, the feature is communicated to the team with usage instructions
   - If not adopted, the team is notified that the feature is not in use (to prevent inconsistent adoption)

5. **Backup and Recovery:**
   - Client data is backed up through the platforms' built-in backup systems
   - The Program Director verifies quarterly that data export functionality works (test by exporting one client's data)
   - If a platform experiences an outage exceeding 24 hours, activate the offline backup procedures in SOP 12

**Exceptions:** Teams with fewer than 3 coaches may consolidate the CRM and communication tool into a single platform for simplicity.

**Review Schedule:** Semi-annually. Updated whenever a new tool is adopted or an existing tool is deprecated.
