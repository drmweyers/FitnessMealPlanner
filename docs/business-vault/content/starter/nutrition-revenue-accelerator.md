# The Nutrition Revenue Accelerator

## Your 14-Day Action Plan to Add Meal Planning Revenue and Land Your First 3 Nutrition Clients

---

### How to Use This Guide

This is not a course. It's a daily playbook. Each day gives you one goal, a clear reason it matters, 3-5 specific actions, and a checkpoint so you know when you're done. Do the work in order — each day builds on the last. If you've already completed a step, skip it. But don't skip ahead.

**Who this is for:** Personal trainers and fitness coaches who already have training clients but haven't added nutrition or meal planning services to their business. You're leaving revenue on the table. This guide fixes that.

**What you'll need:**

- Your EvoFit Meals account (Starter tier or above)
- Access to your current client list
- 60-90 minutes per day
- The willingness to have conversations about food with people who already trust you

**By Day 14, you will have:**

- A nutrition service offering with clear pricing and packaging
- Sample meal plans built and ready to show prospects
- At least 10 warm leads who know you offer nutrition coaching
- Your first 1-3 paying nutrition clients
- A system to keep generating nutrition revenue every month

**Important note on scope of practice:** This guide helps you build a meal planning and general nutrition coaching business. It does not replace the expertise of a Registered Dietitian. Throughout this guide, we'll flag where scope-of-practice boundaries apply. If a client presents with a medical condition requiring therapeutic dietary intervention — diabetes management, eating disorders, renal disease, etc. — refer them to a qualified RD or dietitian. Knowing your scope makes you more credible, not less.

## Day 1: Pick Your Nutrition Niche

**Goal:** Decide exactly who you're going to help with nutrition so your offer doesn't sound generic.

**Why this matters:** "I do meal plans" is invisible. Every trainer on Instagram says that. "I build meal plans for busy professionals who want to eat clean without spending Sunday meal prepping" is a service people will pay for. Your nutrition niche should overlap with — but doesn't have to be identical to — your training niche. The trainers who monetize nutrition fastest are the ones who get specific about the problem they solve.

**Actions:**

1. Look at your current training clients. Which 3-5 have asked you about food, nutrition, or meal prep in the last 90 days? Write down their names and what they asked about. What patterns do you see — are they trying to lose weight? Build muscle? Manage energy? Feed a family while hitting macros?
2. Pick ONE nutrition niche from this list. You're not committing forever — you're testing for 90 days. Good starter niches for trainers adding nutrition:
   - Weight loss meal plans for busy professionals (high demand, easy to deliver)
   - Muscle-building nutrition for men 25-45 (pairs naturally with training)
   - Meal plans for parents who want healthy family meals that support their fitness goals
   - Performance nutrition for recreational athletes (endurance, CrossFit, team sports)
3. Write your nutrition positioning statement: "I build [type of meal plans] for [specific person] so they can [specific outcome] without [specific frustration]."
   - Example: "I build weekly meal plans for busy dads who want to get lean without eating bland chicken and broccoli every night."
4. Open EvoFit Meals and update your trainer profile bio to include this nutrition positioning statement alongside your training description.

**Checkpoint:** You have a one-sentence nutrition positioning statement written down. It's specific enough that someone hearing it would immediately know whether it's for them or not.

## Day 2: Price Your Nutrition Services

**Goal:** Set pricing for your meal planning and nutrition coaching packages using math, not guesswork.

**Why this matters:** Trainers who add nutrition services as an afterthought — "Oh yeah, I can throw in a meal plan too" — never make meaningful revenue from it. Nutrition coaching is a standalone service with its own value. Price it that way. At the same time, your biggest opportunity is bundling nutrition with training for a premium package. Clients who get both training AND nutrition get better results, stay longer, and refer more.

**Actions:**

1. **Decide your service model.** Most trainers adding nutrition for the first time use one of these three:
   - **Standalone Meal Plans:** One-time custom meal plan delivery. Client pays once, you build the plan, they follow it. ($75-150 per plan depending on complexity and your market.)
   - **Monthly Nutrition Coaching:** Ongoing meal plans + weekly check-ins + plan adjustments. ($97-197/month. This is the recurring revenue play.)
   - **Training + Nutrition Bundle:** Your existing training package with nutrition layered on top at a premium. (Add $50-100/month to your current training rate.)
2. **Set your launch pricing.** Create three tiers:
   - **Single Meal Plan:** One custom 7-day plan with grocery list. Price: entry-level. This is your foot-in-the-door offer.
   - **Monthly Nutrition Coaching:** Custom meal plans + weekly adjustments + messaging support. Price: mid-range. This is what you want most clients to buy.
   - **Training + Nutrition Bundle:** Everything above + their training package. Price: premium. Position this as your "full transformation" offer.
3. Write your prices down. Put them in your phone notes. Practice saying the monthly coaching price out loud five times without explaining or apologizing.
4. Open EvoFit Meals and explore the Meal Plan Builder so you understand the time investment per plan. A typical 7-day meal plan using the recipe library takes 20-30 minutes once you know the system. Your pricing should account for plan creation time + check-in time + messaging time.

**Checkpoint:** You have three pricing tiers written down. You can say your Monthly Nutrition Coaching price out loud without flinching. You know your time investment per plan.

## Day 3: Build Your First Sample Meal Plans

**Goal:** Create 2-3 sample meal plans in EvoFit Meals that you can show to prospects as proof of what they'll get.

**Why this matters:** Nobody buys a meal plan they can't see. When you're selling nutrition services, the question in every prospect's head is "What will I actually get?" Sample plans answer that question instantly. They also prove you know how to use the tools, which builds your own confidence before you start selling.

**Actions:**

1. Open EvoFit Meals and go to the Meal Plan Builder. Create a 7-day meal plan for your target niche. Use the recipe library — you have access to thousands of recipes with full macro breakdowns. Build a plan that looks like something your ideal client would actually eat. Not bodybuilder meals. Not plain rice and chicken. Real food.
2. Create a second sample plan for a different calorie target or dietary preference within your niche. If your first plan was a 2,000-calorie weight loss plan, make your second a 2,500-calorie muscle-building plan. Variety shows range.
3. For each plan, review the auto-generated grocery list. This is one of the biggest selling points — tell prospects "You get a complete grocery list for the week. No guessing at the store."
4. Screenshot or export your sample plans. You'll use these in your outreach and consultations. Having a visual to show — "Here's exactly what a week looks like" — converts dramatically better than describing it verbally.
5. Check the Dietary Restriction Quick-Reference Guide in your vault for any common restrictions in your niche. If you train a lot of clients who are dairy-free or gluten-free, make sure at least one sample plan accommodates that.

**Checkpoint:** You have 2-3 sample meal plans built in EvoFit Meals. You can pull them up on your phone in under 10 seconds to show a prospect.

## Day 4: Create Your Nutrition Proof Content

**Goal:** Post 3 pieces of content that establish you as someone who helps clients with nutrition — not just training.

**Why this matters:** Your audience currently knows you as a trainer. They need to learn that you now help with nutrition and meal planning too. Content is how you shift that perception without being salesy. You're not posting "I sell meal plans now!" — you're demonstrating nutrition knowledge and showing what your meal plans look like. Let people connect the dots.

**Actions:**

1. **Post a sample meal plan visual.** Take a clean screenshot of one of your Day 3 meal plans — one full day of meals with the calorie/macro summary visible. Add a caption like: "Here's what a typical Tuesday looks like on one of my client meal plans. 2,100 calories, 180g protein, zero meal prep stress. Every recipe takes under 25 minutes." Post to your main social platform (Instagram, Facebook, or wherever your clients follow you).
2. **Share a nutrition tip for your niche.** Write 3-5 sentences solving a real problem your target audience has with food. Examples:
   - "Most of my clients think they need to eat less to lose weight. What they actually need is to eat MORE of the right things. When I build a meal plan, the first thing most people say is 'Wait, I get to eat all this?' Yes. That's the point."
   - "The number one reason meal plans fail isn't willpower. It's that the recipes suck. If you wouldn't order it at a restaurant, why would you eat it every Tuesday for 12 weeks?"
3. **Post a behind-the-scenes of you building a meal plan.** Record your screen or take a photo of your EvoFit Meals dashboard while building a plan. Caption: "Building a custom meal plan for a new client. Every plan gets tailored to their goals, food preferences, and schedule. This is what personal nutrition coaching looks like."

**Checkpoint:** 3 pieces of nutrition-related content are live on at least one platform. You've started shifting your audience's perception from "trainer" to "trainer who handles nutrition."

## Day 5: Warm Outreach — Your Current Clients

**Goal:** Tell your existing training clients that you now offer nutrition and meal planning services.

**Why this matters:** Your fastest path to nutrition revenue is the people already paying you for training. They trust you. They're already invested in their results. And most of them are frustrated by the nutrition side — they just haven't told you because they didn't know you could help. This is the warmest audience you'll ever have. Start here.

**Actions:**

1. Make a list of every current training client. Next to each name, note whether they've ever asked about food, complained about their diet, or mentioned struggling with nutrition. Those are your A-list prospects.
2. Send your A-list clients (the ones who've mentioned nutrition) a personal message. Use this template:

   > "Hey [Name], I've been thinking about something. You've been crushing it in our sessions, but I know the nutrition side has been frustrating for you — you mentioned [specific thing they said about food/diet]. I've started offering custom meal planning as part of my coaching. I build full weekly meal plans with recipes, grocery lists, and macro breakdowns, all tailored to your goals. I think it could be the missing piece for you. Would you be interested in hearing more about it? No pressure — just wanted to put it on your radar."

3. Send your B-list clients (everyone else) a shorter version:

   > "Hey [Name], quick heads up — I'm now offering custom nutrition and meal planning services alongside training. Full weekly meal plans, grocery lists, the works. If nutrition is something you've been wanting to dial in, let me know and I'll fill you in on the details."

4. Send 10-15 messages today. Track who you've messaged in a simple spreadsheet or notes app.

**Checkpoint:** 10-15 existing training clients have been personally messaged about your nutrition services. Not a mass email. Not a social media post. Personal, 1-to-1 messages.

## Day 6: Warm Outreach — Your Broader Network

**Goal:** Expand your outreach beyond current clients to your broader network of people who know, like, and trust you.

**Why this matters:** Your first nutrition clients don't have to be your current training clients. They could be former clients, gym friends, people who follow you on social media, or friends and family who've been meaning to clean up their diet. The key is they already know you. Cold outreach comes later (or never, if your warm network is big enough).

**Actions:**

1. Open your phone contacts and social media followers. Identify 20-30 people who:
   - Have mentioned wanting to eat healthier or lose weight
   - Follow fitness or nutrition accounts
   - Recently had a health event (new year resolution, wedding coming up, doctor's visit, new baby)
   - Are connected to a lot of people (referral sources)
2. Send each person a personal message:

   > "Hey [Name], I'm expanding my coaching business into nutrition and meal planning. I build custom weekly meal plans — real food, not bodybuilder meals — with full grocery lists and macro breakdowns. I immediately thought of you because [personal reason]. I'm taking on a few clients at a launch rate this month. Would you be interested in learning more?"

3. Send 15-20 messages today. Keep tracking in your spreadsheet.
4. For anyone from yesterday's outreach who responded with interest, schedule a call or consultation for Day 8-9.

**Checkpoint:** 25-35 total outreach messages sent across Days 5-6. At least 3-5 people have expressed interest or asked for more details.

## Day 7: Build Your Nutrition Consultation Framework

**Goal:** Create a structured consultation process so you can confidently walk a prospect from "I'm interested" to "I'm in."

**Why this matters:** Winging a nutrition consultation is how trainers give away free advice and never close the sale. A structured framework ensures every minute of the conversation builds value and moves toward a decision. The consultation isn't a free coaching session — it's a demonstration of what working with you is like.

**Actions:**

1. **Build your 30-minute nutrition consultation framework:**
   - **Minutes 0-8: Their Nutrition Story.** Ask: "Walk me through a typical day of eating. What does breakfast, lunch, dinner, and snacks look like right now?" Then: "What's the biggest frustration you have with food or nutrition right now?" Listen. Don't fix yet.
   - **Minutes 8-15: The Gap Analysis.** Based on what they told you, identify 2-3 clear gaps between where they are and where they want to be. Be specific: "You're eating about 1,400 calories and wondering why you have no energy in the gym. You need closer to 2,000 to support your training and still lose body fat. That's the kind of adjustment we'd make."
   - **Minutes 15-22: Show the Solution.** Pull up one of your sample meal plans from Day 3. Walk them through a full day: "Here's what Monday would look like for you. Breakfast is [meal], around [calories]. Lunch is [meal]. You'd get the full week plus a grocery list. And I adjust it every week based on your progress."
   - **Minutes 22-28: The Recommendation.** "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd recommend..." Present your Monthly Nutrition Coaching package. Explain what's included, how often you adjust their plan, and what results they can expect in 4-8 weeks. State the price clearly.
   - **Minutes 28-30: The Ask.** "Would you like to get started?" Then be quiet. Let them respond.
2. Practice this framework out loud at least once. Time yourself.
3. Prepare a "consultation prep" message to send prospects before the call: "Looking forward to our chat! Before we talk, it'd be helpful if you could jot down what you typically eat in a day (just a rough outline) and your top nutrition goal. That way we can make the most of our time."

**Checkpoint:** You have a written consultation outline and you've rehearsed it. You have a prep message ready to send to booked prospects.

## Day 8: Run Your First Nutrition Consultations

**Goal:** Complete your first 1-2 nutrition consultations and make your first offers.

**Why this matters:** This is where theory becomes revenue. Everything you've done for the last 7 days — the niche, the pricing, the sample plans, the outreach — has been building toward this moment. You're going to have a real conversation with a real person about nutrition and ask them to pay you for it. The first one is the hardest. After that, it gets easier fast.

**Actions:**

1. Send each booked prospect the consultation prep message from Day 7.
2. Before the call, pull up one of your sample meal plans that's closest to what this prospect would need. Have it ready to screen-share or show on your phone.
3. Run the consultation using your Day 7 framework. Stay present. Listen more than you talk. When you show the sample plan, watch their face — if their eyes light up, that's your buying signal.
4. At the recommendation stage, present your Monthly Nutrition Coaching package clearly:
   - "Here's what I recommend: my Monthly Nutrition Coaching. Each week you get a fully customized meal plan through the app — every meal, every snack, with a complete grocery list. I adjust it based on your feedback and progress. You also get direct messaging access to me for any questions between check-ins. The investment is [your monthly price]. Most clients see meaningful changes in the first 2-3 weeks."
5. **If they say yes:** Set them up in EvoFit Meals immediately. Collect payment. Book their onboarding call (Day 10).
6. **If they say "let me think about it":** "Totally understand. I have [X] spots open this month. When would be a good time for me to follow up so I can make sure you get one?"
7. **If they say no:** "No problem. Can I ask — is it the price, the timing, or something else? I want to make sure I'm offering what people actually need."

**Checkpoint:** 1-2 consultations completed. At least 1 offer made. Win or lose, you've done the hardest part — you've asked someone to pay you for nutrition coaching.

## Day 9: Follow Up With Your Entire Pipeline

**Goal:** Contact every person who's shown interest and move them toward a decision.

**Why this matters:** Most nutrition coaching sales happen on the follow-up, not the first conversation. The people who said "maybe" on Day 5-6 aren't uninterested — they're busy. The people who said "let me think about it" after a consultation aren't saying no — they're processing. Your job today is to make it easy for them to say yes.

**Actions:**

1. **Hot leads** (said "interested" but haven't booked a consultation): Send a specific time. "Hey [Name], I've got a 15-minute window on [Tuesday at 7pm / Saturday morning]. Want to jump on a quick call so I can show you exactly what the meal plans look like?"
2. **Consultation follow-ups** (completed consultation but didn't sign up): "Hey [Name], I really enjoyed our conversation. I've been thinking about your [specific goal they mentioned] and I'm confident we can make a big dent in it. I'm holding a spot for you through [date 3 days out]. Let me know if you'd like to get started."
3. **Warm leads** (haven't responded to Day 5-6 messages): One casual bump. "Hey [Name], just floating this back up — no pressure. The nutrition coaching offer is still on the table if the timing works for you."
4. **Referral requests:** For anyone who said "not for me, but..." — follow up: "Did you get a chance to mention it to [person they referenced]? Happy to reach out directly if you want to make an intro."
5. Log every interaction. Update your spreadsheet with statuses: Booked, Interested, Follow Up Next Week, Not Interested.

**Checkpoint:** Every lead in your pipeline has been contacted within the last 48 hours. No prospect is sitting in silence. You have a clear picture of who's hot, warm, and cold.

## Day 10: Close and Onboard Your First Nutrition Clients

**Goal:** Convert your remaining hot leads and deliver an exceptional onboarding experience for signed clients.

**Why this matters:** First impressions in nutrition coaching are everything. A client who receives a polished, professional meal plan within 24 hours of signing up thinks "This is exactly what I needed." A client who waits 5 days for a plan thinks "I could have just Googled this." Your onboarding speed and quality determine whether they stay past month one.

**Actions:**

1. Run any remaining consultations from your pipeline. By now, you should be comfortable with the framework. Focus on the prospect's specific situation — reference details from their messages.
2. For every client who has signed up, complete their onboarding in EvoFit Meals:
   - Send them the dietary questionnaire from your Nutrition Client Onboarding Pack (food preferences, allergies, cooking skill, time constraints, household size)
   - Once you receive their responses, build their first custom 7-day meal plan using the Meal Plan Builder. Use the recipe library — filter by their preferences and calorie targets
   - Include the auto-generated grocery list
   - Send a welcome message: "Hey [Name], your first meal plan is live! You'll find it in your dashboard — full week of meals with everything broken down. Grocery list is attached. Take a look and let me know if anything needs adjusting before you start. I'm here for any questions."
3. Schedule their first weekly check-in (7 days from plan delivery). Put it in your calendar.

**Checkpoint:** Every signed client has received their first custom meal plan within 24 hours of completing the questionnaire. Welcome messages sent. First check-ins scheduled.

## Day 11: Deliver Value and Gather Proof

**Goal:** Check in with your nutrition clients early and start collecting social proof for your marketing.

**Why this matters:** The biggest mistake trainers make with nutrition coaching is going silent between weekly check-ins. A mid-week touchpoint shows you're paying attention and gives the client a chance to flag issues before they become frustrations. It also gives you material for testimonials and content — the fuel for your next round of clients.

**Actions:**

1. Send each nutrition client a mid-week check-in message (3-4 days after they started the plan):
   - "Hey [Name], how's the first few days going? Have you tried [specific recipe from their plan] yet? That one's a favorite with my clients. Let me know if anything needs tweaking — I can swap out any meals you're not loving."
2. Pay attention to positive responses. When a client says something like "I actually looked forward to lunch today" or "This is the first time I haven't felt starving on a plan" — ask if you can share that (anonymously or with their name).
3. If a client is struggling with a recipe or ingredient, swap it immediately in EvoFit Meals. Quick responsiveness builds trust.
4. For training clients who haven't signed up for nutrition: mention it casually at the end of a session. "By the way, [Client Name] just started on one of my meal plans and she's already loving it. If you ever want to dial in the nutrition side, let me know." Social proof from peers is powerful.
5. Post a story or update referencing your nutrition coaching (without naming clients unless you have permission): "First meal plan delivery of the week. There's something satisfying about building a week of food that actually fits someone's life."

**Checkpoint:** Every active nutrition client has received a mid-week check-in. You have at least one piece of positive feedback you can use as social proof.

## Day 12: First Weekly Check-Ins and Plan Adjustments

**Goal:** Run your first weekly nutrition check-ins and demonstrate the ongoing value of coaching.

**Why this matters:** This is the moment that separates a one-time meal plan sale from recurring nutrition coaching revenue. When a client sees you actively adjusting their plan based on their feedback, preferences, and progress, they understand why they're paying monthly. A static PDF meal plan from the internet can't do this. You can. This is your competitive advantage.

**Actions:**

1. For each nutrition client, run a 10-15 minute weekly check-in (via call, video, or messaging — whatever works for your client):
   - "How did this week go? What meals did you love? Which ones weren't your thing?"
   - "How's your energy been? Any cravings or hunger issues?"
   - "Any changes coming up next week — travel, events, schedule shifts I should plan around?"
2. Based on their feedback, update their meal plan in EvoFit Meals for the coming week:
   - Swap out recipes they didn't enjoy
   - Adjust calories or macros if needed (e.g., they're training harder on certain days)
   - Account for any schedule changes
3. Send the updated plan with a brief note: "Updated plan is live. Swapped out [meal they didn't like] for [new option]. Kept [meal they loved] in the rotation. Grocery list is updated too."
4. Log adherence and feedback notes in their client profile. This becomes invaluable for long-term coaching and for handling the 30-day adherence drop-off.

**Checkpoint:** All active nutrition clients have completed their first weekly check-in. Plans have been adjusted and re-delivered. Clients can see the tangible value of ongoing coaching.

## Day 13: Activate Your Referral Engine

**Goal:** Ask your current nutrition clients for referrals and set up a system to keep referrals flowing.

**Why this matters:** A nutrition client who's seeing results and enjoying their meal plans is the most powerful marketing asset you have. Their recommendation carries more weight than any social media post you could make. But referrals don't happen automatically — you have to create the moment and make the ask. Most trainers never ask. That's a mistake.

**Actions:**

1. After a check-in or at the end of a positive interaction, ask each nutrition client directly:
   - "Hey [Name], I'm really glad the meal plans are working for you. Quick question — do you know anyone else who's been struggling with nutrition or wanting to get their diet on track? I have a few spots open this month and I'd love to work with people you recommend."
2. Make it frictionless: "If someone comes to mind, just give them my number or I can send you a link to share. I'll take great care of them."
3. Optional incentive: "If someone you refer signs up, I'll add a free week to your nutrition coaching. Just my way of saying thanks."
4. Update your social content. By now you should have:
   - Sample meal plan visuals
   - At least one client testimonial or positive feedback quote
   - Your own experience building and delivering plans
     Post something that combines proof with a soft call to action: "Just wrapped up meal plan adjustments for the week. Love hearing clients say 'I actually enjoy eating now.' If you've been wanting to get your nutrition dialed in, DM me — I have a few spots open."
5. For every new referral lead that comes in, run the same outreach message from Day 6 but reference the mutual connection.

**Checkpoint:** You've asked at least 2 clients for referrals. You have a repeatable referral ask that feels natural. At least one new referral lead is in your pipeline.

## A Note on Scope of Practice

Throughout this guide, we've focused on general nutrition coaching and meal planning — helping clients eat well to support their fitness goals. This is well within the scope of a qualified personal trainer or fitness coach.

However, there are clear lines. If a client presents with or asks about:

- Medical conditions requiring therapeutic dietary intervention (diabetes, kidney disease, heart disease)
- Eating disorders or disordered eating behaviors
- Pregnancy or postpartum nutrition with medical complications
- Complex food allergies requiring clinical management
- Any condition where dietary changes could interact with medication

**Refer them to a Registered Dietitian or licensed nutrition professional.** You can (and should) continue to work alongside an RD as part of the client's team. This doesn't mean you lose the client — it means you're the kind of coach who takes their health seriously. That builds trust. That earns referrals.
