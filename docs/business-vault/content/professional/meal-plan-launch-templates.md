## Launch 1: The Nutrition Challenge

### What It Is

A time-bound (usually 21 or 28-day), structured nutrition program that a group of people follows simultaneously. Participants get daily meal plans, group accountability, coaching touchpoints, and a clear outcome goal. Challenges are the single best client acquisition tool in nutrition coaching because they create urgency, community, and visible results.

### Challenge Parameters

| Parameter  | Recommendation                                                                                                |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Duration   | 21 or 28 days (long enough for results, short enough to feel achievable)                                      |
| Group size | 8-20 participants (enough for community, small enough for personal attention)                                 |
| Price      | $97-197 per person (perceived as high value relative to ongoing coaching)                                     |
| Platform   | Private Facebook group, WhatsApp group, or Slack channel for daily interaction                                |
| Goal       | Specific and measurable: "Lose 5-8 lbs," "Hit your protein target every day," "Complete 21 days of meal prep" |

## Launch 3: Group Nutrition Program

### What It Is

An ongoing (usually 8 or 12-week) group nutrition coaching program. Unlike a challenge (which is short and intense), a group program is longer, more structured, and designed for lasting transformation. It combines individual meal plans with group coaching calls, creating a hybrid model that's profitable (you coach multiple people at once) and effective (group accountability works).

### Program Parameters

| Parameter            | Recommendation                                                     |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Duration             | 8 or 12 weeks                                                      |
| Group size           | 6-12 participants                                                  |
| Price                | $297-497 per person (8-week) or $397-697 per person (12-week)      |
| Coaching calls       | Weekly group call (45-60 min via Zoom)                             |
| Individual component | Personalized meal plan for each participant, built in EvoFit Meals |
| Community            | Private group for daily interaction                                |

## Announcement Sequences for Existing Clients

### When to Use These

Use announcement sequences when you're launching something new to your existing client base — a new service tier, a seasonal promotion, a group program, or a special offer. Existing clients are your warmest audience and should always hear about launches first.

### Sequence 1: New Service Tier Announcement

**Message 1 (DM or email):**

> "Hey [Name], quick heads up — I'm adding a new option to my nutrition services that I think you'll be interested in. [Brief description of new tier/service]. Since you're an existing client, you get first access before I open it up publicly. Want the details?"

**Message 2 (if they express interest):**

> "Here's the full breakdown: [description, pricing, what's included]. For existing clients, I'm offering [special rate / priority enrollment / bonus]. This goes live to the public on [date]. If you want in, just let me know by [date - 2 days]."

### Sequence 2: Seasonal Menu Refresh Announcement

**Message 1:**

> "[Name], heads up — I'm refreshing your meal plan with seasonal recipes for [season]. You'll see some new dishes in your next plan. Any specific requests or foods you've been craving? I want to make sure the new rotation hits the mark for you."

### Sequence 3: Referral-Driven Launch Announcement

**Message 1:**

> "[Name], I'm launching a new [challenge/program/service] and I want to tell you first. [One sentence about what it is]. I'm also offering a referral bonus: for every person you refer who signs up, you get [incentive]. Know anyone who's been wanting to get their nutrition sorted?"
