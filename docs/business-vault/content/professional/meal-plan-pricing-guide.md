# Meal Plan Pricing & Packaging Guide

## How to Price Nutrition Coaching, Bundle with Training, and Create Recurring Revenue

---

### Before You Read This

Most trainers who add nutrition services either undercharge dramatically (because they feel like meal plans "aren't worth that much") or price randomly (because they copied someone on Instagram without understanding the math). Both lead to the same place: frustration, burnout, and eventually dropping the service.

Pricing nutrition is different from pricing training sessions. Sessions are time-bound — you trade an hour for money. Nutrition services deliver ongoing value without requiring your physical presence every time. That changes the math and it should change how you think about pricing.

This guide walks you through the calculation, gives you regional benchmarks, provides three ready-to-use package templates, and teaches you the pricing psychology that makes clients choose the option you want them to choose.

## Section 1: The Pricing Calculator

### Step 1: Know Your Time Investment Per Client

Before you set a price, you need to know what each nutrition client actually costs you in time.

**Initial setup (first week):**

| Task                                   | Time           |
| -------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| Review dietary questionnaire and forms | 15-20 min      |
| Calculate macros and calorie targets   | 10-15 min      |
| Build meal plan in EvoFit Meals        | 15-30 min      |
| Review and customize plan              | 10-15 min      |
| Deliver plan and explain it to client  | 15-20 min      |
| **Total initial setup**                | **65-100 min** |

**Monthly ongoing:**

| Task                                     | Time           |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| Plan updates/adjustments (2-4 per month) | 20-40 min      |
| Monthly check-in call                    | 20-30 min      |
| Responding to questions/messages         | 15-30 min      |
| Progress review and macro adjustment     | 10-15 min      |
| **Total monthly maintenance**            | **65-115 min** |

So a nutrition client costs you roughly 1-2 hours per month after the initial setup. That's the denominator in your pricing equation.

### Step 2: Calculate Your Minimum Hourly Value

You already know your training session rate. Use that as your benchmark.

**If your training rate is $80/session (60 minutes):** Your minimum hourly value is $80.

**If your nutrition work takes 1.5 hours/month per client:** Your minimum nutrition price is $80 x 1.5 = **$120/month**.

That's your floor. Go below it and you're earning less per hour for nutrition work than for training — which means every hour spent on nutrition is a hour you could have earned more training someone.

### Step 3: Add Your Value Premium

Your floor is based on time. But nutrition services deliver value beyond your time:

- **The plan itself has intellectual value.** You calculated macros, selected recipes, built a structure. That knowledge and customization is worth something independent of the minutes it took.
- **Convenience value.** The client doesn't have to research, plan, shop, or figure anything out. You've eliminated hours of their weekly decision-making.
- **Outcome value.** Better nutrition leads to better results, which leads to longer client retention, which leads to more training revenue. The nutrition service makes your training service more valuable.

Add 25-50% above your floor to account for this value.

**Using the example above:** $120 floor x 1.35 = **$162/month** -> round to **$165 or $175/month**

### Step 4: Validate Against Your Market

Before finalising, check what others in your area and niche are charging for nutrition services. Here's how:

1. Search "[your city] nutrition coaching" and note the top 5 prices
2. Check what online nutrition coaches in your niche charge
3. Ask in trainer communities what others charge for meal plans
4. Look at nutrition apps and meal plan delivery services for the "automated" baseline

Your price should be above the automated services (which charge $10-30/month) and competitive with local nutrition coaching (which ranges widely).

## Section 3: Three Package Templates

### Package 1: The One-Off Meal Plan

**What it is:** A single, customized meal plan delivered as a one-time purchase. No ongoing coaching, no monthly check-ins. The client gets their plan and runs with it.

**Best for:** Clients on a budget, clients who want to "try before they commit," or as a lead-in product for your monthly service.

**What's included:**

- Initial questionnaire and preference review
- 7-day customized meal plan (built in EvoFit Meals)
- Full recipes with portions and macro breakdowns
- Consolidated shopping list
- Meal prep guide for the week
- One round of revisions within 7 days

**What's NOT included:**

- Ongoing plan updates
- Monthly check-ins
- Message support
- Macro adjustments based on progress

**Pricing formula:** Your hourly rate x 2 hours (average time to build and deliver)

**Example pricing:**

- Small market: $100 - $150
- Mid market: $150 - $250
- Major metro: $200 - $350

**How to position it:**

> "This is a great starting point if you want to see what a customized plan looks like for your goals. You'll get a full week of meals, all the recipes, and your shopping list. It's designed to be something you can follow immediately. If you love it and want ongoing plans and coaching, we can upgrade to my monthly service anytime."

**The strategic play:** The one-off plan is your gateway drug. Price it low enough that it feels like an easy yes, but deliver it at a quality that makes them want the monthly service. 40-60% of one-off plan clients upgrade within 30 days if the plan is good.

---

### Package 2: Monthly Nutrition Service

**What it is:** Ongoing customized meal plans with regular updates and a monthly check-in. This is your bread-and-butter nutrition product.

**Best for:** Clients who want structured nutrition support with accountability. This is what most clients actually need.

**What's included:**

- Initial questionnaire, preference survey, and allergy review
- Bi-weekly customized meal plans (new plan every 2 weeks)
- Full recipes, macros, and shopping lists with every plan
- One 30-minute monthly nutrition check-in (video, phone, or in-person)
- Messaging support for quick questions (24-hour response time)
- Macro adjustments based on progress
- Plan swaps and modifications as needed

**Pricing formula:** Your hourly rate x 1.5 hours/month x 1.35 value premium

**Example pricing:**

- Small market: $100 - $175/month
- Mid market: $150 - $275/month
- Major metro: $200 - $400/month

**Billing:** Monthly recurring. Require 30-day notice for cancellation.

**How to position it:**

> "This is my core nutrition service and what I recommend for anyone serious about their goals. You'll get a fresh plan every two weeks so you're never bored, we check in every month to review progress and adjust your targets, and you can message me anytime with questions. It's like having a nutrition coach in your pocket."

**Retention strategy:** The monthly check-in is your retention engine. It's where you celebrate progress, catch issues early, and reinforce the value of the service. Never skip it. Even a quick 15-minute check-in is better than nothing.

---

### Package 3: Training + Nutrition Bundle

**What it is:** Your training package plus integrated nutrition coaching. One price, one relationship, comprehensive results.

**Best for:** Existing training clients (the upsell) and new clients who want the full package from day one.

**What's included (nutrition portion):**

- Everything in the Monthly Nutrition Service
- Plus: nutrition plan is synced with training program (e.g., higher carbs on training days, adjusted macros during deload weeks)
- Plus: nutrition check-in is integrated into training sessions (no separate appointment needed)
- Plus: performance-focused meal timing recommendations around training sessions

**Pricing formula:** Training package price + nutrition add-on (60-80% of standalone nutrition price)

**Why discount the bundle?** The bundle discount is justified because:

1. The check-in happens during training time (no additional appointment)
2. You already have the client's data from training
3. The administrative overhead is lower
4. The bundle increases retention, which increases lifetime value

**Example pricing:**

| Component                 | Standalone         | Bundled                   |
| ------------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------- |
| 12-session training pack  | $1,020             | $1,020                    |
| Monthly nutrition service | $175/month         | $125/month (bundled rate) |
| **Bundle total**          | $1,195 first month | **$1,145 first month**    |

The saving looks modest on paper, but the per-session training rate stays intact and the nutrition add-on feels like an easy yes at the discounted rate.

**How to position it:**

> "Here's what I recommend: the 12-session training pack with my nutrition service built in. That way your training and your eating are completely synced — your meal plan adjusts based on your training cycle, you get higher-carb days when we're pushing hard and recovery-focused nutrition when we're deloading. It's the difference between training in isolation and having a fully coordinated program. The bundle rate for nutrition is $[X]/month instead of $[X] standalone."

## Section 4: Pricing Psychology

### The Anchor Effect

When you present three options, the client doesn't evaluate each one independently. They compare them. This is called anchoring, and it's the most powerful pricing tool you have.

**How to use it:**

Always present your packages in this order:

1. **One-off plan** (lowest price, least value)
2. **Monthly service** (middle price, best value for most clients) — **this is the one you want them to buy**
3. **Training + nutrition bundle** (highest price, maximum value)

The one-off plan makes the monthly service look like a deal. The bundle makes the monthly service feel accessible. Most clients land on the middle option — which is exactly where you want them.

**Never present just one option.** A single option creates a yes/no decision. Three options create a "which one?" decision. "Which one?" converts at a dramatically higher rate.

### The "Anchor High" Strategy

Start every pricing conversation by mentioning the bundle first.

> "My most comprehensive option is the training + nutrition bundle at $[highest price]. That includes everything — your training sessions, a fully customized meal plan, and integrated nutrition coaching. For clients who want nutrition only, my monthly service is $[middle price], which includes bi-weekly plans, a monthly check-in, and messaging support. And if you just want to try a single custom plan, I do those for $[lowest price]."

By stating the highest price first, you've anchored the client's perception. Everything after that sounds more affordable by comparison. The monthly service — your target product — now feels like a reasonable investment rather than an expense.

### Price Presentation Rules

**Rule 1: Always quote the package price, not the per-meal price.**
"$175/month" is how you present it. Not "$5.83 per day" or "$0.97 per meal." Per-meal pricing commoditises your service and invites comparison with grocery costs.

**Rule 2: State the price once, confidently, and move immediately to value.**
"The investment is $175 per month. That includes your customized plan every two weeks, all the recipes and shopping lists, a monthly check-in with me, and messaging support. Based on your goal of [X], I think we'll start seeing real changes within the first 3 weeks."

**Rule 3: Never apologize for your price.**
Don't say "I know it's a lot" or "I understand it's an investment." Those phrases signal that you think your price is too high. If you think it's too high, the client will too.

**Rule 4: Use "investment," not "cost."**
"The investment is $175/month" frames the money as something that produces a return. "The cost is $175/month" frames it as an expense. Words matter.

### The Founding Member Play

When you're launching your nutrition service for the first time, a founding member offer creates urgency and rewards early adopters.

**How it works:**

> "I'm launching my nutrition service this month and I'm looking for 5 founding members. Founding members lock in the rate of $[X]/month — that's $[Y] less than my standard rate. The founding rate is locked in for as long as you stay on the service. Once I fill the 5 spots, the rate goes to $[standard price]. Interested?"

**Rules for founding member pricing:**

- Discount no more than 20-25% off your standard rate
- Cap it at 5-10 spots (scarcity drives action)
- Set a deadline (2-3 weeks)
- Honour the locked rate genuinely — don't raise it on them later
- Use the founding members for testimonials and social proof

## Section 5: Recurring Revenue Architecture

### Why Recurring Beats One-Off

One-off meal plans are good for lead generation. Monthly services are good for building a business. Here's the math:

**One-off model:**

- You sell 4 plans per month at $150 each = $600/month
- You need to find 4 new customers every month
- No compounding — if you stop selling, revenue stops

**Monthly model:**

- Month 1: 4 clients at $150/month = $600
- Month 2: 4 existing + 3 new = 7 clients = $1,050
- Month 3: 7 existing + 3 new = 10 clients = $1,500
- Month 6: ~18 clients (assuming 85% retention) = $2,700
- Month 12: ~28 clients = $4,200

Monthly recurring revenue compounds. Each new client you add builds on the base you already have. After 6-12 months, you can stop actively selling and still have a substantial revenue stream.

### The Retention Equation

Recurring revenue only works if clients stay. The industry average for nutrition coaching retention is 3-4 months. Your target should be 6+ months.

**What drives retention:**

| Factor                                 | Impact                          |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Regular check-ins                      | +40% retention vs. no check-ins |
| Visible results within 30 days         | +50% retention                  |
| Plan variety (new recipes regularly)   | +25% retention                  |
| Responsive communication               | +30% retention                  |
| Training integration (bundled clients) | +60% retention                  |

The single biggest retention lever is the monthly check-in. Clients who feel seen and supported stay. Clients who receive a plan and hear nothing for a month leave.

### Building Your Recurring Revenue Dashboard

Track these monthly:

| Metric                            | Formula                                     | Target                   |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)   | Sum of all active monthly nutrition clients | Growing month-over-month |
| Client count                      | Total active nutrition clients              | Growing                  |
| Average Revenue Per Client (ARPC) | MRR / client count                          | $125+                    |
| Churn rate                        | Clients lost / clients at start of month    | Under 15%                |
| Net new clients                   | New clients - churned clients               | Positive                 |
| Lifetime Value (LTV)              | ARPC x average client tenure (months)       | $750+                    |

**The magic number:** When your monthly nutrition churn drops below 10%, your base is growing almost entirely from new client additions. That's when the compounding effect really kicks in.

### Upsell Paths Within Nutrition

Once a client is on your nutrition service, there are natural upsell opportunities:

1. **One-off plan -> Monthly service** (40-60% conversion rate if the plan is good)
2. **Monthly plan -> Plan + coaching** (add more check-ins or accountability features)
3. **Monthly plan -> Training + nutrition bundle** (the ultimate upsell)
4. **Monthly service -> Intensive program** (8-week or 12-week transformation challenge at a premium rate)

Each upsell increases your revenue per client without requiring a new sale.

### Example Custom Packages

**The "Busy Executive" Package:**

- Bi-weekly meal plans with no-cook and quick-cook options only
- Travel-day frameworks included in every plan
- Messaging support (priority response within 4 hours)
- Monthly 20-minute phone check-in (not video — they're always in transit)
- Restaurant dining guide for their top 5 venues
- Price: $250-350/month

**The "Competition Prep" Package:**

- Weekly meal plans with precise macro splits by training day
- Weekly 30-minute check-ins with photo review
- Macro adjustments every 2 weeks (or as needed during peak week)
- Supplement guidance aligned with prep phases
- Water and sodium manipulation protocol (final 2 weeks)
- Price: $400-600/month (premium, time-limited)

**The "New Mom" Package:**

- Bi-weekly meal plans optimized for recovery, energy, and (optionally) breastfeeding nutrition
- Family-friendly recipes that work for the whole household
- Quick-prep focus (nothing over 20 minutes)
- Flexible structure — fewer rigid meals, more "choose from this list" options
- Monthly check-in with flexibility for rescheduling
- Price: $150-225/month

**The "Group Nutrition" Package (4-8 people):**

- Individualized meal plans for each participant
- Weekly group coaching call (45 min via Zoom)
- Shared recipe library and meal prep tips
- Group accountability challenges
- Individual messaging support
- Price: $75-125/month per person (lower per-client, higher total revenue)

### How to Assemble a New Package in 10 Minutes

1. Identify the client segment (who is this for?)
2. Identify their primary constraint (time, budget, complexity, medical, family)
3. Select building blocks from the table that address the constraint
4. Calculate your time investment per month
5. Apply your hourly rate + value premium
6. Name the package (make it sound like a solution, not a list of features)
7. Write a one-sentence positioning statement

## Section 8: Upsell Scripts

### The Plan-to-Coaching Upsell

_Use this when a one-off plan client is nearing the end of their plan (or has been using it for 2-3 weeks)._

>
> [Let them respond.]

## Section 9: Subscription Pricing Models with Retention Math

### Why Subscription Models Win

The economics of subscription nutrition coaching are dramatically better than one-off plans or even month-to-month billing. Here's why:

**Month-to-Month Model:**

- Client can cancel anytime
- Average retention: 3.2 months
- Lifetime value: $175/month x 3.2 months = $560
- Acquisition cost to replace: $50-100 (time, marketing, consultations)

**3-Month Commitment Model:**

- Client commits for 3 months upfront (with a small discount)
- Average retention: 5.1 months (they often continue past the initial commitment)
- Lifetime value: $165/month x 5.1 months = $842
- 50% higher LTV than month-to-month

**6-Month Commitment Model:**

- Client commits for 6 months (with a moderate discount)
- Average retention: 7.8 months
- Lifetime value: $150/month x 7.8 months = $1,170
- 109% higher LTV than month-to-month

### The Commitment Pricing Ladder

| Plan           | Monthly Rate | Discount | Total Commitment | Avg. Retention | Expected LTV |
| -------------- | ------------ | -------- | ---------------- | -------------- | ------------ |
| Month-to-Month | $175         | None     | $175             | 3.2 months     | $560         |
| 3-Month Plan   | $165         | 6% off   | $495 upfront     | 5.1 months     | $842         |
| 6-Month Plan   | $150         | 14% off  | $900 upfront     | 7.8 months     | $1,170       |
| 12-Month Plan  | $135         | 23% off  | $1,620 upfront   | 11.2 months    | $1,512       |

**The strategy:** Offer all four. Present the 3-Month Plan as the recommended option. The month-to-month option exists for people who refuse to commit, but its higher price incentivizes the longer plans. The 12-Month Plan is for your most committed clients — and it generates the most cash flow upfront.

### How to Present Subscription Plans

> "I offer a few different commitment levels. Let me walk you through them:"
>
> "Month-to-month is $175 — total flexibility, cancel anytime."
>
> "The plan I recommend for most clients is the 3-month commitment at $165/month. You save a bit, but more importantly, 3 months is the timeframe where real, visible changes happen. It takes about 66 days for nutrition habits to become automatic, so 3 months gives us enough runway to build habits that stick."
>
> "If you know this is a long-term commitment, the 6-month plan at $150/month gives you the best value and the best results. Most of my clients who do 6 months end up continuing because by that point, they can't imagine going back to unstructured eating."
>
> "Which timeframe feels right for where you are?"

### Retention Math: When to Offer Discounts vs. When to Hold Price

**Hold your price when:**

- The client is in their first 3 months (they haven't experienced enough value to evaluate fairly)
- The client is seeing strong results (they're getting what they paid for)
- The client hasn't raised budget concerns (don't solve a problem they don't have)

**Offer a retention discount when:**

- A client mentions cancelling due to budget (not dissatisfaction)
- A client has been with you 6+ months and is considering leaving
- You're approaching a renewal date and want to lock them in for another term

**The retention discount script:**

> "I don't want to lose you, and I know the budget side is real. Here's what I can do: if you commit to another [3/6] months, I'll bring your rate down to $[X]/month. That's my way of rewarding loyalty. But I can only offer this at the time of renewal — I can't retroactively discount. Does that work for you?"

## Section 6 (Original): Common Pricing Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Pricing Based on How Long the Plan Takes to Generate

With EvoFit Meals, you can generate a complete meal plan in minutes. That doesn't mean the plan is worth "5 minutes of work." The value is in the customization, the expertise behind the macro calculations, the food selection, and the time the client saves. Price on value, not on production time.

### Mistake 2: Matching App Prices

Meal planning apps charge $10-30/month for generic plans. You're not an app. You're a human who knows this specific client, their goals, their training program, and their life. The comparison is irrelevant. Don't address it unless the client brings it up — and if they do, the response is simple: "An app gives you a template. I give you a plan built specifically for you, adjusted based on your progress, with a real person you can ask questions to. They're different products."

### Mistake 3: Free Nutrition "Included" with Training

Some trainers throw in nutrition advice for free as a training perk. This devalues the service, creates an expectation that nutrition help should be free, and makes it nearly impossible to charge for it later. If you want to include basic nutrition tips in training — fine. But a structured meal plan service should always be a separate, paid offering.

### Mistake 4: Annual Discounts That Kill Cash Flow

Offering a big annual discount (e.g., "$150/month or $1,200/year — save $600!") sounds generous, but it tanks your monthly cash flow and locks you into a rate for 12 months. If you want to incentivize commitment, offer a modest 3-month or 6-month discount (10-15%) instead.

### Mistake 5: Not Raising Prices

Your first price is your launch price, not your forever price. Review and raise prices every 12 months as you add clients, collect testimonials, and improve your service. Existing clients get 30 days notice and a loyalty lock-in option. New clients pay the new rate immediately.
