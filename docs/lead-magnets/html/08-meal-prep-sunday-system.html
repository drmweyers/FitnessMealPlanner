The System

The 2-Hour Timeline

Follow this exact minute-by-minute schedule. Every step is designed to run in parallel so nothing sits idle.

Minutes 0 – 15
Start Grains + Marinate Proteins
Put rice and/or quinoa in the rice cooker. Season chicken breasts and place in marinade. Start boiling water for eggs. These run passively while you work on the next steps.
Minutes 15 – 30
Prep All Vegetables
Wash, chop, and portion all vegetables for the week. Spread on sheet pans with olive oil and seasoning. Slide into the oven at 400°F. Prep overnight oats base in jars.
Minutes 30 – 60
Cook All Proteins
Cook marinated chicken on the stovetop or grill. Bake any other proteins (fish, turkey). Remove eggs from boiling water and ice bath. Slice and portion all proteins once cooked.
Minutes 60 – 90
Assemble Containers
Build each meal: protein + grain + vegetables. Add different sauces and seasonings to each container to create variety. Prepare overnight oats with toppings in separate jars.
Minutes 90 – 120
Cool, Label + Store
Let containers cool to room temperature (10–15 minutes). Label each with the meal name and day. Stack fridge meals (Days 1–3) on top shelf. Freeze remaining meals (Days 4–7) in freezer bags.
💡 Pro Tip: Batch Your Tasks
The secret is parallel processing. While grains cook passively, you're chopping vegetables. While proteins sear, you're assembling containers. Never stand idle.

The Recipes

5 Base Recipes That Scale

Master these five recipes and you can create endless meal combinations. Each yields 4 servings and scales easily.

🍗 Chicken Breast 5 Ways
Season 4 breasts (6 oz each) with different rubs: lemon herb, cajun, garlic parm, teriyaki, or Mexican spice. Bake at 400°F for 22–25 min. Rest 5 min, slice.
280 cal 42g P 2g C 8g F
🍚 Rice & Quinoa Base
Cook 2 cups dry rice and 1 cup dry quinoa in rice cooker. Fluff with fork, mix together. Season with salt and a squeeze of lime for brightness.
210 cal 6g P 42g C 2g F
🥦 Roasted Vegetables
Chop broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini, and sweet potato. Toss with 1 tbsp olive oil, garlic powder, salt, pepper. Roast at 400°F for 25–30 min.
120 cal 3g P 18g C 4g F
🥚 Hard-Boiled Eggs
Place 12 eggs in pot, cover with cold water. Bring to boil, cover, remove from heat. Let sit 12 min. Ice bath for 5 min. Peel and store. 3 eggs per serving.
210 cal 18g P 1g C 14g F
🌞 Overnight Oats (4 Jars)
Per jar: 1/2 cup rolled oats + 1/2 cup milk + 1/4 cup Greek yogurt + 1 tbsp chia seeds + 1 tbsp honey. Mix in jar, refrigerate overnight. Top with berries, banana, or peanut butter before eating.
320 cal 15g P 48g C 8g F

⚠️ Safety Tips

Cool Before Storing
Let food cool to room temperature (max 30 min) before sealing containers. Hot food in sealed containers creates moisture and bacteria.
Thaw Safely
Move frozen meals to the fridge the night before. Never thaw on the counter. Microwave defrost works in a pinch.
The Smell Test Isn't Enough
When in doubt, throw it out. Stick to the fridge life guidelines above. Label every container with the prep date.
Fridge Organization
Days 1–3 on the top shelf (eat first). Days 4–7 go straight to the freezer. This prevents waste and ensures freshness.
