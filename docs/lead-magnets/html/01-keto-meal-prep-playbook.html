Chapter One

Why Keto Works

The Science of Ketosis

When carbohydrate intake drops below 20-50g per day, the body depletes glycogen stores within 24-72 hours. The liver then begins converting fatty acids into ketone bodies — beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB), acetoacetate, and acetone — which become the primary fuel source for the brain and muscles. This metabolic state, called nutritional ketosis, fundamentally shifts how the body produces and uses energy, turning it into a fat-burning machine around the clock.

Fat-Adaptation Timeline

Week 1
Transition
Glycogen depletion, water weight drops 3-7 lbs. Possible "keto flu" symptoms.
Week 2
Adaptation
Ketone production ramps up. Energy stabilizes. Cravings start to fade.
Week 3
Optimization
Steady ketosis. Mental clarity improves. Appetite naturally decreases.
Week 4
Fat-Adapted
Efficient fat oxidation. Stable energy all day. Peak performance zone.

Net Carbs vs Total Carbs

Total Carbs − Fiber − Sugar Alcohols = Net Carbs

Net carbs are the only carbs that impact blood sugar and ketosis. Fiber passes through the digestive system without being absorbed, and most sugar alcohols (erythritol, xylitol) have minimal glycemic impact. Always read nutrition labels and calculate net carbs. Example: an avocado has 12g total carbs but only 3g net carbs after subtracting 9g of fiber. Target: 20-25g net carbs per day for reliable ketosis.

Chapter Five

Keto Meal Prep System

The Sunday 2-hour prep that sets your clients up for a week of effortless keto meals.

0:00 – 0:15  |  Setup

Organize & Preheat

Preheat oven to 400°F. Lay out all containers (get 14 glass containers with lids). Wash and dry all vegetables. Set out cutting boards for raw meat and veggies separately. Pull all proteins from the fridge to take the chill off.

0:15 – 0:45  |  Proteins

Batch Cook All Proteins

Oven: Sheet pan of chicken thighs (seasoned with salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika) + bacon strips on a separate rack. Stovetop: Brown 2 lbs ground beef with taco seasoning (no sugar). Also: Hard-boil 12 eggs in a large pot. All proteins cook simultaneously.

0:45 – 1:15  |  Vegetables & Sides

Prep Veggies & Fat Sources

Chop: 2 heads broccoli, 3 zucchini (spiralize or dice), 1 lb asparagus, 2 cups mushrooms. Roast: Broccoli and Brussels sprouts with olive oil at 400°F for 20 min. Prep: Wash and bag spinach/lettuce for salads. Slice avocados last (day-of) to prevent browning. Make a batch of guacamole with lime juice to extend freshness.

1:15 – 1:40  |  Sauces & Assembly

Build Your Containers

Make: Batch of keto ranch dressing, garlic butter sauce, and chipotle mayo. Assemble: Pair one protein + one vegetable + one fat source per container. Label each container with the day and meal. Stack in the fridge in order: Monday front, Sunday back. Each container should hit roughly 450-600 calories.

1:40 – 2:00  |  Snacks & Cleanup

Portion Snacks & Clean Up

Portion: Pre-bag 7 snack packs (1 oz nuts + cheese stick or deli meat). Fill a container with celery/cucumber sticks. Pre-portion cream cheese and almond butter into small containers. Freeze: Any extra cooked protein for the following week. Clean as you go — kitchen should be spotless by 2:00.

Container System

Use 28 oz glass containers with snap-lock lids for meals. Small 4 oz containers for sauces and dips. Silicone bags for snack portions. Glass prevents flavor transfer and reheats safely in microwave.

Storage & Freshness

Fridge (3-4 days): Cooked proteins, roasted veggies, hard-boiled eggs. Freezer (2-3 months): Extra ground beef, chicken thighs, breakfast casserole portions. Thaw overnight in fridge before eating.

Common Keto Mistakes

Five pitfalls trainers see again and again — and exactly how to fix each one.

1

Not Supplementing Electrolytes

Keto is a natural diuretic — the kidneys excrete more sodium, which pulls potassium and magnesium with it. Clients experience headaches, muscle cramps, fatigue, and dizziness in the first 1-2 weeks. Most blame the diet and quit, when the fix is simple supplementation.

Fix: 5,000mg sodium (salt food liberally + bone broth), 1,000-3,000mg potassium (avocado, spinach, supplement), 400mg magnesium glycinate before bed. This eliminates "keto flu" almost entirely.
2

Eating Too Much Protein

Clients think keto is "eat all the steak you want." But excess protein triggers gluconeogenesis — the body converts amino acids into glucose, which can spike insulin and kick them out of ketosis. This is the #1 hidden reason clients plateau after initial weight loss.

Fix: Protein should be 0.6-0.8g per pound of lean body mass. A 180 lb male at 25% body fat needs ~81-108g protein. Prioritize fattier protein cuts (thighs over breasts, ribeye over sirloin) and add fat sources to every meal.
3

Hidden Carbs Sneaking In

Sauces, condiments, and "health foods" are landmines. A single tablespoon of BBQ sauce has 6g carbs. Sugar-free products may contain maltodextrin (higher glycemic index than sugar). Even vegetables like onions and tomatoes add up fast when used generously.

Fix: Read every label for the first 2 weeks. Use the food list on Page 4. Stick to simple seasonings: salt, pepper, garlic powder, herbs. Make sauces from scratch (mayo, ranch, butter sauces) where you control every ingredient.
4

Not Tracking Food Intake

Many clients eyeball portions and assume they are in ketosis. Without tracking, most people underestimate carbs by 30-50% and overestimate fat intake. "Lazy keto" works for some veterans, but beginners need structure to learn proper portion sizes and carb counts.

Fix: Track everything in an app for the first 30 days (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, or Carb Manager). Use a food scale for the first 2 weeks. After 30 days, most clients develop an intuitive understanding and can reduce tracking to spot-checks.
5

Giving Up During Adaptation

The first 7-10 days are the hardest. Energy drops, cravings spike, and the scale may stall (or even go up temporarily from water fluctuations after the initial drop). Clients panic and revert to carbs, resetting the entire adaptation process and never reaching the fat-burning benefits.

Fix: Set expectations upfront — warn clients about the adaptation period BEFORE they start. Schedule a check-in call on Day 3 and Day 7. Celebrate non-scale victories (reduced bloating, better sleep, steady energy). Commit to a full 21-day trial minimum.
