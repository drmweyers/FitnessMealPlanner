Protein & Muscle Growth

The science-backed fundamentals every trainer should know when programming nutrition for hypertrophy clients.

📊 How Much Protein Do You Actually Need?

Research consistently supports 1.6–2.2 g of protein per kilogram of body weight per day for maximizing muscle protein synthesis. For a 180 lb (82 kg) individual, that translates to roughly 130–180 g of protein daily. Going above 2.2 g/kg shows diminishing returns for muscle growth, though it won't cause harm in healthy individuals.

  • Beginners & moderate lifters: aim for 1.6 g/kg
  • Advanced lifters in a caloric surplus: 1.8–2.0 g/kg
  • Lifters in a caloric deficit (cutting): push to 2.2 g/kg to preserve lean mass

⏱ The Post-Workout Window: Myth Debunked

The "anabolic window" — the idea that you must consume protein within 30 minutes of training — has been largely debunked by modern research. A 2013 meta-analysis by Schoenfeld et al. found that total daily protein intake matters far more than timing. The real window is roughly 4–6 hours around your workout (before and after combined).

  • Eat a protein-rich meal 2–3 hours before training
  • Follow up within 2–3 hours post-workout — no rush needed
  • Spread protein across 3–5 meals for optimal MPS signaling
  • Aim for at least 0.4 g/kg per meal (roughly 30–40 g per sitting)

🧬 Complete vs. Incomplete Proteins

Complete proteins contain all 9 essential amino acids in sufficient quantities. Animal sources (chicken, beef, fish, eggs, dairy) are inherently complete. Most plant sources are incomplete on their own but can be combined for a full amino acid profile.

  • Complete: Chicken, beef, fish, eggs, dairy, soy, quinoa
  • Incomplete (combine them): Rice + beans, peanut butter + whole wheat, lentils + grains
  • Leucine is the key trigger for MPS — aim for 2.5–3 g per meal
  • Whey protein has the highest leucine content (~11%) of any source

💡 Snacking Strategy for Muscle Gain

Space snacks 3–4 hours apart from main meals to maintain elevated muscle protein synthesis throughout the day. Each snack should deliver at least 30–40g protein to hit the leucine threshold needed to trigger MPS. Prep snacks in bulk on Sundays — clients who meal-prep snacks are 3x more likely to hit their daily protein targets.

Pro Tip: Lean Sources

Chicken breast, turkey, cod, and egg whites give you the most protein per calorie. Use these when cutting to maximize protein while staying in a deficit.

Pro Tip: Bulking Sources

Salmon, whole eggs, and bison offer protein plus healthy fats and micronutrients. Use these when in a surplus and calories are less restricted.
