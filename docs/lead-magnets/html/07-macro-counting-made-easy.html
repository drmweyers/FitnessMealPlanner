What Are Macros?

The three macronutrients that make up every calorie you eat.

"Macros" is short for macronutrients -- the three nutrients your body needs in large amounts. Every food you eat is made up of some combination of protein, carbohydrates, and fat. Understanding macros lets you control what those calories do, not just how many you eat.

🥩

Protein

4
calories per gram

Builds and repairs muscle. Highest satiety. Essential for recovery and body composition.

🍞

Carbs

4
calories per gram

Primary energy source. Fuels workouts and brain function. Stored as glycogen in muscles.

🥑

Fat

9
calories per gram

Hormone production. Vitamin absorption. Cell health. Most calorie-dense macro.

💡 Why Macros Matter More Than Calories Alone

  • 1,800 cal of protein-rich food builds muscle; 1,800 cal of sugar does not
  • Hitting protein targets preserves lean mass during fat loss phases
  • Adequate fat intake supports testosterone, estrogen, and thyroid hormones
  • Carb timing around workouts directly impacts performance and recovery
  • Two people at the same calorie level get vastly different results based on macro split

How to Calculate Macros

Three steps from body stats to a personalized macro plan.

Step 1

Calculate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Calories your body burns at complete rest. Use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation:

Men: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age - 5
Women: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age - 161
Step 2

Determine TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

Multiply BMR by your activity factor to get total calories burned per day:

Sedentary (desk job): BMR x 1.2
Light (1-3 days/wk): BMR x 1.375
Moderate (3-5 days/wk): BMR x 1.55
Active (6-7 days/wk): BMR x 1.725
Very Active (2x/day): BMR x 1.9
Step 3

Set Your Macro Split

Apply your goal-based percentages (see page 4) to your TDEE. Divide protein and carb grams by 4, fat grams by 9.

Protein grams = (TDEE x protein %) / 4
Carb grams = (TDEE x carb %) / 4
Fat grams = (TDEE x fat %) / 9

The Hand Portion Guide

No scale? No problem. Your hand is a built-in portion tool that scales to your body size.

✋

Palm = 1 Protein Serving

Flat palm, fingers excluded
~25g protein
Chicken breast, fish fillet, lean beef, tofu block. Thickness and area of your palm.
✊

Fist = 1 Carb Serving

Closed fist
~25g carbs
Cooked rice, pasta, potato, oats. One fist-sized portion of starchy carbs.
👍

Thumb = 1 Fat Serving

Entire thumb length
~10g fat
Butter, oil, nut butter, cheese. One thumb-sized portion of added fats.
🤲

Cupped Hand = 1 Fruit/Veg

Open cupped hand
~1 serving
Berries, chopped vegetables, fruit pieces. Fill your cupped hand for one serving.

Tracking Without an App

The plate method, pre-portioned prep, and visual templates your clients can use immediately.

🥬
1/2 Plate: Vegetables

Non-starchy veggies. Broccoli, spinach, peppers, salad greens. Nearly unlimited -- high volume, low calories, loaded with fiber and micronutrients.

🍗
1/4 Plate: Protein

Lean meat, fish, eggs, tofu. About 1-2 palm-sized portions. Prioritize this quarter first to hit protein targets.

🍚
1/4 Plate: Carbs

Rice, potato, pasta, bread. About 1 fist-sized portion. Increase this quarter on training days, reduce on rest days.

📦 Pre-Portioned Meal Prep
  • Cook proteins in bulk (Sunday batch cook)
  • Pre-portion into containers with a scale once
  • Pair each protein with a carb and vegetable
  • Label containers with macro totals
  • Stack 4-5 days in the fridge at once
  • Grab-and-go eliminates daily decisions
📋 The Mental Checklist Method
  • Each meal: "Do I have a protein source?"
  • Each meal: "Do I have vegetables?"
  • Each meal: "Is my carb portion fist-sized?"
  • Each meal: "Is my fat portion thumb-sized?"
  • End of day: tally palm/fist/thumb counts
  • Adjust tomorrow if today was off-target
